Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Sun Inn

Much extended old inn, now a 'Pub and Brasserie', and certainly doing a good trade in food on a Sunday lunchtime. The bar and informal dining areas are in the more interesting part of the building that still retains some well-heeled character in several seating / dining areas despite the remodelling and refurbishment. I found three real ales on the various handpumps: Doom Bar, Landlord and a decent Windsor & Eton Mandarin (£4.10), with a reversed Sharp's Atlantic clip also noted.

Graham Coombs left this review about The Sun Inn

Changed a bit since my earlier review, with the construction of a large brasserie area in former outbuildings which releases the former restaurant area back to drinking use. Still lots of wood and brickwork but I am afraid that the gastropub feel has now swept in, although the old bars round the side are still quite cosy. Small terrace area at rear. Beers unexcitingly were Doom Bar, Atlantic and Landlord; the latter was acceptable but could have been better.

moby duck left this review about The Sun Inn

As described below ,very much a traditional pub,four handpumps,the two regular beers are Courage Best and Directors with two guests, at the time of my visit these were Wells and Youngs Bombardier and Otter Bitter, a pretty underwhelming choice,however my pint of Otter was well kept,the usual Lagers were available on tap.A nice enough pub but with the ordinary choice of beer I wouldn't go out of my way to visit.
