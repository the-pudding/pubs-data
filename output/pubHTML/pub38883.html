Soup Dragon left this review about The Beacon Way

A detached 1960s estate pub, with extensions, in white painted brick and scalloping decoration, with a patio area.

The interior has a couple of rooms. The main room is an L-shaped and is both a lounge and bar area. The decor is a red, brown and white, with white ceiling and with one end being a pool and darts area, the other, a lounge where the poker tables were being played as i left. There are a few landscape pictures on the walls and a TV, which was showing the footy, so there was no music on. At the back there is a function room that was once a restaurant area. This has a stage area and is in red and white. The pub had a mixed crowd of mainly locals (apart from the poker) and the service was friendly

Usual taps really with Banks's MILD smooth and Spitfire and a fair Spekky Hen on handpull, the third tap had it's badge turned round

All in all, i will go again.... and have, always happy to pop in here for one
