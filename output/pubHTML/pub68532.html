Aqualung . left this review about The NewYearField (JD Wetherspoon)

I can confirm that this one is no longer a Lloyds bar. It is however a part of a horrible modern shopping centre and is fairly typical of that type of outlet. It's a single storey open plan room with an Island bar that is rather unhelpfully cramped in on one side. It was doing a fair trade at midday on a wet Saturday afternoon.
It was difficult to see all the beers on due to one side of the bar being obstructed. I did notice the Scottish JDW Trio Of Doom, Stewart Jack Black, Windswept Wolf, and Orkney Dark Island. I went for a bottle of the Green Devil IPA (£2.99). It's not the worst shopping centre Spoons I've been to.

David Ross left this review about The Newyearfield (Lloyds No 1)

A huge, square shaped Lloyds No. 1 bar/diner, this is just what you’d expect from one of the chain’s pubs. Incorporated into Livingston New Town’s main shopping and leisure complex, it is convenient for the shops, cinemas, night clubs and sports centres nearby, and for buses and Livingston F.C.’s football ground. There is an impressive range of beers on draught, including three real ales, and efficient service, for drinks at least, is provided from the well staffed central island bar. There is also a paved outdoor seating area with umbrellas/parasols sheltering the tables. Unlike other reviewers, I didn't find anything to complain about here, but I just found it rather ordinary and nondescript.

On 17th July 2012 - rating: 7
