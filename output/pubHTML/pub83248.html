Real Ale Ray left this review about The Two Halves

Thanet Pub of the Year 2018 and rightly so. I found this micropub to be comfortable, with a good choice of seating, especially near the front windows, which were opened on such a good day. The owner was friendly and chatty and liked a bit of a laugh and joke. Five ales, Pig and Porter All the Small Things, Humpty Dumpty Broadland Sunrise, Woodforde's Wherry, Mighty Oak Oscar Wilde Mild and Dark Star APA. I went for the Humpty Dumpty Sunrise from Norfolk, which was superb.

Aqualung . left this review about The Two Halves

The Two Halves has a much more minimalistic and brighter look to it than the two nearest micropub rivals with a laminate floor a tall table and a couple of lower tables. The walls are adorned with fairly low key pictures and it has the advantage of overlooking the seafront. It does beers and ciders from a cold room plus a few wines and soft drinks.
There were three beers Black Paw Santa Paws, Loddon Hoppit and Rooster's Three French Horns. I went for the Santa Paws which was in very good nick.
Although not as extrovert as the other two nearby micropubs the owners were friendly and I liked it. It opens at 15:00 or 12:00 Fri-Sun and on days when it is expected to be busier.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Two Halves

Another new micro-pub, with this small shop-conversion in Marine Terrace looking out directly over the harbour. Limited amount of seating inside, but a couple of school-type benches out on the pavement. Light decor, with some modern artwork on the walls. Four real ales on offer, including the unfined Andwell Mosaic (£1.50, half)
