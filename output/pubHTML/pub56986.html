Aqualung . left this review about The Assembly Rooms (JD Wetherspoon)

This is a far nicer building than the typical Spoons shop conversion as noted before. Having said that it still has the Spoons curse of the upstairs toilets accessed by a narrow staircase!

On my relatively early doors visit today it was surprisingly busy, presumably due to the market area outside.

It's a tad expensive for a Spoons (£2.49 for a "Standard" guest). Presumably this reflects the affluent profile of the area.

There was something of an Adnam's festival today which left a restricted choice but the White Horse Wayland Smithy was in excellent condition.

On 14th June 2013 - rating: 8

Bucking Fastard left this review about The Assembly Rooms (JD Wetherspoon)

A very thorough MM review requires little addition from me.I thought the 'spoons conversion has been done well and the internal space is thoughfully divided up into discrete sections thus avoiding the drinking barn atmosphere.I was also impressed that of the 10 handpumps,eight were given over to festival ales,while the remaining two offered real cider.A festival selection from Hyde's Burnt Sienna,Otter Seville Bitter,Hilden's Number4,Mauldon's Blackberry Porter,Everard's Malty Tasker,Bateman's Mocha,Rudgate Persuit of Hoppyness and Wickwar Station Porter seemed an interesting and wide selection to me.
While wandering around the town of my birth,I was aware of the loss or conversion of so many pubs in the town centre over the years,so that these days this 'spoons is probably about the best real ale option if you find yourself on a shopping expedition or visiting the nearby theatre.
