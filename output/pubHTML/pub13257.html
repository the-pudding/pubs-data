Graham Coombs left this review about The Angel

Very old pub but a bit run down and rather needing a bit of tlc. The front room is laid up for eating, with most of the action taking place in the central bar area, with a small room behind just big enough for a pool table. There is a courtyard area on the way in, and a separate patio garden at the back. Draught beers were St Austell Liquid Sunshine, Robinsons Dizzy Blonde and OSH.

E TA left this review about The Angel

Andover's oldest surviving building, dating from the 15th Century, this former inn has been a coaching inn, hotel and restaurant over the years, and it's now a town centre pub which also serves food and coffee to shoppers during the morning. A small courtyard garden is mainly used by smokers, while inside there are two bars at different levels. Stone floors, exposed structural timbers and inglenook fireplaces provide architectural interest. As a pub it feels wasted and under-exploited – it's rowdy, and the locals utterly fail to appreciate the gem they have. We found people inhere hostile and utterly uncouth, although the staff were much friendlier and maybe a bit embarrassed about the lower-order primates to which Quinno, below, refers. They run regular karaoke nights on Fridays, and roast lunches are available on Sundays. The beer was actually better than expected – GK IPA, Old Speckled Hen and Magellan were in pretty good shape and rather enjoyable. I'd be happy to come back for the architectural charm and the beer quality, I'd include in a crawl, but the unangelic morons would put me off staying here too long.
