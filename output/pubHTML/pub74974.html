Old Blue left this review about The White Horse Inn

This is a cheerful looking, square brick-built village pub with a stream bubbling past the front and a well kept garden to the side. The inside isn’t very big, with a central bar and traditional seating and tables either side. Wood panelling separates the green skirting and dado rail, above which the pale walls display lots of photos and prints. Half hidden behind the pub is a long, grey painted corrugated metal hut which houses a skittle alley.

Amongst the drinks were three hand pumps serving Palmers Best, Copper Ale and 200, and the landlady, staff and locals (and their dogs) were particularly friendly and welcoming. We also ate here one night - the food was very good and generously portioned. It was quite difficult to drag ourselves out of here: I’d say this and the New Inn in Shipton Gorge are the best Palmers houses I’ve ever visited.

On 4th June 2012 - rating: 9
