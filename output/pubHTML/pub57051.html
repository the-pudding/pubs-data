Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Tafarn Y Porth (JD Wetherspoon)

Mid-sized 'spoons housed in a former supermarket located just outside the town walls right by the impressive East Gate. Limited amount of window space on three sides (with one having a good view of the old fortifications), so light enough in places but quite gloomy in the middle, although the relatively pale furniture and decor helps a bit. However, there is also a large patio beer garden to one side. Eight real ales and a cider from the two banks of five handpumps, but disappointingly nothing from Wales let alone anything remotely local. Opted for Bass (£2.15) in the end, but the management should really show more imagination.

Aqualung . left this review about Tafarn Y Porth (JD Wetherspoon)

This is housed on the ground floor of a rather drab looking square building. Inside it's not too bad consisting of a bright but low ceilinged room with a curved bar on the left and a patio through some glass doors to the right. The furnishings are fairly typical.
The bar has two sets of five hand pumps which had 3 ciders (one available soon), the two GK beers, Big Bog Hinky Punk & Spring Bog, Moorhouse White Mist, Brain's Reverend James and Wold Top Wold Gold. I went for the local Big Bog Hinky Punk which was in very good nick.
It's not a great Spoons but I thought it was better than average especially for a relatively small place like Caernarfon.

On 24th April 2016 - rating: 7
