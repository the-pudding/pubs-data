paulof horsham left this review about The Hangar

The Hangar is definitely up the sophisticated end of the micropub scale, and a world away from the simple one room with basic furniture set-up. As well as half-a-dozen cask ales, there's a few craft keg, as well as an extensive collection of gins and a fair few wines as well. The pub was rocking early on a Saturday evening with all seats taken and a few braving the chill outside as well. It's clearly established itself in the area and the locals were a friendly bunch.

You could do as we did and add this one into a Sidcup-based micropub crawl. Well worth a visit

Aqualung . left this review about The Hangar

Yet another micropun in the Sidcup postal area, this one is only a couple of bus stops away from the Broken Drum but is in an area with no neighbouring pubs. It's a proper micropub with no electronic distractions although there is a TV displaying the beers on offer.
I'm not sure why it's called the Hangar as I could find no evidence of an aerodrome in the area.
It was once a carpet shop and is a typical rectangular room with a bar counter at the rear, benches along the walls and high tables and chairs. Their were a fair few in on a Saturday afternoon. The bar counter only has the keg options as all cask ales and ciders are served directly from the cooled cellar. There is a range of spirits that I didn't look into but suspect it's mostly speciality gins. The keg options were a lager, three Meantime brews and Wiper & True Stout.
The five cask options were ELB Foundation & Wolf, Bad Seed Session IPA, 360 degrees APA and Adnam's Broadside. These were all priced at £3.60. I went for the 360 degrees APA and had another as it was in good nick.
This is another micropub well worth visiting.
