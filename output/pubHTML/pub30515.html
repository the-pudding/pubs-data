Aqualung . left this review about Black & Grey

This is a large looking corner pub which isn't that big inside so I assume there is another room, possibly used for the live music and TV Sport advertised. The nmain bar is fairly small and quite sedate, on my afternoon visit it was food orientated but not gastro.
There are three hand pumps which had one unused, Anarchy Blonde Star and Black Sheep Best. I went for the Black Sheep (£3.55) which was in good nick but overpriced for such a mainstream pedestrian beer.
I doubt that I would return.

Real Ale Ray left this review about Black & Grey

Previously known as Bodega, which was a tapas bar. The pub reverted back to a traditional style pub earlier on in the year. The interior has an L shaped room with the bar area facing you as you enter. The bar had a traditional polished timber finish and service was from the front and side areas. The main features in here was the striking coloured wallpaper, depicting tropical birds, and an upright piano, with a notice across the front, 'play me only if you can'. The beer choice wasn't overly exciting, Hobgoblin dark, Bombardier and Black Sheep. There was also a small courtyard down the side with small tables and chairs. Staff were friendly and welcoming.

On 20th July 2017 - rating: 6
