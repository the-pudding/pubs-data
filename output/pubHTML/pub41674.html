Quinno _ left this review about The Dog & Gun

Recently re-opened. Grubby from the outside and not much better within – the inside is fairly bashed-about with horrid meringue-style artex walls, modern black ceiling beams, dirty carpet and smells of damp. Pool table. Two ales on; Moles Gold which spluttered its way through the pump and was inevitably terrible and went back (not taken off) plus an utterly indifferent Doom Bar as a replacement – why no Stonehenge given it’s only a short walk to the brewery? Should have gone with the tub of Black Rat cider perched atop the bar. Also incredibly grubby glasses. Unless some big money is spent here, I can see this place being demolished for housing before long. Dire.

E TA left this review about Dog & Gunn Inn

A pub which has gone through some troubled times recently but has now emerged as a fine village pub, the Dog and Gun is a detached Victorian house located in its own grounds on the outskirts of Netheravon adjacent to the A345. It consists of a single large bar with an open fireplace and a dining area. Outside there is a good sized garden, a smokers' patio and a car park. It does traditional pub food which is well cooked and very good value for money. The staff were very welcoming, Despite its proximity to the Stonehenge Brewery, it only stocks Wadworths beers. I had a pint of Malt and Hops, which was in excellent condition. Well worth dropping in for lunch if passing, and makes a good addition to an Avon Valley crawl.

On 20th December 2011 - rating: 7
