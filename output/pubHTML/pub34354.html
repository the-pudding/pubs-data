Soup Dragon left this review about The Trafalgar Inn

Update: Having heard the pub had been refurbished I thought I would take another look. The exterior of this Marston's double-fronted cottage with lower level extension had been repainted and their is a patio area. The two rooms are still present: an L-shaped bar with pool table, dart board and TV and a family restaurant room with TVs, grabbing machine, decent level music. The colour scheme is a bit garish, but it is so much better than was years back. The food was Ok, with a basic menu. Service was friendly and one family were in at my early visit. Three ales were on: Pedigree, Hobgoblin Gold and a decent Jennings' Snecklifter. No longer an abomination, it is a solid pub, worthy of a swifty in the bar.
*************************************
Dec 2009: This is a large detached, white rendered pub, occupying more than one building.

The interior has two rooms, one of which had its lights out and was all dark, so i didn't see it - the other room, God knows if it is a bar or lounge, is modern and all in white - so bright i nearly got a sun tan. There is a white sofa, comfy chairs, fake flowers, wood floor, pool table - it is out of place in Wimblebury!

The service was fine and there was nobody else in. This was because, not surprisingly after the last review, the place closed just a few days later, then reopened with a relief gaffer a few days after that. I can only feel sorry for the wedding party and the closure and re-opening isnt going to make them feel any better, i am sure

Beer; tap stuff, without even a MILD. No real ale. The banks's bitter on keg was ok to be fair.

The relief gaffer seemed a nice bloke and i wish him well, but the pub is an abomination to someone of my tastes.

Simon Barratt left this review about The Trafalgar Inn

The New Trafalgar is a contemporary alternative to Friday/Saturday night out in the town centre. Friday night in the bar is hilarious with Karaoke in the lounge and many a free shot from the team to keep you smiling. Its large function room is perfect for special occassions and is free to hire.

Sunny evenings and weekends are well spent in the larger than normal beer garden and excellant kids play area. Outdoor speakers bring the pub atmosphere outside, whilst inside the Nintendo Wii, stylish pool room, many large screen tv's and state of the art Duke Box ensures hours of fun.

Sunday night's quiz is guaranteed giggles and more free shots, a gallon of beer for the winners.

Your guaranteed to love this pub.
