Soup Dragon left this review about The Anchor Inn

A Victorian terraced Marston's pub and eatery with a rear patio area. The pub seems to consist of a three-storey and an ajoining two-storey element, part of which is now a chippy, with an arch for horse traffic. The pub is in white render, with red painted sills and lintles around the windows and a ground floor in red paintwork, with modern anchor etched windows.

The interior has an L-shaped room around the serving bar that faces you on entry. The decor is a mix of white, exposed brick and some wood panel, with a carpet and fake tile mixed floor and white ceiling. The bar area is at the front and has a brick fireplace with a Victorian looking grate. It has a dart board, a TV (not on at my visit) and a few Marstons pictures - though it looks a little bare on the walls. The back of the pub is a dining area, with bistro art. There was no music. The service was fine and the place had a locals feel - it had a busy mixed clientele.

Beer; usual tap stuff with a decent enough Marston's Pedigree on handpull.

A better than basic town centre pub as it does food and has a real ale. A place to pop into for one for me.
