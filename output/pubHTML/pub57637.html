Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Orbital

Brewers Fayre attached to a Premier Inn and located in a large retail park, so you know exactly how much character to expect. In that respect, it doesn't disappoint, with all the usual anonymous corporate furniture and decor in the various seating / dining areas. At least it does do a real ale, albeit just Doom Bar (£3.09) from one of the two handpumps.

Soup Dragon left this review about The Orbital

A large Brewers Fayre eatery and Premier Inn, brick build, with white painted facade and a timber frame and canopy.

The good old interior is open plan and congratulations to Brewers Fayre, you are as close to the awful Ember Inns as you could possibly be. This is a particularly bad pubco place. The dreary plain green walls, with some wood panel, are decorated with dreary pictures. The sofa and comfy chairs are matched by the arty lamps and vases. There was Sky TV. The service was fine, the clientele few and young.

Beer; tap tosh without MILD and Pedigree on handpull, though sadly this was as dead after a few sips as the atmosphere in this place. I would have took it back, but this slough of despond sapped my energy and i just managed to get out, leaving most of it, as a message to the landlord on the quality of his ale.

Never again
