Alan Winfield left this review about The Old Court Hotel

The Old Court Hotel is a smart looking white painted pub that is close to a very busy road junction.
Once inside there is a single long oblong shaped room which runs the width of the pub,the bar faces on entry.
The room is part bare boarded and carpeted,the seating normal tables and chairs and settees.
There were two unclipped pumps on the bar,i asked for a drink of real ale and a box of beer was soon brought from the back,i was expecting this to be cloudy but it turned out to be a really nice drink of Abingdon Bridge.
There was background music playing which is a good job because the pub was empty on our Saturday afternoon visit.
Not really my sort of place,but the beer was nice enough.

Pub visited 16/5/2015

Old Boots left this review about The Old Court Hotel

Formerly the Court Inn, the old justices Court House is across the road, this is a deeply modernised bar, restaurant and hotel in a decent old building. The bar is pretty much a restaurant area with table and chair seating. Two T bars of the usual stuff and a couple of handpumps but none actually in use although one had a reversed clip that I couldn't read. It is liable to close early if there's no trade about.
