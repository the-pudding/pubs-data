Alan Hurdle left this review about The Deva Tap

This pub has gradually improved over the years from its time as being called "Kash". No longer owned by Deva brewery but retains the name. Several good hand pulled beers. One long room with the bar across one end but with several separate seating areas. Near the station and worth a call on the way to the Old Cottage!

Rob Hunter left this review about The Deva Tap

Visited last Friday lunchtime. The place was dead but it is a bit out of the centre being nearer the train station. There are five hand pulls at the bar, one of which was off when I visited. In addition there are 11 keg options and three ciders listed on the board. I had the Devil's Bed Post by Blackjack at £2 for a half. It was pulled through before being served, which was good to observe.

Inside there is a stone floor, grey paintwork, nice chilled out music playing, and it felt relaxed despite me being the only customer in.

Food is served - burgers, pizza, chicken, sandwiches etc.

The chef was singing loudly in the background to different music, which I found amusing.

On 30th April 2019 - rating: 8

Jon B left this review about The Deva Tap

I didn’t notice any ‘Deva beers’ to be honest, they certainly weren’t on any of the handpumps. Anyway, as modern style beer bars go, this is a nice one. Friendly and unpretentious though the food looks expensive for ‘bar bites’. Outdoor seating very pleasant on a sunny April afternoon.
