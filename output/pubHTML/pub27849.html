moby duck left this review about The Three Kings

A chunky corner pub,the L shape main room has been modernised inside with the majority of the tables set for eating. Seven handpumps adorn the bartop but sadly only a choice of three, London Pride, Twickenham Sundance and No18 Yard 4-4-2. I seems this is a pub caught between two styles,boozer and gastro, and achieving neither,needless to say it was empty on my visit but to be fair 15 mins after opening on a Sunday lunchtime.

Rex Rattus left this review about The Three Kings

The refurbishment mentioned below has resulted in this pub moving upmarket, and that also means that there is more of a focus on dining. But the ale selection also seems to have improved somewhat, with Timothy Taylord Landlord and Le Champion (tour de France related possibly?), London Pride, and Springhead Robin Hood (£2 a half) available, with a further handpump apparently unused. I also spotted three keg offerings from Meantime - London Lager, Yakima Red, and Pale ale. I decided that it must be food led because the majority of the tables were laid for diners, but I didn't see a menu when I was in at about 2.45 PM on a Wednesday.

It's obviously bare-boarded in here now, with mainly dining room style tables with normal chairs, but with a couple of chunky tall tables, with a tall bench and tall stools. A raised area at the back on the left has a clutch of sofas in it. All of the tables had a wine glass with a red and yellow rose in them. It looked quite stylish actually. There are a couple of brick fireplaces on the right which have clearly seen considerable use in the past, and the upper parts of the walls and ceiling are painted that indigo colour so beloved of upmarket pub/restaurants. The original matchboard panelling on some of the walls has been painted white. On the plus side there are no fruit machines or other similarly unnecessary mechanical distractions.

It all looks quite elegant and classy, but it doesn't look to be a drinker's pub. My Springhead Robin Hood was in good nick though.

On 30th April 2015 - rating: 6
