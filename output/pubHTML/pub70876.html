David Ross left this review about Saints 'n' Sinners

A small, one room pub, with a half partition intended to separate a carpeted lounge section from the wooden floored bar, although effectively they are the same place. “Celtic Whiskey” on sale and a picture of the club’s 1967 European Cup winning team on the wall tell you that this is the favourite haunt of the town’s Celtic supporters. It can be extremely busy during big Celtic matches. There are occasional musical performances by a singer calling himself “The Dubliner” and regular karaoke sessions on Sunday nights. Saints ’n’ Sinners has a friendly bunch of regulars and the homely atmosphere of a real Irish pub, as opposed to the manufactured chain variety.

On 20th January 2011 - rating: 8
