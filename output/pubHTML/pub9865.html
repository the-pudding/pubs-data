Steve C left this review about Mackland Arms

The Mackland Arms is a u shaped Shepherd Neame pub with their usual draught and four hand pumps that were drawing Master Brew (x2), Spitfire and Late Red during my recent early Thursday evening visit. There were six or seven locals sitting around the bar and I was quickly involved in friendly conversation with them and the governor. Sky Sports is advertised, but the television was being used to play Magic FM making the jukebox redundant. I managed to step over the massive, but friendly, pub dog to note the dartboard around to the rear of the bar. I didn’t visit the advertised beer garden.

This is a friendly local pub that I would gladly visit again.

On 24th February 2013 - rating: 8

E TA left this review about Mackland Arms

One of the few proper pubs left in Rainham, the Mackland is a lively town local in the middle of a terrace near the station and just off the High Street. It's a Shepherd and Neame house with plenty of trappings - dart board, children's room, pub dog and garden. It's also the only pub in Rainham with a thriving euchre team. The single L shaped bar had two ales on - I had the Late Red which was just past its best but still very drinkable. The staff are welcoming and the atmosphere convivial. In my opinion the best pub in Rainham which deserves its GBG entry and is an essential part of a town crawl.

On 3rd February 2011 - rating: 8
