Soup Dragon left this review about The Mount Pleasant

An impressive detached Georgian looking pub and eatery, with a large beer garden. It is a square building in undressed brick that has been extended at the back, with a touch of green paintwork.

The interior has a central serving bar with the pub open plan all around it, however the more bar area is to the left when entering via the main front door and the food area is to the right. The decor is a mix of green, red and white walls, with some exposed brickwork and with a white ceiling. There is a mix of pictures on the walls, from bistro art in the dining bit to old photographs and old adverts in the bar area. There was also a TV here, which had the Villa on at my visit. The lounge has a nice brick fireplace with a wood burning stove. The is a small secluded area at the back of the lounge. Tere was no music. The service was fine and the clientele was mixed and it was quite busy. I didn't eat here so i can't comment on the food.

Beer; usual tap stuff with Banks's MILD and Banks's Bitter, St Austell Tribute, a decent Hobsons's Town Crier and Enville Ale on handpull.

A really nice pub, especially on a hot day with the grounds around it. It has a good selection of beer, which is well kept. Definitely worth a visit if around Wombourne.
