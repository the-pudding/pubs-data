Soup Dragon left this review about The Weatherbury

A large detached corner pub in undressed brick, with red paint work, which also does accommodation and has a patio area. In the GBG 2010.

The interior is open plan in an L-shape, where the entrance is at the join and the serving bar is to the right. It is in white and red, with some wood panel and fakey looking ceiling beams. There are mirrors, brewery ephemera, hanging jugs, a marble and tile fireplace, decorative ironwork all giving a traditional feel to the place - as does the stools and transport cafe style seating - no sofas here. The tradition games are evident, so darts and a pool table. The TV wasn't on and the music was general and at a decent level. Service was fine and the clientele consisted of a few locals.

Beer; tap stuff with London Pride (i left untouched as ever) but decent Piddle Silent Slasher, Cottage Pionair Leopard and a very good Hook Norton Copper Ale.

A great all round proper pub. Decent surroundings, class beer and pub games. So please i managed to find this place, the last of my stay in Weymouth.
