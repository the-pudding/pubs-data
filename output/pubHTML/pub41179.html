ROB Camra left this review about The Rosse

A good looking stone built fairly traditional pub on two levels. There's a pool table and a quiz machine to while away the hours. Four handpumps with three beers available, tended by a friendly barman. I'm not really a fan of Wishbone brewery, but the Wishbone Volk I had in here was the best beer I've ever had from this brewery. I liked it in here and will definitely call again.

Real Ale Ray left this review about The Rosse

A decent pub this one and well worth a visit. The barman was friendly and chatty and fellow drinkers in here were all respectable. They had four ales on, so went for the Bridestones Sandstone, which was in good shape and in spite of its low ABV, the beer still had good flavour.

Alan Winfield left this review about The Rosse

The Rosse is a large stone built pub that is situated on the corner of a busy road junction.
Once inside there is a decent sized oblong shaped room with the bar facing,there is comfy bench seating facing the bar and tall tables and chairs near the bar,there is a raised area to the left which has a pool table and a few tall tables and chairs,for entertainment there was a large screen to the right showing rugby and a TV to the rear left showing football score updates.
There were four real ales on the bar,i had a drink of Wharfdale Tether Blonde which was a very nice drink,the other beers were Wharfdale Ro Sham Bo,Taylor Boltmaker and Saltaire Rasberry Blonde.
This is a decent enough stop off for a couple of drinks.

Pub visited 28/11/2015
