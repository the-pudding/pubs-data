Al Bundy left this review about Stanley Arms

A good little corner local. Multi-roomed with the bar in the small room to your right as you enter. An old replica c18th century rifle/firearm is above the bar. 3 handpumps with just Holts Bitter and Mild on. Had a mulleted man with a mallet in which was a bit worrying.

ROB Camra left this review about Stanley Arms

Small, but multi roomed pub with the usual Holts's smart green tiling on the inside walls. There's a tap room to the right as you enter complete with a dartboard and a small lounge to the left. Straight ahead is a tiled drinking corridor leading to a further small room with a pool table. The tap room was full with the post work crowd on my Friday early evening visit, so lots of banter going on. I had a half of Holts Bitter at £1.10 served by the friendly barmaid and listened in for a while. This is a proper traditional workingmans local and long may it continue to be so.

Alan Winfield left this review about Stanley Arms

The Stanley Arms is a small corner pub situated on the busy liverpool road.
Once inside we went into a small basic bar,there may have been a lounge to the rear but we did'nt look,the bar had normal tables and chairs with a lino floor.
The pub is a Joseph Holt tied house,there were two real ales on the bar,we had a drink of the excellent Holts Bitter which went down a treat,the other beer was Holts Mild.
I really like these sorts of pubs,this was the reason me and the wife made quite a few visits to Eccles.
