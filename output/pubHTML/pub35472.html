Gill Smith left this review about The Boat House

Definately had a feel of an upmarket hotel as we entered, but we passed the reception area to the bar. Most of the room taken up with tables set up for restaurant use, but they do have an area for those wanting a drink only. One real ale handpull serving Fullers London Pride which was obscured by their freestanding food adverts but they also had a Rebellion IPA in key keg at the back of the bar which we chose. Not bad actually. Better than most key keg products as it was not chilled to death.

On 15th November 2012 - rating: 7

Chris Porter left this review about The Boat House

Best place for food & drink in Chertsey by a mile. Views of the river Thames and a hotel as well, we were really impressed. Good 'Pub Grub', shepherds pie, cumberland sausage, all tastes homemade. The rest of the 'town' is a write off, typical boozers; pissed regulars, smokey - you get the picture. No wonder this place is always busy.

On 21st December 2006 - rating: 8
