Tris C left this review about White Horse

A haven't been here since around 1983 but the reviews below indicate that there's been little if any change. Indeed you shoot at 60 mph past the pole sign with the empty frame (hence the Pub With No Name) and then there's no turning back for miles, but it would seem from Google that there's now a road sign which defeats the purpose somewhat - this is a local pub for local people no more, it would seem. Then it's up a country road and turn into a gravel drive and car park. I don't know if the lavatories are still outside in a hut with a slatted door and lift-up latch, but I hope so. The interior was very traditional with the likes of hop bines, and the website paints a picture which I recall.
Back then, the pub sold its (in)famous No Name Bitter which I seem to recall was brewed on-site. It was very moreish but with a hefty ABV which ensured that you had a bastard behind the eyes for most of the next day!
This place is well worth a trip and I'd love to return some day.

Quinno _ left this review about White Horse

Known as the Pub With No Name due to the sign holder at the base of the hill, which has long been empty. The pub boasts a nice array of ales and ciders - beers sampled included Oakhams JHB, with No Name Strong and Best also. The cider was a particularly dry one (Heart of Hampshire I think) which certainly stripped the plaque off the teeth, should keep my dentist happy. The owners were happy to chat in between tasks and we were made to feel welcome. A couple of small downsides - I found was that the interior was a bit dark for a sunny day, and I wanted to get outside. I dare say it's a better bet in the winter months as the real fires are lit. Also the beer, whilst fair, wasn't of the quite of the top quality I was hoping for. I am being picky though – it's worth a return visit.
