Quinno _ left this review about The Plough Inn

Looks like it has recently been done-up. L-shaped bar with white and duck egg shades inside plus booth seating and log wall. Perspex slab in bar floor affords a direct view of the cellar. Leaded windows at the front with stained glass picking out the Arkells name are nice. Games room to rear with table football and darts and an old arcade game. Narrow rear garden. Unexpectedly impressive looking bar - 12 pumps with 5x Arkells and 2x Donnington. Five ciders from Thatchers and Westons. Sadly all the ales we tried were below-par. I’d give it another go as it’s decent enough as a pub but they need to sort the beer out.

On 10th July 2015 - rating: 6

Alan Winfield left this review about The Plough Inn

The Plough is a small proper pub on the edge of the old town and it is next to a deep railway cutting that is now unused.
The pub was quite cosy and comfortable inside and it is an Arkells tied house there were two real ales on the bar i had a drink of Arkells 3B and this was a very nice drink the other beer was Arkells 2B.
I thought this was a very nice unaltered pub and i would be happy to come here again.
