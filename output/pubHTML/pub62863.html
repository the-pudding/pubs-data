Rex Rattus left this review about The Fig Tree

This is a Greene King pub judging by the exterior signage – and confirmed by the ales available. There was no GKIPA for some reason, but they did have Abbot as well as something called Cotswold Old Sodbury Mild (£1.60 a half), which the barmaid informed me was brewed by Greene King. Now there’s a surprise. But for a GK beer it wasn’t bad at all. Food seems to be available most times, with a standard pub grub menu available – e.g. British Beef & Ale Pie at £6.99.

The pub looks to be housed in a conversion from an old civic building, and which a previous reviewer says was a police station. The main drinking area is broken up and separated by ironwork balustrades, pillars, slightly raised area, and at the back a conservatory has been added to provide additional seating. There are tall tables/tall stools all over the place; some normal tables and chairs and a few plush bucket armchairs. There’s loads of outside seating, which seemed to be very popular when I was in there on a very nice Wednesday afternoon.

I can’t get very excited about this place – I rarely do about conversions – but it is comfortable, clean, and bright and apparently well looked after. The outside seating is a big plus, but I’m not a great fan of Greene King ales so probably won’t be making a return visit.

On 17th May 2014 - rating: 6
