Gill Smith left this review about Ye Olde Vic

Well all I can say is I know exactly what the last reviewer means as we were exactly the same. This was the last pub for us on a coach trip of pubs in the area ending with 8 bars in Stockport, so my memory was hazy, but it was a great place that welcomed us with a smashing community spirit even to appreciating the singing from a few of our crowd. The last 3 pubs on our trip, Hope Magnet and here have made us want to book accomodation up here to have a weekend and be able to spend more time in these fantastic bars. We enjoyed our Abbeydale Reveller but wanted to try so many more. Will be back.

Malden man left this review about Ye Olde Vic

All I can say is that this is a magnificent pub, I have a very hazy memory about it after a day around Stockport pubs and the football but I recall all sorts of stuff on the walls and ceilings covered in pump clips.I appear to have acquired one. I somehow ended up behind the bar at one point and actually pulled someone a pint. It all started due to a discussion with the landlord about sparklers and great fellow he is he invited me behind the bar to witness for myself. I cannot remember the beers as by now I couldn't note stuff but there were plenty on and in good shape. They were listed on a screen. I may have had a pickled egg.
Loads of fellow Altrincham fans in, apparently last time in town our supporters drank the place dry. There's a garden out the back, I remember that much.There were some stairs, I think that's why my arm hurts.

Rob Hunter left this review about Ye Olde Vic

My last stop last Saturday before getting the train. Had seen something about Ye Olde Vic recently so figured it was worth trying to squeeze a visit in. Glad I did, it is an interesting place, like stepping back in time to another era. There was a bank of hand pumps at the bar and I think most of them were on. I think the landlord must be particularly proud of his "beer board" TV screen as he directed us to it while we were choosing our beers, despite having a clear view of the pumps at the bar right in front of us. I can't help thinking the TV is a bit overkill for a place of this size and as others have said does stand out as the one modern thing in what is otherwise a bit of a time warp.

The real fire is a great feature and we were lucky enough to get a seat next to it. There were a few people in at the time of our visit but it was not what you would call busy. From the cask ales on offer I had the Bramble Stout by Burton Bridge Brewery, which was rather enjoyable! I was a bit pressed for time due to the train (which I ended up missing by about 10 seconds) so only stayed for one, but could easily have had another if time had permitted. Nice friendly pleasant atmosphere, in my opinion, with character.

On 16th March 2018 - rating: 7
