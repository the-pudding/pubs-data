Aqualung . left this review about Yr Ieuan Ap Iago (JD Wetherspoon)

The name of this pub is the Welsh name for John James who penned the Welsh national anthem. It's an old stone building for which I can't pin down the original use, but it doesn't look like a shop. The entrance is up some steps leading to a room set out for eating with the bar area on the right at the rear. The bar is in a high ceilinged room with exposed aircon ducts. There is also a patio. On my Thursday evening visit there was a good mix of people.
There are two sets of five hand pumps which arguably had the best choice of S Wales valley Spoons. There were two ciders, the JDW Trio Of Doom, Acorn Barnsley Bitter, Borough Iron Runner, Glamorgan Thunerbird & Old Rig and Boss Blonde. I went for the Old Rig (£2.15) which was in excellent condition.
I thought this was a good Spoons.
