Komakino . left this review about The Phoenix Bar

Formerly The Roundabout, this is unashamedly a music venue, with Open Mic nights, Karaoke and bands on Friday and Saturday nights, including the odd D&B night. The internal layout is roughly lower-case H-shaped with an entrance-facing bar, a game room, with dartboard, pool table and jukebox via an open doorway to the right, table and chair seating in front and to the right, with some tables covered with a slew of album covers 'covering', then a performance area to the rear left. Plenty of adverts for upcoming music-related nights with the odd musical instrument dotted around on the walls (guitars, violins, drumsticks and cymbals). Two Rebellion ales on - Roasted Nuts and Zebedee - the latter £3.80 and borderline watery. Not sure whether someone had block-booked the jukebox or not, but the background muzak remained ABBA-based throughout. As mentioned below, it's near the bus station, but in terms of choice of ambience, The Chiltern Taps or Rose & Crown are other, better, alternatives.

On 21st October 2018 - rating: 5
