Aqualung . left this review about Tullie Inn

This place is situated next door to the end of the line from Queen Street Low Level. It's a large tourist orientated Inn with accommodation, food (including breakfast) and a separate restaurant that I didn't check out. There are outdoor areas for when it's not raining. The bar area is a large modern open plan room that is divided into areas. Most of the seating is tall but there are some low tables and chairs.
The bar has three hand pumps which on my visit just had Loch Lomond Southern Summit (£4.30) which seemed expensive to a Sassenach but after coming up lively was in good nick. Given the lack of quality cask ale in these parts this one is worth a try.

Graham Coombs left this review about The Tullie Inn

Very large and very modern, with lots of glass and light wood. Emphasis on food but keeps interest in beer with several good ales (including from Loch Fyne at time of visit) and things like 'Meet the Brewer' events. Only shadow is that the owning company went bust in early 2015 so remains to be seen what happens in the future.
