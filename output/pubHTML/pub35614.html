Quinno _ left this review about The Spotted Dog

A fairly nondescript L shaped pub that nonetheless boasts a large and impressive grassed beer garden with nice views of the Surrey Hills. The left area is the nicest, with some cosy touches and a dart board whilst the right is more of a public bar style area with TV sports. External pool room to rear. Selection of newspapers available. Three ales, Wychwood Piledriver (spluttered and died), Pride and a lacklustre Doom Bar. Everything seemed a bit haphazard (exemplified by the tables not being cleared of what looked like Ollie Reed’s last drinking session). There’s enough here to make this a good pub but my visit didn’t overly inspire.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Spotted Dog

This pub is a bit of an odd mixture of traditional and modern, both inside and out. For example, contrast the old-fashioned bay windows with the cantilever pub sign design which would look more at home above the door of a gastro pub. Inside, the formerly separate bars have been opened out to form a 'L'-shaped layout, with a single step dividing the two parts. The interior has been heaviliy refurbished at some stage, with the furniture and decor being quite light in colour. It also has a modern decking terrace at the back, with a nice view of the Surrey hills above the town roofs and some distinctly old-fashioned gents toilets. Darts board. Three real ales on handpump - Pride, Bombardier and a decent pint of Courage Best (£3.20). Overall, I thought that the atmosphere had significantly improved since my previous visit in 2008 when I found it slightly characterless.
