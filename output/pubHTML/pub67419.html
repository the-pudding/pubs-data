Alan Winfield left this review about Bassa Villa

Bassa Villa is an old looking pub that is on the nice looking Cartway.
Once inside there is an oblong shaped room which is divided into two areas by a wall,the room runs to the rear,the bar is on the left side and the room is carpeted,there are pew seats and normal tables and chairs for seating,the TV was showing sky football.
There were two real ales on the bar,i had a drink of St Austell Proper Job,which was a nice drink,the other real ale was St Austell Tribute.
There is a nice outside area which overlooks the River Severn.
I thought this was a pleasant pub to have a drink in.
Sadly i forgot to take a photo of it.

Pub visited 29/4/2017

Old Boots left this review about Bassa Villa

An old interior in new clothes in a building at the bottom of the historic Cartway. Standard café bar fittings rub shoulders with the ancient beams of stripped out walls. Its main attraction is the river terrace overlooking wild fowl and the bridge, a civilised way to drink a pint on a warm afternoon. There’s only one pump on the counter so as expected it was on great form, on this occasion it was Kinver Edge.

Called in ajust after 11am on the first Sunday of the Bridgnorth 10 day Music and Arts Festival,and although only early the outdoor seating area was almost full.There are excellent views from the riverside terrace of the Severn and the Lowtown district,and as Soup Dragon points out, the bar area is very small and the only handpulled beer is the Kinver Edge.Worth a visit on a nice day.
