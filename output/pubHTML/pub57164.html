E TA left this review about The Sun Inn

A large roadside pub which has managed to retain a pleasant local feel despite being on a major ringroad junction. There is ample car parking and a garden area, though the traffic makes it noisy. Inside, as described below, there is a single square bar whose massive bay window provides a panoramic view over the nearby roundabout. There are two dartboards and gaming machines, while something called ‘Rock & Roll Bingo’ appears to be practised on selected evenings once a month. There was a tv screen, but it was switched off. The food is the standard pub fare, good value for money given its location, and there are ‘express’ deals available at lunchtime. Dogs are welcomed as are children. The décor is reasonably modern but in keeping with the building, enhanced by some new stained glass windows, including on the door to the disabled lavatory, while the sun motif on the carpet probably goes unnoticed much of the time. Two pumps dispensing Arkells ales, but the Gold was off so I plumped for the 3B, which was in very good condition. Worth stopping for lunch or a pint when passing.

On 9th March 2016 - rating: 6
