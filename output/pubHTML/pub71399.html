Aqualung . left this review about The Benjamin Fawcett (JD Wetherspoon)

This is a red brick building that is said to be an amalgam of two former pubs but doesn't really look like it. Inside it's a fairly typical large rectangular room with a low ceiling, a patio at the rear and the usual fixtures and fittings.
The bar is on the left and has ten hand pumps which had the JDW Trio Of Doom, RCH East Street, Caledonian Seasoned Traveller IPA, Hop Studio Dubbel, Rudgate Valkyrie APA & Lollipop, North Somerset OSM and White Horse Blowing Stone. I went for the RCH one which was in good nick. I thought this was a decent Spoons.

Real Ale Ray left this review about The Benjamin Fawcett (JD Wetherspoon)

The interior of this Spoons was very poorly illuminated and what with the dark stained woodwork, made it even darker. In spite of this pub being located at the far end of town, it was still full on our Saturday lunchtime visit. The pub consisted of six sections, the front porch area, then a section with the staircase up to the loos, then four seat oblong seating areas on the right, finishing at the rear patio doors. The bar area ran along the left hand wall. There were ten handpumps in use on this visit, so we went for the Vale Brewery Punk is Dead.

On 3rd April 2016 - rating: 6
