Bucking Fastard left this review about Brewhouse & Kitchen

Smaller than a lot in this chain,it's situated on the ground floor of a modern building.The interior decor is interesting with a low ceiling and lots of industrial ducting ,a U shaped bar and a mix of furniture with a long high bench running down the right wall,divided from the bar by a half height wooden partition,while there is seating with booths to the front and left.6 gleaming copper vessels to the rear right,again not the biggest plant but brewing takes place regularly.There is a hipsterish menu at hipsterish prices,but also a beer guide listing a lot of keg options and some bottles,while there are 8 handpumps offering on my trip brewed on site Arcangel,Spandau B,Myddleton,Britton,Chaplin (a decent APA) and Badas (very good collaberation with Innis & Gunn,oak aged) with one pump for Orchard Pig cider and another unused.
There are flatscreens but they are not distracting,while the muzak was well chosen and played at a sensible volume.The clientele was mainly young professionals on my Friday afterwork visit,but overall it was a lot better than expected,quite a cosy place and worth another visit.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Brewhouse & Kitchen

The former BRB at the Arc has been transformed by this brewpub chain into something much more interesting. Features an elongated 'U'-shaped bar, with the brewing vessels in the far inside corner. Variety of semi-modern furniture and a mix of decor that seems to work perhaps a bit better than it should. Also has some outdoor furniture along the side-street façade. Five of the own cask beers available from the eight handpumps, including Man's Best Friend harvest ale (£3.95). At least 16 craft keg taps divided between the counter and bar-back.
