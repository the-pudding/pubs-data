. Wittenden left this review about Spread Eagle Inn

An attractive stone and brick built pub close to the National Trust's Stourhead Gardens. Despite the heavy National Trust presence here, the pub is on a public road, next to the village church. We visited on a sunny October morning, on the day the clocks went back for the winter. We were fairly early, but half of Wiltshire and adjacent counties were there to worship the trees in the Gardens, and to have lunch at the Spread Eagle. Even just after noon, the pub was packed with mainly diners, but we were able to find a table outside in the courtyard.
Inside, there are three rooms, all laid up for dining,but with some space for drinkers in the front bar. Orders for both food and drink were processed at the bar, which made for a rather hectic experience, but the staff were friendly and seemingly unflustered. Calmer moments prevailed in the shrines to Farrow and Ball further into the pub.
On the bar, in addition to the standard fizz fonts, were three hand pumps offering Stourton Pale and Kilmington Best from the fairly local Wessex micro brewery, together with Butcome Bitter. The first two beers, at any rate, were well presented . I seem to think that there were a couple of craft keg beers available, but don't quote me on this.
Our meals -ham 'n'eggs for me- were reasonable if a bit on the pricey side. However, if given the choice between a real pub and a National Trust cafe,I which one I'd choose.

Michael Wright left this review about Spread Eagle Inn

Located next to National Trust property of Stourhead, thus obviously caters for grockles as there are no locals to speak of
More of a restaurant than pub for this reason, food served all day, 3 bitters I noted

Finding a seat could be hard at times as they are very busy on sunny days

Food was standard pub grub at more than standard prices, expect around £10 meal, £3 pint, luckily they take credit cards, you could need one

The staff are busy , and the woman taking the payments was curt verging on rude at times, I never heard a please or thank you all the time I, and others, were waiting to pay at the bar

Not 4 stars, but churlish to give it 2 based on one staff member, so 3 it is

Not one to drive to visit, but OK if there
