Graham Coombs left this review about The Waterloo Inn

A little two room local just off the seafront, unspoilt to the extent that it is straight out of the 1970s. Sadly the (keg) beer range is too, although the regulars didn't seem to notice. Could be a cracking little pub with minimal effort.

Steve C left this review about Waterloo Inn

There were ten or so locals in the right hand bar of the Waterloo Inn when I visit just before 11am on Saturday morning. Horse racing was airing on the plasma screen as the jukebox competed for sound. The left hand bar that houses a second plasma screen (off) and the jukebox was devoid of customers. At the rear of this bar is an area with two dartboards. A standard keg range was available and the four hand pumps (2 in each bar) were unused. To be honest I’m not sure that real ale is going to be popular in a pub where the locals are buying Sambuca chasers before most pubs have opened for the day. For those that need a break from the smell of arse there are three picnic tables out the front.
I found the service to be friendly, but this is a pub for locals.

On 23rd August 2016 - rating: 4
