Soup Dragon left this review about Market Hall Tavern

Another Banks's cottage style pub, with patio area. It takes its name from the market hall that once stood opposite (later a cinema, now demolished - a Boots is on the site). This Victorian/Edwardian pub has three upper sash style windows and two lower bays, one either side the central entrance. It is a mix of plain and cream painted brick, with some black and white paintwork and a canopy over the entrance. The interior is now one L-shaped room, in red and grey at the front and cream and brown at the back, where there is a pool table and dart board. There is a brick fireplace. Several TVs are dotted about with Sky Sports and the horse racing on (indeed, you can write your bet out in the pub and pop to the bookies after). There is a pull down screen (not in use) and there was no music. There are a few mirrors, birsto style pictures on the walls and there is a mix of red bench and comfy seating. The service was fine and the place busy enough with locals. I think they have live bands on as well. Beer; usual tap stuff with Peroni, with Sunbeam (just went), Banks's Bitter and MILD (ok) on handpull. A run-of-the-mill locals pub that has the games, sport and real ale. Could do far worse!
