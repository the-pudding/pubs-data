A very good brewery tap belonging to Ludlow Brewery. Two levels with a small mezzanine level above the entrance. 7 handpulls all serving Ludlow beers plus 4 fizzpumps for dullness. Plenty of outside seating at the front. Very good.

Aqualung . left this review about Brewery Tap

The old goods shed is all that remains of the Ludlow goods sidings and it now houses the Ludlow Brewery and Tap Room. As you approach from the station the entrance and front patio appears to be the old parcels loading bay but it may be fake as the bay would have been above the level of the rails. Of course, they could have levelled it all out after removing the rails.
Inside it's been converted and modernised to a very high standard with an upstairs seating area to your left as you enter, the bar at the rear right with part of the brewery above and as noted below, a couple of serious looking brewing vessels to the left. There is more of the brewery further back and presumably the pub cellar. The floor is slate tiled and furnishings are a mix of sofas and chunky wooden benches and tables. A really big plus here is that there is no TV Sport. I was surprised how many were in on my late Thursday morning visit with a mix of shoppers, some who looked like regulars and the odd brewery worker. They weren't all here for thee beer as some were on coffee or wine.
There are still six Ludlow beers and a cider and on this occasion a strong Ludlow Whiskey Well served from a cask in the cellar. I tried the Black Knight, Stairway, Boiling Well, Whiskey Well and Best which were excellent. All were £3.00 a pint apart from the Whiskey Well which was £3.30. I've only tried a few Ludlow beers in the past but they tasted great in here, probably enhanced by the hop aroma from the brewing. My Ludlow visit was restricted to this place and I would gladly do it again.

Old Boots left this review about Brewery Tap

A very slick and professionally run brewery tap situated at one end of the former railway goods shed that houses the brewery. The counter with seven pulls is located under the malt store and mill. Next it are the mash tun and copper and a small retail area. The rest of the space is given over to seating at leather sofas, armchairs and chunky but elegant wooden benches and tables, there is also an upper seating level in one corner. Six Ludlow beers on sale plus a cider and a t-bar of keg ciders etc. A collection of albums hold press cuttings, information on the brewery and brewing, and as it's an old railway building a bit of railway porn. Well worth a visit if in Ludlow but check the opening hours.
