Bucking Fastard left this review about The Samuel Pepys Diary Rooms

A smart internal refurb has produced a comfortable feel,with lots of pastel colours,several different section in an open plan setup.Rear doors lead to a beer garden,while a raised section feels mainly for diners.Food served until 2.30pm,then again after 5.30pm although the pub stays open all day.
I was surprised to see 7 handpumps,with only a Doom Bar tap reversed,no bad thing.That left me a choice of Holts Two Hoots,Woodforde Reedlighter,Timothy Taylor Golden Best,Castle Rock Harvest Pale,Marston's 61 Deep and Adnams Ghost Ship (a little warm,out of condition ,£1.85 half)
The food offering was a lot cheaper than the surroundings would suggest ,most mains under £10 apart from steaks and some daily specials.Not a bad place but maybe throughput cannot justify 7 real ales .At least an effort has been made to attract real ale drinkers.
