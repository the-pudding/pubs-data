Real Ale Ray left this review about Friends of Ham - Ilkley

Another Friends of Spam outlet. This one would you believe, was even more pretentious and irritating than the one in Leeds. The barman, when serving me, addressed me as Buddy and the usual guys thing still prevails. We got here mid Saturday afternoon and the place had quietened down slightly. The interior is small and we found it difficult to navigate, trying to get past the elitist mummies and daddies with push chairs and children in tow, all out for an afternoons slice of spam and olive.
Some bright spark had also decided to place the cashiers till at the top of the stairs, which created a decent bottle neck.
The place stills attracts a lot of luvvies and the guy kissing his lady friend's forehead and holding hands near the window, under a dried up pig's leg, was enough for me to drink up hastily and go to a proper pub. Beer wise there was three handpumps, so I went for the Wild Child Brewery, Expatriate Session IPA.

Alan Winfield left this review about Friends of Ham - Ilkley

The Friends of Ham is situated just up wells road from the train station.
Once inside the bar faces,the seating is normal tables and chairs,there is also a separate room to the rear which is down one step,this room is square shaped and has a stone floor,the seating here is comfy bench type and normal tables and chairs,there are candles on the tables,this room seemed quite intimate,the sort of room to have a drink with the wife or girlfriend.
I had a drink of Ilkley Tribus which went down well,the other beer noted was Ghost Zenith.
There were more real ales on the bar,but it was too busy to note all.
I prefer this one to the Leeds pub.
