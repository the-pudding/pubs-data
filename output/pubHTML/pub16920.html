E TA left this review about Freemasons Arms

A working village pub on a busy main road. There is plenty of parking at the back. The pub consists of two good size bars, carpeted, with a mix of furniture. It has a good, homely feel and the welcome from staff and locals was genuine. There are the usual gaming machines, tv screens and basic food menu which one might expect in a village local. Two ales on – I had Masterbrew, which was in pretty good condition and quite refreshing. Worth dropping in when passing.

On 7th February 2014 - rating: 6
