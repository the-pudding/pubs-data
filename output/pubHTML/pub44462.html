Oggwyn Great left this review about The Aston

Previously known as the Last Inn , the Aston calls itself a Country Dining Pub , there are two entrances both to the side of the pub , one marked Bar the other Restuarant , they both lead to the same room , the bar door takes you into the bottom half of the pub with low beams and an open fire , steps lead up to the restuarant area in what is basically one L shaped room , for a dining pub it came as a suprise that the kitchen is closed in the afternoons (open again at 5.30).
There is a decent selection on beer on with Hoegarden and Peroni on draught and a good selection of cask ale , Pedigree , Wye Valley Golden Ale , Wood Shropshire Lad and Three Tun XXX , The Golden Ale was very good .
Service was pretty good .
Its not a bad pub this

anne marie golder left this review about The Last Inn

BE WARNED. This pub does not serve food on a Monday. We wasted money on 2 taxis to have a belated Valentine's celebration there only to find out after being there an hour, and ONLY because the landlord told 3 other ladies who had come in to eat. Also, take some earplugs as if it is the landlords dinner time the TV will be turned up loud so he can watch the news. I am genuinly upset as we were really looking forward to the night out as The Last Inn has been our "special occasion" pub for years. So when I read the other reviews we decided to return and were left bitterly disappointed. We'll try again but will make sure we drive and make sure we're not celebrating anything.
