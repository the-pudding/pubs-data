Komakino . left this review about The Bell

A Fuller's pub which offers Thai and English cuisines and located on the Aylesbury Road as it leads out of the village. This has a bar located bar left (if you enter from the front) of a rectangular room dissected lengthwise by a wall covered in bookcase wallpaper and opened at either end along with two central openings housing shelves. The room entered into acts as the bar area and is the narrower of the two with a tiled floor, standard tables and chairs (with a couple of their taller cousins next to the bar) and bar stools against the bar itself, which is a squat L-shape, meaning two to three locals huddling around it make it seem busy. Walking through the opening to the bar's right takes you to the second room, which is in essence the restaurant area, all bare-boarded with all tables set for dining (and only two of those occupied) and with a longer extension of the bar itself. London Pride was the sole option over seven handpumps (4 pumps were badged with Pride, one was unclipped and ESB and HSB were reversed), so low points for a lack of originality.

On 15th May 2017 - rating: 4
