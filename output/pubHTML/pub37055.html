Nick Davies left this review about White Horse

A few years ago the White Horse was a time warp Ansell's classic, just the bar and snug at the front and a must do for anyone planning a Leamington crawl. Naturally none of that was good enough for M&B's head office bean counters and around 2006 the premises to the rear were converted into the huge space we have now, with the front rooms lying unused and unloved for most of the time.

It is one of M&B's Castle outlets, so the format is much the same as their Ember/Vintage Inns, with a better beer range but mediocre catering offer: the Brake Bros stuff you get doesn't live up to the fancy descriptions and ambitious pricing. You'd probably tolerate the same fare in a Harvester.

Purity UBU was very nice indeed, Doom Bar not so good.

If the front rooms are in use it's well worth a visit. If not there's little to detain you.

john mcgraw left this review about White Horse

A multi roomed pub with a large side bar and a smaller bar which was closed at the time but I assume it is open later in the day. A good range of well kept real ales on offer. The only thing that spoils this pub is the loud music that was blaring out which really spoilt any conversation and was far too loud.

Thuck Phat left this review about White Horse

There are some comprehensive reviews below so this one will be brief.
It's a large, popular and trendy pub serving food to a mixed but mainly younger clientele. Don't let that put you off as there's a good atmosphere and isn't uncomfortable.
Beer on was: Oakham Citra, Purity UBU, Andwell Red IPA and Liberation Ale. A good selection for a trendy food focused boozer and the Citra was excellent.
I'd be happy to return for the Citra alone.
