Steve C left this review about Archies

Archies is a modernised bar on the High Street with a frontage that opens up onto a small decked area that holds some seating. The interior consists of a bar on the left hand wall that faces ten or so tables that are located throughout the small inside. I counted four plasma screens, but only one was on which was showing a muted music channel for some reason. In the toilet is a sign saying that BT Sport will be shown from August, but there were no signs of it during my visit a couple of weeks ago.
Standard draught is available from the bar and the five hand pumps were drawing Gower Brewery’s Gold and Deuchars alongside Facer’s North Star Porter and Flintshire Bitter. The fifth hand pump was drawing Country Perry. Pub food is available and I thought that the burger and fish cake dishes that we ordered were good value for money, but the fifty minute wait was a bit excessive.

I found the service here to be extremely friendly and I would return if passing, but I will make sure that I’m in no hurry if I plan to order food.

On 19th August 2013 - rating: 7

Richard Hartley-Williams left this review about Archies

Archies is a one roomed bar and has recently undergone a very tasteful refurbishment. The house beer is Flintshire Bitter from Facer's Brewery. Two other real ales are served, sourced mainly from local breweries, although beers from further afield frequently make an appearance.
