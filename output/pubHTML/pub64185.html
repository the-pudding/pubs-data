Soup Dragon left this review about The Swan Inn

A large Georgian styled terraced pub and eatery in a cream and grey render with white sash windows and string-courses along with dormer windows

The interior has a café upstairs, with the ground floor given over to a mix of dining tables and blue patterned perimeter seats. The small serving bar faces you on entry. The décor sees mainly cream walls, with grey and a wood dado rail, with a white and wood ceiling and a blue patterned carpet. There is a stone fireplace feature and a pool table. A few mirrors (a nice Courage Best one) and some old local photographs adorn the walls. There was a TV, but it wasn’t on at my visit and the music was mixed and at a decent level. The service was fine and the clientele consisted of a few locals along with some holiday makers.

Beer; the usual tap stuff with Sharpe’s Doombar and a very good St Austell Tribute on handpull

A rather straight-forward place, in that the two real ales on are ubiquitous in the area, but saying that the Tribute was very good.

On 7th November 2011 - no rating submitted

Blackthorn _ left this review about The Swan Inn

An good sized looking pub situated on the main street just down from the harbour. Inside it is perhaps a little disappointing, not really living up to what you might expect. In fact with it's blue carpet and blue bench seating, it reminds me more of a Conservative club than your average pub.

There's a large fan and some wood panelling on the ceiling giving a slightly colonial feel to the place. There's a plasma at one end and a fruit machine, with a quiz machine and pool table at the other. There seemed to be a few chavs in on a recent Saturday night visit.

Beers on tap were Courage Best and Doom Bar. Ciders were Strongbow and Weston's Scrumpy Supreme.

On 21st September 2010 - rating: 5
