Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Simpsons Tavern

The small, wedge-shaped Gentleman's Bar has just 4 seats, with most customers standing outside in the adjacent alleyway. Traditional wooden furniture and decor, but not too dark a shade. To the other side of the main entrance is the Grill Room, with the Wine Bar downstairs and Restaurant at first-floor level. Four of the five handpumps were working, offering the regular Bass (£3.95, served in the right glass), Pride, Doom Bar and Southwold. NB - Only open between 11:30 and 15:30, Mondays to Fridays.

Clive Thompson left this review about Simpsons Tavern

One of London’s old drinking dens, dating back to the 18th Century, that gets busy with City workers most lunchtimes and evenings. It’s tucked away in the City’s ‘Lanes’ so might take a bit of finding. There is an upstairs restaurant and grill house plus a wine bar at ground level where you’ll also find a small traditional corridor bar with real ales available. Watch out for the ’no mobiles’ rule.

Alan Winfield left this review about Simpsons Tavern

The Simpsons Tavern is an old pub located in a court yard just off cornhill.
Once inside this pub has an old feel to it with wood pannelled walls the left side was more of a restaurant and the right side had a bar with mainly stand up drinking as there wer'nt many seats.
There were two real ales on here i had a drink of Harveys bitter and this was a very nice drink the other beer was Bass the Bass has been served here for years.
The pub was full of suits but it had a decent atmosphere to it.
I quite liked this pub and would have enjoyed it more at a quieter time of day.
