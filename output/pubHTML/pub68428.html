E TA left this review about The Mariner

Formerly The Jubilee and The Carpenters Arms, this is a traditional busy seaside pub consisting of a single large bar on the ground floor of an impressive 3 storey building. There are tv screens and a pool table in the single large carpeted bar. Three ales on, I had a Caledonian Flying Scotsman which I found to be in perfect condition. There were seats outside at the front with views of the Harbour, but you could only take a plastic (as opposed to a glass) if you wanted to sit ouside. They advertise regular pub quiz nights. A decent boozer, as much a part of the seaside experience as fish and chips and buckets and spades, all of which were also present.

Steve C left this review about The Mariner

Six picnic tables with views of the harbour line the frontage of the Mariner. The interior is L shaped and at the rear of the pub are a pool table and a large plasma screen that was airing muted Sky Sports News. I wasn’t surprised to not see any Sky Sports advertisements. There was some background music playing from the likes of Frank Sinatra. I found the décor to be very comfortable and the nautical theme running throughout isn’t in your face or tacky.
Alongside the some standard draught taps are three hand pumps that were drawing Sharp’s Doom bar, Caledonian’s Flying Scotsman and Robinson’s Uncle Sams.

I thought that the service was very friendly and I would have no problem returning.

Clive Thompson left this review about The Mariner

There are two pubs on the Stade that are like chalk and cheese, this one and the Ship. The Mariner is run by an old school couple, at least I assumed they were a couple. On my first solo visit the guv'nor was engaging with his, mostly elderly, clientele by playing 40's and 50's music and encouraging a singalong. I came back the next lunchtime with some friends and we had a couple of relaxing pints of Summer Lightning, watching the tide come into the harbour with an easy listening sountrack playing in the background. Just what we needed after a heavy day on the lash the day before. This is the sort of place where time stands still, if you allow it to.
