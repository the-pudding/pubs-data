Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Tariff & Dale

Trendy bar (up a half-flight of stairs) and restaurant (downstairs). Stripped-out interior with metal columns propping up the ceiling and mostly whitewashed brick walls. What fittings there are have an industrial feel, with just a basic cage for bottled drinks adding a slightly unusual note. Just Ultra Pale from Outstanding (which it wasn't really, especially at £4.20) from one of the pair of handpumps.

ROB Camra left this review about Tariff & Dale

Interesting, well designed, modern decor in this fairly new bar. The bar is to the left as you enter and even further to the left round a corner is a hidden away pool table. There are alcoves and booths for a more private conversation. There's a great B & W picture of a girl swigging champagne from a bottle. Fairly busy with the post work crowd on my Friday early evening visit, but still comfortable and the bar staff were efficient. 2 handpumps on the bar serving First Chop DOC and Outstanding Extra Pale. The DOC was in good nick, but set me back £3.80. I liked it in here and the other half will love it.

Al Bundy left this review about Tariff & Dale

A pub/restaurant with the restaurant downstairs. The bar is to your left as you enter. Though its not, it does resemble a cellar bar with bare walls and 6 pillars surrounding the bar. Has 2 handpumps serving a First Chop beer and an Outstanding Brewery beer. Several craft beers on draught too. Has caged bottles of wine next to the bar too. Its a narrow bar with American diner kinda seating.
