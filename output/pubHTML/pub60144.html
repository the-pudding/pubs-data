Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Nursery Inn

Substantial backstreet local, retaining its original layout with four remarkable rooms (each with their own servery) around the central servery. All very tidy, with variations of highly attractive 1930s furniture and decor in each area. It was too dark to see the rear bowling green properly when I visited, but you could see this being a fine spot in sunny weather. As a Hydes house, 1863 (Original) Bitter, Lowry, Old Indie mild (£2.54) and the seasonal Hoppy Appeal and First Frost were available from various handpumps scattered around the counters. Already busy late on a Friday afternoon, and you could see why; this class act is well worth a special visit.

ROB Camra left this review about The Nursery Inn

This is an ex CAMRA National Pub Of The Year (2001). It's a recently, sympathetically, refurbished community pub right in the middle of a good quality estate. Two large rooms, both nicely done, with a small snug with a separate entrance to the side. Lot's of great wood panelling as part of its "Nationally Important Pub Interior". Well priced, good looking meals were coming out of the kitchen on my Friday, early evening visit. 9 handpumps on the bar, beers are mainly from Hydes including their Beer Studio micro, plus a couple of guest ales. My pint was in very good nick and I settled down in the busy, buzzy front room. This is a very well run pub and if I lived around here this would be my local.

Real Ale Ray left this review about The Nursery Inn

This was our first pub to visit on the day and what a good place to start. We walked up from the station and this was at least a 20 min walk. We arrived just on opening time which is 11.30. First job was to sample their Hydes beers, which I have to say, were all kept extremely well. The manager and staff were friendly and chatty and made you feel very welcome. We decided to stay for lunch as prices were good and when we saw what was coming out of the kitchen, how could we refuse - so top marks to the food also.
There were seven ales on, so we went for the Hydes Manchester Bitter, Lowry and 1893 Light Mild. Great pub and well worth a visit.

On 24th April 2014 - rating: 10
