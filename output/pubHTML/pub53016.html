Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Hawkenbury

Country pub at the end of a small terrace on the road between Staplehurst and Headcorn; so, although it is located in a hamlet, it does get some passing trade. It has a pleasantly traditional interior, food is offered (indeed, there are two smart dining rooms), various events are advertised, and there is a self-proclaimed 'mega beer garden' (with children's play area) and further patio tables along the frontage and by the side entrance, so there is something here for most people. Just Pride and Hobgoblin Gold (£4.00) were available from two of four handpumps on this overdue re-visit, but the chalk-board suggests a variety of beers (often local) are usually offered.
