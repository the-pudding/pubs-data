Will Larter left this review about The Inn At Whitewell

Well, so this is how the other half lives. This is a seriously posh hotel and apparently you need to have a really big car to drive here. I arrived on my bicycle, but I wasn't alone, as a group of cyclists turned up soon after me and also found their way to the bar. There's an old white-washed pub-like building to the left of the smart hotel, but the door here merely admits to the hotel and a longish walk then ensues, past the foyer just inside the main hotel entrance and around the back where there is a fantastic view over the river at the rear of the hotel. I assume there's an extra charge for looking at the view, as I paid £2.10 for a half of Black Sheep Bitter. The others available were Timothy Taylor Landlord and Pendle Witches Brew, with a further pump clip turned around, which seemed to be Tirril Kirkstone Gold. There's a patio garden overlooking the river, but I was content to stay in the room with the view, getting my money's worth.

Date of visit: 15th June 2017
