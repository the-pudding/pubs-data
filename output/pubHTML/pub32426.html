Quinno _ left this review about The Leopard

Another of Burlem’s traditional two-roomers, this is a large one with many intact Victorian stylings with the rooms one behind the other. Sadly the place is pretty tatty and there were very few customers in on a Saturday night, unlike all the other pubs we visited within a few hundred yards walk. A wheels of steel was blaring out a disco to no-one in particular and it all felt rather sad. Still, we plonked ourselves down on the bottle green banquettes in the front bar. There were a few ales on but not much goodness on the quality front overall – the Coach House Toffee was vinegar and went back and the replacement Bass was sub-standard. Desmond at least got a drinkable Otter. It could be a lovely place that competes with the other big hitters in Burslem, but at present any pub that can get barely more than 8 people in on a Saturday night and then serves up poor quality beer is staring over the precipice. I'll be generous with a 5 as the inside is well-worth a look.

Alan Winfield left this review about The Leopard

The Leopard is in the middle of Burslem.
This is quite a nice pub with a couple of rooms and a dining area at the back of the pub.
I went in the lounge which was quite smart and fairly busy there was a good choice of beers on the bar i had a drink of Titanic Lifeboat which was very nice the other beers were Woods Shropshire Lass, Archers IPA and a beer from Milestone there was also a real cider on but dont know what it was called.
This is a decent pub to have a few drinks in and i would be happy to come back here again.
