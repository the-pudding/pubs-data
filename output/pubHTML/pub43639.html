Rob Hunter left this review about Royal Oak

Paid a visit today while in Helmsley. The layout is as previously described, whereby you enter into a small lobby and have a door to the left labelled LOUNGE, and one to the right labelled BAR. The bar feels traditional, with bare boards, dark wood and some ornate carving on the bar. The room to the right has a few tables, a pool table and a wood burner. This then loops round in a horseshoe round the bar to the lounge - though I did not investigate this area.

Three real ales on the hand pulls, today offering Pedigree (£1.90 for a half), Razor Back and Wainwright. The Pedigree was served with a particularly generous head.

The Royal Oak feels like a fairly nice down to earth pub, but the beer selection would not have me rushing back.

Will Larter left this review about Royal Oak

There's a choice of doors in the lobby when entering the Royal Oak. The lounge to the left looked smart, so I went to the right where the bar has bare boards and a dark wood bar counter with rails and carvings. There were three hand pumps serving Wainwright, Hobgoblin and Ringwood Wild Boar. I went for the latter, which turned out to be probably the best beer I've had recently from the Marstons stable. There was some background music, a solid fuel stove, and a smattering of local drinkers. It's slightly more expensive here than across the square at the Feathers, and about £1 a pint dearer than the Helmsley tap, but that wasn't open on this midweek night. Would I return? Not with a skip in my step.

On 1st December 2017 - rating: 5
