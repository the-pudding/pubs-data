moby duck left this review about Ape & Apple

I called in on the Saturday morning after Aqualungs visit,Its as he described of course, there are 11 handpumps in total all clipped but only the three beers available, Bitter,IPA and Two Hoots at the very reasonable price of £2.93, one solitary handle was dispensing Old Rosie Cider.
It had a reasonably pubby feel but it wouldn't be anywhere near my first port of call on a crawl, more like an after thought.

Aqualung . left this review about Ape & Apple

My second Holts pub of the day turned out to be another bank conversion so not a typical example. It's a single rectangular room which to be fair to them has been made to look like a pub with a mix of carpets and tiles. It was busy but not rammed on an early Friday evening visit.
There are a number of hand pumps but I only noticed Holt's Bitter, IPA & Two Hoots. The Bitter (£2.93) was in good nick. I thought this place was OK. It was good to see it wasn't overrun with TV screens.

Malden man left this review about Ape & Apple

This is a solid enough Holt's house, a large rectangular room at ground floor with a few dividing screens to one end. The bar is on the back wall, the centre area is tiled then either boarded or carpeted depending upon which side you choose to sit. A fair bit of exposed brickwork to one end with dressing mirrors mounted on it, floral wallpaper elsewhere. Mixed seating of all known types, local photos of yesteryear, the inside of the entrance has an ornate wooden feature with a clock on the top. Holt's beers were Bitter, Mild, Two Hoots and IPA plus a rather uninspired line of guests in Bombardier, Doom Bar, OSH plus one called Bootleg Racketeer. Standard pub style meals are served.
There is apparently a rooftop courtyard but I didn't bother to explore this, all in all a solid and seemingly dependable city centre boozer.

On 15th November 2015 - rating: 6
