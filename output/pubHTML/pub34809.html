Steve of N21 left this review about The Greyhound

Family run free house in the Suffolk village of Chevington.
Internally it looks like it was historically a series of separate rooms, but is now a collection of interconnecting spaces around the central bar, which includes a lounge, a restaurant room and a further room housing a pool table at the back on the way to the large pub garden out back. Plenty of traditional
Pub bric a brac adorn the walls, including a plate collection and a deer’s head in the bar area. The dining room has a collection of Indian cloths due to the fact that as well as a standard pub food menu , there is also an interesting curry menu where the curries are graded 1 to 12 depending on strength.
Can’t comment on the food as we were only here for the beer. In this respect there are two handpumps on the bar dispensing two Adnams brews. And although my pint of bitter was perfectly fine, I’nm surprised that was enough to get this place in the 2012 GBG..

On 31st October 2013 - rating: 6
