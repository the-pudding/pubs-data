Quinno _ left this review about The Water Mill

A large pub, with a traditionally decorated exterior giving it a tastefully aged look. The inside is surprisingly spacious (perhaps a little too vast in places?) with a central staple-shaped bar (with some attractive picked colour screens on the gantry). Off to the left is a bar billiards table in full working order and to the right is a newly-opened extension housing a pool table, dart boards and the newly-furbished toilets. Plasma screens were dotted around which on our very quiet Sunday visit were showing motocross, though nobody actually appeared to be following proceedings. Décor is perhaps a little tired in places and maybe a little tasteful brightening-up might not go amiss. The beers were We Valley HPA, Harveys and GK Abbot, with one clip turned. My HPA was in fine fettle as you’d expect from a GBG-listed pub. We settled in the bar billiards area and hogged the table for a couple of hours. Quite nice in that area, with a collection of books/magazines plus some nice individually-lit landscape paintings. Chap behind the bar was friendly and it felt like a nice place to be. Worth seeking out if you like a traditional local pub with good beer and punter-friendly facilities and I’d happily pop in again.

On 7th August 2013 - rating: 8

Steve C left this review about The Water Mill

The Water Mill Inn is a stone’s throw from Wivelsfield Railway Station and is open until midnight every night except Friday and Saturday when it is open until 1:00. There is live music every Friday night, poker every Wednesday and a quiz on Thursdays. The bar area is L shaped with a couple of plasma screens and bar billiards. There is a large extension to the right that houses another plasma screen, a pool table and two dartboards. Sky Sports is shown and the Carling Cup semi-final was being watched by a few locals. The bar is stocked with standard draught and two banks of four hand pumps were drawing Cornish Coaster, Harvey’s Best and Summer Lightening with the forth being unused.
There is a grass covered area with some picnic tables to the right of the pub.

The service was friendly and the locals were a good laugh. I liked it in here and would have no problem returning.

On 15th January 2012 - rating: 7
