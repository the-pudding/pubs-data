Graham Coombs left this review about The Joseph Conrad (JD Wetherspoon)

Large 'Spoon's opposite the station on the end of the shopping street, in the former Tuttle's department store. The main bar feels a bit packed in and enclosed, but there is a more-pleasant and brighter upstairs bar as well. The beer range is fair, with several local ales on offer (fewer upstairs though). Overall quite a nice atmosphere.

Aqualung . left this review about The Joseph Conrad (JD Wetherspoon)

This one is named after a Victorian EU migrant who wrote a book called Lord Jim which was about a bloke called Jim who was also a Lord.
As you might guess from the address it's right by the Rail Station and is set in a former department store with two levels. There is a lift to the upper floor but it seemed to have been commandeered for the staff by placing an empty delivery rack in it. The downstairs area is roughly square with a wood floor and slightly better than usual fixtures and furnishings. It was busy on my Saturday afternoon visit with a mix of people.
The bar has two sets of five hand pumps which had the JDW Trio Of Doom doubled up, a Green Jack house beer named after the EU migrant above, Trawlerboys Best & Red Robin and St Peter's India Pale Ale. I went for the St Peter's beer (£2.49) which was in good nick.
It's not a bad Spoons by any means but somehow on a Saturday afternoon I found it a bit dull.
