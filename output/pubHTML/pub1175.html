Thuck Phat left this review about The Stag

The Stag is a nice old building which is quite a walk from the town centre. To be honest it probably isn't worth it.
Although from the outside the pub looks very pretty and welcoming, inside it's been Fullers'd with a modern makeover which includes two big screens at either end of the single room. These were showing footie on our visit which was being watched by a few punters at the bar and in seats whilst the odd group was eating. There's a fair bit of wood in evidence and plenty of deer pictures on the wall in an attempt to give a traditional feel.
Beer was Fullers Pride and Front Row along with Ossett Big Red as a guest. The Pride was in reasonable nick although not the best I've ever had.
A bit disappointing really.

On 6th March 2013 - rating: 5

Bucking Fastard left this review about The Stag

Occupying a fork in the road,the building pre dates the Victorian expansion of the town,it's probably Georgian.However once inside there has been an unsympathetic refurb from Fullers with an overly long bar running down the righthand wall which would be better suited in a city centre bar.The open plan interior with a low ceiling includes two flat screens for sport and an unusable dartboard towards the front.Food is served and each table had a small candle,but most punters were in for a drink.Muzak was blaring out.
Although there were six handpumps,the three choices were doubled up and none of the stronger ,and characterful,Fullers beers were on offer on my visit.London Pride and the Six Nations themed Front Row(3.7%)were complemented by a guest in the shape of Ossett Big Red.
The exterior promises a lot more than is delivered inside,another rather dull Leighton Buzzard boozer.

On 23rd February 2013 - rating: 6
