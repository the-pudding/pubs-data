Old Boots left this review about The Countrymans Inn

A double fronted stone built pub with bay windows. On entering there is a single public bar across the front, the counter is straight head while round the back is a dining room. Five pulls here with Theakstons plus guests usually from Wensleydale it seems, decently kept as well. It has low dark beams, a stone fireplace and a laminate floor as the pub was extensively renovated a few years back but in a traditional style. General country decor of horse furniture and tools and objects of unknown purpose, a pump clip collection is fixed to the beams over the counter. Definitely a good village pub don't worry about the dining room, the food is good but this isn't a pub themed restaurant.
