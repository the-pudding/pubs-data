Tris C left this review about The Anchor

Dating from at least the mid-19th century and rebuilt in the 1930s for Hall's, this pub is somewhat isolated, all alone at the northern tip of Jericho.
On arriving, one thing you notice is the Hall's ceramic plaque as well as the HOB monogrammed ironwork. Inside, I can't remember the last time I saw such a drab pub, in effect completely devoid of any décor and painted off-white. The floor to the bar area is bare boarded but the neighbouring room, where tables are laid for dining, has a black and white chequerboard floor. The bar room has black leatherette peripheral bench seating and to be fair, a rather attractive fireplace. In the bar, an elderly and seemingly intellectual group mused over the political leanings of David Aaronovitch. The eating area seemed empty, with a young attractive (hooray?) cadre posing on the beer patio; music was muted as was the lighting in the gent's cubicle as the bulb had failed - do the staff here not check their facilities?
Ales: One pump, clip reversed, Vale of Glamorgan's Speak Easy which went off as I arrived, Wadsworth's 6X and IPA at a horrific £4.50 a pint - I know Jericho's pricey but nowhere around here charges this much. This is the kind of price I'd expect to pay in one of London's top ten most expensive postcodes.
The dreary pub, dreary choice of ale, remote location and insulting prices mean that I will not be returning.

On 3rd September 2016 - rating: 3

Quinno _ left this review about The Anchor

Imposing and impressive 1930’s pub building with some original features (doors especially) but now firmly in the young upmarket dining set. Three ales on, all Waddies; 6X, Henry's IPA and a so-so Bishops Tipple. Extremely busy and one particular young lady hooray seemed aggrieved that I needed to ask her stand aside so I could reach the gents. A box ticked, anyway. 5.5
