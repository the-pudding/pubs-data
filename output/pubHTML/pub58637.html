Alex Conway left this review about The Nag's Head

This pub is full of potential but sadly on my visit showed all the signs of a pub in an inevitable downward spiral to closure with the to let sign draped across the front doing nothing to help matters.

But once inside the pub is a pretty tidy old place, fully carpeted and plenty of nice cushioned seating. But it was dead! not a sole in the place except me and my party of four. Admittedly it was a Wednesday but come half past eight I would have expected a few more customers. this enivitably led to a very dull and quiet atmosphere.

there were four hand pumps with John smiths and Doombar (both £3.10) both being repeated, i had a john Smiths and it was kept very well and was a very nice drink.

This pub is a great shame as it has all the features off a good village pub but without customs will see certain closure in the future, sadly at current rates I give it a year unless something can change.

** update September 2016
new owners, new lease of life , bustling and lively at 4 on a Saturday afternoon, they now have 2 guests beers and a cask cider along side the old guard of cask John smiths and doom bar and he only pub in york to sell John smiths magnet bitter! £3.25 and a danm sight better than the smooth flow crap, not sure it will replace my real ale but it's worth a try to sample this hard to find beer! this pub is improving

Alan Winfield left this review about The Nag's Head

I thought this was a really nice pub with a lounge at the front and a bar running to the bach on the right hand side.
The lounge was furnished in a way that i like and there was also a seating area up a couple of steps.
There were two real ales on which were York Yorkshire Terrier this was very nice there was also John Smiths cask on i thought this was a really decent pub and i would be very happy to come here again.
