Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Dartford Working Men's Club

Mostly single-storey club, with the entrance set between two bow windows decorated with hanging baskets. Fairly typical, slightly dated but well-maintained, club interior with plenty of seating but also very busy on a Sunday lunchtime (especially as the rear room had been booked for an event). Fortunately, the autumn weather was still benign, so the round wooden tables on the front terrace were another option. Excellent choice of real ales in a full range of styles from 14 of 15 handpumps on the main bar counter, with my pint of DBE Chesil cask pilsner coming in at just £2.80. Only surprise was finding just one beer from Kent (and the other end of the county at that), but you can see why this place is a multiple Camra-award winner (and you can gain admission with a valid membership card).
