Alex Conway left this review about The White Horse

A very rough and ready locals Thwaites brewery pub on the edge of the city walls. The pub was very busy on my Friday afternoon visit with a large group of visiting lads from Newcastle. based on the type of pub i found the beers very expensive, A pint of Lancaster Bomber (Wainwright was off) and a Thwaites seasonal brown ale worked out at £3.50 each. We sat in the corner near the coal fire that sadly was not on and enjoyed our drinks in relative peace.

Defiantly not a tourist pub and one I would only re vist if ever doing a crawl along the other pubs down Bootham, been in worse though.

Steve C left this review about The White Horse

The White Horse was rammed with party folk during a recent Saturday afternoon visit. After being released from a hug by a burly drunken reveller I made my way to the bar to find a selection of standard draught products alongside four hand pumps. One of these was unused and the Wainwright was off leaving Lancaster Bomber and Chin Chin as the ale options. I’m a fan of Thwaites beers and enjoyed a half of both available options.

The interior consists of seating to the left and a bar that runs along the back central wall. To the right is a pool table and plasma screens for showing live sport are located throughout.

I found the service to be very friendly and there was a good vibe in this pub. It is not often I find a busy pub with drunken patrons that I enjoyed visiting, but this was certainly one of them.

On 19th April 2015 - rating: 7
