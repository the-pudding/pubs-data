Aqualung . left this review about The Pennsylvanian (JD Wetherspoon)

Given the Provincial Town nature of the High Street that this Spoons resides in it is surprisingly large.
It is on paper a Lloyds No 1, but at lunchtime was very much a standard Wetherspoons.

It's a long and narrow shop conversion with the bar in the middle and a small area at the rear where some natural light comes in. Of the twelve pumps the usual GK suspects were doubled up and there were a couple of punchy ciders on. Of the six remaining pumps there were two Rebellion beers and the Portobello American IPA plus others from the guest list. Sadly two of these were "Available Soon".

At least there was no sign of any Doombar or London Pride in here today. I went for the Portobello American IPA at £2.49 which was in fairly good nick.

Bucking Fastard left this review about The Pennsylvanian (JD Wetherspoon)

A standard shop conversion 'spoons with three internal sections and as previously mentioned somewhat lacking in natural light.The frontage can fully open out on hot days,and the first section on entering is the quietest area with a community board.The middle vaulted section included a long bar along the left hand wall,while beyond this is a third area in a library style extending to exits at the rear of building.
At first sight 12 handpumps would set the ale drinkers pulse racing,yet on my visit Abbott and Ruddles Bitter were doubled up and there were no less than five "available soon" clips which all failed to materialise on my session.However my Mordue Workie Ticket was in good condition and there does seem to be a caring attitude behind the bar.
A popular watering hole ,this is a viable ale drinking venue among an uninspiring pub choice in this attractive town centre.
