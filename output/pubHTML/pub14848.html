Please Note: This review is over a year old.

Komakino . left this review about The King Harry

Under the clutches of M&B, this old pub is located next to a busy mini-roundabout and opposite a tree-shaded church. Some old touches such as low beams in the bar area, a bare wood floor and brick fireplace, this rises from the sunken bar area, served by a snorkel-shaped bar up to a thin dining area. Ales were a bit of a let-down with St Austell Proper Job (x2), TT Landlord (x2) and Sharp's Doom Bar, but a bottle of Thornbridge Wye (cucumber pale ale) was a bit of a saviour, albeit at an alum-mouthed £4.75. I learned this was a vestige from a recent Thornbridge tap takeover, so it's clear the ale range isn't always this anodyne.
