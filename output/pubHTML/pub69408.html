Steve of N21 left this review about Railway Arms

Popped in for a quick one whilst killing some time before a suitable train to Euston and not a lot seems to have changed in the intervening six years since the last review.
The locals are still a friendly chatty bunch as is the current landlord, the teles are still on but with volume at a non obtrusive level and the ales are predominantly GK, with IPA on and Abbott reversed for my visit.
Fortunately the third pump had Hopback Summer Lightning on at a promotion price of £3.50 a pint, presumably to move the barrel. My pint certainly had an end of barrel feel to it but was still better than a pint of IPA would’ve been.
So still a decent corner pub close to Bushy station in which to grab a quick pint, especially now if you have a smart phone with the National rail app which will allow you to time your exit from the pub to perfection.
Only additional point to add is that the pub does not have a kitchen so there is no food available bar the usual range of bar snacks

E TA left this review about Railway Arms

This friendly Greene King pub is a good, lively local which, due to its location, also makes a good pitstop when leaving the railway station. The Victorian corner building has been knocked through to form a single, large U-shaped bar, although a change of flooring from wood to carpet remarks the 'saloon' area from the 'public'. The regulars were friendly and the staff pleasant and helpful. Large tv screens were the only real downside, but these were not too intrusive. Free wifi is available. Three ales on, all GK of course, but I did enjoy my pint of Fireside. Worth a visit, and I would include in a crawl.

On 29th October 2013 - rating: 6
