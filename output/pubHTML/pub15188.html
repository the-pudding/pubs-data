E TA left this review about The Vine Inn

A pleasant location overlooking the village green, The Vine has a single large bar which is full of character and a smaller games room to the rear with a pool table. There was a varied menu which included local fish. It gets very busy during tourist season but was fairly quiet this time, with just a few locals in. They run regular pub quizzes. Three ales on draft, plus various Badger beers in bottles. I had Ringwood Best and Itchen's Tea Clipper, both on good form though served just a bit too cool - possibly why they didn't make the 2012 GBG. It's well worth finding if you're driving, and it's worth the walk from Bembridge Marina if sailing in.

Soup Dragon left this review about The Vine Inn

An unrendered brick built corner pub, with double front gable and covered porch. in the GBG 2010

The interior is open plan and near enough one long room that ends with a raised pool and dart area. The decor is mainly white, with olive greeny colour and wood panel. It does food, but i didnt eat. There wasnt a lot in the way of pictures, apart from one corner and there was TV. The service was ok and the clientele a full mix, it is equally popular with families and holidaymakers as locals.

Beer; tap stuff, with Bass, London Pride and a decent Goddard's Inspiration.

An all round solid pub, with nice views of the green - i think this is why the cricketers are on the sign! it always seems to be busy when i have visited and i am sure i will be back.
