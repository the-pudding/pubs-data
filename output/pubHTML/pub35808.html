Graham Coombs left this review about Anchor & Horseshoes

An attractive and quite old pub, as evidenced by the ceiling beams, with traditional furnishings. There are several doors, although the interior is all joined through. The small front bar hosts a dart board and a TV, with the middle part opening out into a fair-sized room with tables. Garden at rear. Beers seemed in fair condition, even if a little unambitious with GK IPA, Abbot, Doom Bar and Tribute on pump. Food not sampled but looked ok. Car park is pay and display with cost refunded on bar sales. A good, traditional, no-frills pub.
