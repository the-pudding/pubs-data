Bucking Fastard left this review about The Duke

You enter into a wooden floored bar area with wooden tables and chairs and a large banquette to the front,further back opposite the bar there is a rank of high tables by a mirrored wall,with a seperate square dining room to the side.The open kitchen is in one corner ,with the chef turning out decent food.There are lunchtime specials at £6.50 which seemed great value,there is a shortish menu with small bite and main meals (£10-£15) and the lunch I had was very decent,it deserves it's gastro label.
On the ale front there are 3 handpumps drawing on my trip Doom Bar,Sambrooks Wandle and Twickenham Grandstand (murky but drinkable).An unexciting range.The 10 keg taps had a lot of the usdual dross,but some craft beers from the likes of Siren Craft and Harviestoun but I wasnt convinced that much care was being taken here on the beer front.
Not a natural for a real ale visit,but decent food if that's your market.My lunchtime visit did not allow the views that previous reviewers enjoyed.

Quinno _ left this review about The Duke

More gastrobar than gastropub. Notes and memory sketchy but there was a mirrored right hand side plus some jockey prints. A dining room was off to the right side. Lots of posh mature local totty amongst the clientèle - Desmond, Jimbo and my good self were copping an eyeful of the pins and cleavages on display. Three ales on; Doom Bar, West Berks Mr Chubbs and an excellent Twickenham Grandstand. Also a selection of ‘craft’ wotnottery. Surprisingly not as dire as I thought it would be on first entering.
