Al Bundy left this review about Hole in the Wall

A very good pub pub situated just off the main drag. Two roomed plus a small area that you pass if going into the left side room. The two rooms were showing some England Rugby international and despite that, and some nonsense about having to "stand for the Queen" when the national anthem was played, the pub is worth a moment of your time. 6+ handpulls with a good variety of styles.

Will Larter left this review about Hole in the Wall

At any other time this back street local just off the main street in Long Eaton would be my sort of pub. However, as the 17th pub on my Alan Winfield Memorial Pub Crawl it was all I could do to make myself understood when ordering my beer, while simultaneously propping myself up on the bar without (I hoped) seeming incapable of standing without propping myself up on the bar. There were six hand pumps in this very pleasant pub, with a real cider and five real ales (see photos). I went for a half of Bass, which I seem to recall was in good condition, though I may be kidding myself.

Date of visit: 5th September 2018

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Hole in the Wall

Side-street pub of the old school, with a busy public bar and (up a couple of steps) a rather quieter lounge. Traditional furniture and decor throughout. Five real ales on handpump - Extra Pale Ale, Legend (£3.00) and Centurion ND from Nottingham, the regular Bass, and Bishop's Farewell from Oakham - plus a potent 7.5% Broadoak KB cider.

On 4th February 2017 - rating: 7
