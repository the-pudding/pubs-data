Real Ale Ray left this review about The Grayston Unity

We really enjoyed our visit here and managed to get a seat at the end of a table near the bar. Five handpumps along the small rear bar, plus a choice of pork pies and samosas. I went for the Goose Eye Chinook, which was in excellent condition and went hand in hand, with a vegetable samosa. Prices in here were also good, l paid £3.10 for my pint.

On 19th May 2019 - rating: 8

Will Larter left this review about The Grayston Unity

Well described by OB below. I would only add that the pub is quite easy to find either from Broad Street (if coming from the Pump Room micropub) or from Commercial Street (if coming from the Victorian Cafe or the Alexandra). I enjoyed the slightly chaotic mix of random rooms and ad hoc furnishings, which somehow add up successfully to a welcoming and comfortable pub. Though I can imagine it wouldn't float everyone's boat. Beer was in very good condition and served enthusiastically. There was an interesting choice of music (reggae and ska while I was here) and a friendly atmosphere.

ROB Camra left this review about The Grayston Unity

As described below by OB. There's also a small outside area with 4 tables and chairs where they have live music. It was pleasantly busy on our Thursday evening visit with lots of chat going on. Goose Eye beers are the house brew here plus 2 changing guests. My pint of Saltaire Solaris was in good nick. We'll certainly be calling in here again.
