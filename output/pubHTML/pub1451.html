Quinno _ left this review about The Bridge House

Newly refurbed and reopened after a long period of closure. A pleasingly multi-area’d interior and done out in white and duck egg shades that whilst obviously new isn’t overly clinical and will feel fairly cosy after getting that lived-in feel. Pub is welcoming to diners and drinkers with three ales on; Rebellion IPA, Wells Poppy and Harveys Sussex Best. Both Desmond and I went a bundle on the Harveys and were rewarded with a first class beer, served-up by a friendly bar lady. Also noted an obvious wine rack to the side of the bar, so presumably a better-than-usual selection. A major talking point between Desmond and I was the black and white photo of various stars of the 70’s outside a pub (turns out it’s the Belgian Arms in Holyport, the landlord’s previous place, after a charity golf tournament) stars included Tarby, Brucie, Ronnie Corbett, Sean Connery plus a load of others whose faces we recognised but couldn’t conjure a name for. Also there’s a collection of framed cigarette cards. Pub is home to a small sausage dog, so small you might actually tread on him, so watch out! Smelt like a log fire was on the go somewhere but we didn’t spot it. Football was about to be put on the telly, perhaps a little surprising at first but given the next pub down is a very posh eatery (they do food here, of course), this one’s going for the more local market. Overall this was a very encouraging first impression – I’d be up for popping back in a few months to see how things pan out.

Cheryl Bailey left this review about Bridge House

Pub looks welcoming on the outside. But, scruffy bar, scruffy furniture and no one there on a supposidly busy Saturday night should ring warning bells! The food was horrendous. Very poor menu followed by very poor food. If we were driving we would have walked straight back out the door. All we can say is sack the chef! If you want rubbish food, over priced wine and no atmosphere go the Bridge House.
