Alan Winfield left this review about The Patch

The Patch is yet another corner pub on lordship lane.
Once inside there was an L shaped room to the front which had a large area to the front which had leather settees to the left,with normal tables and chairs to the right,the room is bare boarded like most pubs in this area of London,there was an area to the rear wihch was set up for diners with tables laid out.
There were two real ales on the bar,i had a drink of Exmoor Gold (£2.20 for a half)this was a decent drink but i had to ask for it to be topped up and at that price i would have expected dancing girls or waiter service, preferbly both to serve me,the other beer was Adnams Broadside.
The pub was fairly busy on my mid Saturday afternoon visit,this pub was ok for one drink but at those prices no more than that.
