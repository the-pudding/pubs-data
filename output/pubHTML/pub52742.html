Bucking Fastard left this review about The Dolphin

A Robinson's tied house,a street corner local away from the town centre but close to bridge 40 on the Macclesfield Canal.There is a pleasingly traditional layout with a front snug to the left of the entrance ,another snug with bar access to the right,and a rear section with an iron Victorian fireplace with tiling and two long bench seats .It's all very authentic,although live sport will be played on the flatscreen in the bar room snug.There is a seperate pool room at the back of the building and several sports trophies are on display,so it's very much a community pub with no sign of food.
The 5 handpumps were offering on my trip Robinsons Wizard,Dizzy Blonde and Unicorn doubled up (in decent nick).
A solid boozer with some good photos of Macc dotted about,fine if you like Robbies ales.

On 4th August 2017 - rating: 6

Alan Winfield left this review about The Dolphin

The Dolphin is a typical back street pub that is at the end of a row of terraced houses and it is well away from the middle of Macclesfield.
This pub is a Robinsons tied house and it was fairly comfortable there were two Robinsons beers on the bar i had a drink of Robinsons bitter and this was very nice the other beer was Robinsons mild.
