Quinno _ left this review about The Maltsters Arms

Attractive whitewashed Brakspear pub. As mentioned below by another esteemed local reviewer, the interior is indeed rather neo-rustic (but none the worse for it). Split between drinking and dining, as the normal way round here, with a snug central bar area. Three Marston list ales at the bar – my 64 Deep turned out to have gone the same way its name alluded, bottom of the barrel, but was exchanged with apology by the manager when I trundled back with it (the clip having being turned round in the 2 minutes I was away). My replacement (which I forgot to note) was fine. Not going to knock any socks off feet but solid enough.

. Wittenden left this review about The Maltsters Arms

Attractive part whitewashed pub in an affluent and attractive village. Mainly aimed at the lunching and dining brigade,but the beer is well kept, if not exactly exciting-from the Marstons stable.Cheery bar staff, and the food was enjoyable on our visit on a Friday lunchtime in October 2016.I'm sure the exterior featured in an episode of Midsomer Murders or Lewis or somesuch hokum.
