Ian Mapp left this review about The Fox Inn

I thought I knew Worcestershire but on spotting this in the GBG, I had no idea where it is. Turns out its less than 10 miles from where I live.

So a walk and a pint. Very remote, yet strangely packed place. 7pm on a Wednesday and we had the last of the traditional wooden tables, next to the huge log burner.

Wye Valley Butty Bach in superb condition.

Gill Smith left this review about The Fox Inn

This was a busy community local on Saturday night when we called in and the friendly landlady served good quality real ales. Tried all 3 available which were Wye Valley Butty Bach, Wye Valley HPA, and Malvern Hills Feel Good. There was a wood burning stove on the left hand side where we sat with a small fan on it to spread the heat. Cosy.

Alan Winfield left this review about The Fox Inn

The Fox Inn is a very small pub that is set well back from the very quiet road.
There is a small area at the front of the pub for outside drinking and this area was packed on our visit as there was a 50th birthday party being celebrated.
Once inside the bar was facing and there was only the one small room this had bare boards to the right with a rug on them,the left side had a stone floor and also had a rug on it,most of the seating was small pews and stools.
There were three real ales on the bar i had a drink of Wye Valley HPA and this was a very nice drink the other beers were Wye Valley Buty Bach and Cannon Royal Arrowhead bitter,there was also a real cider but i car'nt remember the name of it.
This was a proper old fashioned pub and i was very happy having a drink in here.
