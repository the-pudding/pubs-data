Quinno _ left this review about The Coach & Horses

Fullers pub with a nice position overlooking the green. Two rooms, low ceilinged front bar with not much character. Rear has pool and darts. Generally unkempt - sticky tables, flies in the gents. Quiet on my visit, though those who were there were quite chavvy punters. Three Fullers ales, Chiswick (so-so), Pride and ESB. Seems to get plaudits due its location but frankly it seems no better than the Boathouse down the road when judged as a pub.

Nick Davies left this review about The Coach & Horses

Superbly located little pub on the Krinny (that's the Kine Croft to you). Two small rooms, a functional public bar complete with darts and Sky TV, and a slightly plusher best room which seems popular with the more mature locals. It's fairly cosy in winter, and pleasant to sit outside in summer. In any other country there would be tables and umbrellas half way across the park in summer but no doubt the council jobsworths would get up tight if we did that here. It rightly gets fearfully busy when the fair or any other outdoor event is staged outside. With the usual Fullers range, Chiswick, Pride, ESB in decent condition it's well worth calling in when on a tour of the town's cultural attractions.

On 17th September 2011 - rating: 7
