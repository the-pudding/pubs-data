Paul McScotty left this review about Blane Valley

Situated on the cusp of the main Merchant City pub/bar/club area the Blane Valley is one of the better traditional type pubs in Glasgow - an ornate exterior is complimented by a bright welcoming interior (cream and light orange decor) it has plenty of tables and places to put your drink when chatting. The bar itself is on the right as you enter and whilst not the largest bar area it seems well stocked, nothing out of the ordinary re beer (the usual taps) and some nice whiskeys etc ) prices for the area all seem reasonable (pint Tennenet's - very nice- £3,20 at Sept 2014). The bar is very popular for food and does some traditional food that looked really nice and good value (sirlon steak, Haddock etc). Staff were nice and the punters seem a fairly mixed and decent crowd. Has a couple of flat screen tvs and papers to read during the day. I am told it also has an excellent karaoke night every Friday and Saturday. Well worth a visit for a night out or (like me) a quick pint.
