Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Charlie Hall (JD Wetherspoon)

Mostly unremarkable 'spoons bingo-hall conversion, but the front features a slightly curious raised covered terrace and the main part of the hall-like rear extension has a double-height ceiling for some reason. Usual furniture and decor throughout. Modest selection of real ales from the two banks of five handpumps, including some duplicated clips, with A Tall Christmas Tale from Wickwar (£2.19) being the most interesting selection.

Aqualung . left this review about The Charlie Hall (JD Wetherspoon)

This is an archetypal Spoons barn. It consists of a large high ceilinged rectangular room with the bar on the left. There are all the usual fittings but there is a small extension at the back with a wooden floor and some natural light. This area also leads to the patio and houses the toilets so there is not the usual climb up several flights of stairs.
The bar has two sets of five hand pumps that on my visit had GK IPA & Abbot doubled up, 2 ciders, Doom Bore, B & T Crooked Hooker, Banks's / Arcadia Angler's Ale and Stonehenge Danish Dynamite. I went for the Danish Dynamite which was in first class condition.
This is a fairly average sort of Spoons but it gets an extra point for the beer quality.
