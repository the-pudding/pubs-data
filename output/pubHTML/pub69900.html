Alan Winfield left this review about The Goodfellowship Inn

This is a very large suburban pub in a leafy area of north Hull.
Once inside there was one very large room with the bar facing as you entered, there was a raised area to the front and the main part of this room had mainly normal tables and chairs with a few tall ones dotted about the place the pub seemed comfortable enough,there was also another very large room to the side of the pub which i think would be used for functions.
This pub is a Marstons house and there were three real ales on the bar i had a drink of Marstons Perfect Union and this was quite a nice drink the other beers were Pedigree and Hobgoblin.
The pub was quite busy with a lot of people dining and there was also a large outside drinking area at the back of the pub.
Not really my sort of pub but the beer was decent enough.

Graham Coombs left this review about The Goodfellowship Inn

Massive suburban pub, with adjoining function suites. Main section has a collection of areas clustered round a large central bar, including some smaller side areas and a larger room at the rear. Seems pretty well run and kept in good order - occasional quizzes and other events. A fair selection of beers from the Marston stable seemed drinkable (I was disappointed by the Christmas Pudding ale, but that is not the pub's fault). We didn't try the food but it sounded ok. Only minor gripe was two conflicting lots of music filtering through from functions on a Saturday night, but would be happy to return.

On 13th December 2010 - rating: 8
