Reviews of The London Pride (Average Rating: 2 of 10) Add Review see review guidelines

Komakino . left this review about London Pride

A run-down estate pub, more akin to a Social Club bar in terms of decor, my review of which could and should probably mirror that of The Hind & Hart in Colindale. Part of a concrete mini-shopping concourse, slightly sunken in level from the rest, this had a tarpaulin sign outside proclaiming the place was open. A staggered L-shaped bar is to the right-hand wall and part far corner, with a pool table to the left on entry, three upholstered button bench seats in green, a tall table in the middle of the room and stools against the bar. A small (four) group of very sweary locals - one ordered a taxi to a Bookies in nearby Fenny Stratford during my stay - and no sign of an ale: they'd even run out of Foster's and Kronenbourg. A pint of a dodgy-tasting Stella was £3.40. A jukebox played a selection of, to be fair, decent tunes, and there was a nice large print of a Medieval-looking London on one of the walls. An early contender for my 2019 Worst Pub of the Year - it's easy to see why there have been no previous reviews prior to me.
