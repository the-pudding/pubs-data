Aqualung . left this review about The Woodseats Palace (JD Wetherspoon)

Like the previous reviewer I had this down as a shop conversion and only discovered the cinema connection from one of their boards upstairs. It's a rectangular room with the bar on the left and typical fixtures & fittings.
Available from the ten hand pumps were GK IPA & Abbot, a cider, Little Critter's C Monster, Elland Catch the Rainbow and Bradfield Yorkshire Farmer. I went for the C Monster which was in good nick and went down well with a large breakfast.
It's a reasonable JDW but nothing outstanding.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Woodseats Palace (JD Wetherspoon)

Looks like exactly what it once was, a converted 1960s supermarket, although the name harks back to the cinema that once stood on the site. Absolutely 'spoons by numbers inside, with all the standard furniture and decor. Reasonable selection of real ales from the two banks of five handpumps, though, divided between the usual suspects, more interesting (although not exactly local) beers such as the Springhead Left Lion (£2.45) and offerings from Yeovil, Jurassic, Elland and Gadd's plus a couple of ciders.

On 12th February 2017 - rating: 6
