Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Fox and Hounds

Greene King pub ideally located for those walking in the National Trust woodland high on the Greensand Ridge. The small rooms inside the old part of the bulding have been partially opened out but it has been extended with a dining area to the rear. There is also a fair-sized beer garden to one side which is very popular in fine weather. Annoyingly, they stopped doing food just before we arrived in mid afternoon (although they had moved on to cream teas which seemed to be in demand with others). Three real ales on handpump - IPA, Abbot and a reasonable Morland Original.

On 25th April 2011 - rating: 7
