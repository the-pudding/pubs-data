Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Punch Hotel

The notably ornate façade is always worth a look if walking past (as I have now done quite a few times), but finally deciding to step inside the much-altered interior was sadly as dull as I was expecting. However, it does benefit from being thoroughly unfashionable, and was thus pretty civilised on a post-football Saturday evening when most of the other city centre pubs were getting decidedly 'lively'. Three Marston's group real ales on handpump: Jennings Cumberland, Ringwood Boon Doggle and Old Empire IPA (£3.20).

ROB Camra left this review about The Punch Hotel

A very ornate pub on the outside leads into a fairly bland interior which is smaller than I expected. The left hand room has lots of dark wood panels and the ceiling is interesting. It was very quiet on my Thursday evening visit. There are 3 handpumps, 2 were on and both were badged with the desperately bland Cumberland Ale which was kept well enough. Could be so much better than it is.

Alan Winfield left this review about Punch Hotel

This is a large landmark pub just outside Hulls main shopping centre.
Whenever we have been in the Punch it has been very busy with shoppers but we have always managed to grab a seat,there is always one real ale on the bar which is usually Marstons Bitter this is quite a decent drink.
The pub does cheap pub lunches and also has a carvery which seems very popular.
If you are shopping in Hull with the wife this pub is ideal for a quick drink and refuel.
