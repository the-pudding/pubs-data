E TA left this review about Cams Mill

A fairly new Fullers pub on the main road to Gosport, the pub was built at the entrance to the Carms estate. Flagstone floors and oak boards decorate the interior. Outside there is a carpark which is badly laid out and too small for the trade the pub attracts, but the semi-rural setting is pleasant. There are some tables and chairs outside, but the best view they afford is of the aforementioned carpark, so it's only really smokers and children who sit there. They carry all the Fullers beers, and there is a rather dull selection of wine. Meals looked good, but we did not eat on this occasion. My enjoyment of my pint of Seafarer was marred by its being both too cold and too expensive. The pub is dog friendly, even providing water for our canine cousins convivially to consume. Fullers wifi is available. The biggest drawback is the prices – they are extortionately high for this part of the country (£2.50 for a small bowl of peanuts, for example), and for this reason alone I shall not be returning when there are equally fine pubs, but cheaper, a stone's throw away.

On 9th May 2014 - rating: 6

This is a sort of golf club house, but is open to general public

I was there for a wake mid summer afternoon

Loads of parking, hot food available, staff polite & helpful, chav free zone due to location

Not worth a special trip, but nice to know it is there

Good location, walk along the river Cam / Fareham creek maybe, then have a pint or meal here
