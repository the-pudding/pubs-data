E TA left this review about Lamb & Lion

A 16th Century alehouse which has been substantially refurbished in recent years. There is a car park and a large, well-kept garden with benches and tables. There is also a roof terrace on a single story extension, again with tables and chairs. Entry to two function rooms called 'Cider Warehouse' and 'Port and Cheese Room' is from both pub and garden. The inside has been opened out to form a single open-plan bar with flag floors and low beams. The staff are welcoming and the locals friendly. Food looked good but we didn't eat on this occasion. Three ales on, Tribute and Proper Job are the staples plus a guest. The beer was in very good condition, well worth stopping for, and I'd like to return soon to try the food.

Brew Guru left this review about Lamb & Lion

Indeed reopened in 2014 with a massive refurbishment. The interior has been extended out the back with a large dining area and the main bar opened up and spruced up. There are also spartan furnished rooms upstairs if downstairs is full. The pub is managed by the Woods Cider people so expect to see one of their ciders on the bar. There is also a cider barn accessible via the courtyard out the back. There is a large car park and open lawned area with outside seating. Beers were from St Austall with Proper job and Tribute plus a beer I can't recall from Lancashire as a guest ale. Pretty decent country dining pub now, worth a visit.
