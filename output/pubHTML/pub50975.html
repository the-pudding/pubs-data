David Ross left this review about The Crown Hotel

From outside, this looks like a quaint, traditional pub. Inside, however, it has been modernized and is now a typical 21st century lounge/diner with more customers eating than drinking. It was on the expensive side, with Guinness selling at £3.50 a pint. Tennent's lager, Belhaven Best and Strongbow cider were also on draught.

Vanessa Shoard left this review about Crown Hotel

We visited The Crown a month ago on our return from touring Scotland in our motorhome. The barman made us very welcome during the evening and we enjoyed a comfortable and relaxing evening there. We spent the night in the town in our motorhome and woke the next morning to find a pint of milk and newspaper on our door - it could only have been left by the barman, it was a very thoughful gesture and was most appreciated. He puts faith back in human nature and kindness - nowadays a very rare occurrence. We would like to say The Crown has a very cherished member of staff there!
