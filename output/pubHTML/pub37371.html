Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Lord Nelson

Traditional corner local retaining a two-bar layout, with an exposed timber ceiling in the main bar (aka the Admiral's Cabin). Pool table and a bit more seating in the other bar. Imaginative use of chalkboards; otherwise, relatively plain furniture and decor. Doom Bar and Byatt's Brewhouse Best (£3.10) from two of five handpumps on the two counters.

Soup Dragon left this review about Lord Nelson Inn

A cream rendered corner pub with brown paintwork and with writing and painting on the exterior like an old Victorian pub (or a modern Bathams one!)

The interior has two rooms; one in white with a pool table in and the main room, which is in wood panel and decked out like a ship of the line (forgive the pun) - indeed, fake barrels are used as a part of the bar itself. As you can imagine, Nelson and Trafalgar cover the walls with lots of other ephemera, like the odd cannon, dotted about. The service was fine, the clientele small, but mixed and they did do food though we never got to it as it was before 6pm!

Beer; tap stuff and a decent Everards Beacon

Despite a limited real ale choice, the beer was good and the place is just fun to visit if you like that sort of thing - i do, so i would go back
