Nick Davies left this review about Peacock

A pleasant enough village pub in a chocolate box village somewhat off the beaten track. It's had a rather unfortunate refurbishment which has left the floor behind the bar about a foot higher than the punters' side: when you place your order you feel like five-year-old kid asking a grown-up for more.

Strong gastro pretentions, complete with crematorium chairs, and all but a couple of tables laid up for eating on a midweek afternoon. Marston's related beers, Doombar, Bombardier, we opted for rather tired Wainwright. Everyone else present was on lager, I should know better.

Nice enough spot for the well-heeled (but not well-heeled enough to retire to the Cotswolds proper) final-salary populace to enjoy their pan-fried (what else do you fry it in?) duck breast.

This one doesn't tick enough boxes (like nice beer) for a return visit and ticks enough wrong boxes (like all the tables laid up) too. Though it is open all day, which gets it an extra point.

Not to be confused with the Peacock in nearby Tysoe.

On 22nd February 2019 - rating: 6
