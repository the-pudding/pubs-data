Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Duke of Gloucester

Modern JW Lees pub / restaurant, apparently named after an experimental steam locomotive with an innovative valve system developed locally (but ultimately doomed to failure as diesel traction soon superseded it). Anyway, the place has a sensible mix of seating / dining areas with a more-imaginative-than-most variety of furniture and decor in the different spaces in the main ('Station') part of the building. Also has a smaller and plainer 'Waiting Room' public bar with a pool table. The brewery's Bitter, MPA and seasonal Dirty Dozen were available from the various handpumps in each bar.
