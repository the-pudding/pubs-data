Soup Dragon left this review about The Golden Ball

This is a pleasant Georgian/Victorian build with front patio and rear beer garden. The interior has multitude of areas, two more bar-ish and three more for dining. All were generally white and beam, with nice fireplaces, some bench seating and a few photos on the walls. Due to the heat, I didn't stay inside to note much. TV wasn't on and there was no music. Service was fine and everyone was in the beer garden - mainly locals I think. Usual tap stuff with Hobson's Twisted Spire, Town Crier and a Woodfordes Volt IPA on pull. The Spire was fine. A nice pub, definitely one to return to.

Old Boots left this review about The Golden Ball

Billing itself as Ironbridge's oldest pub it is not a good pub to walk to from the bottom of the gorge. The hill is steep and may bring on a raging thirst or a heart attack, the walk however steep is worthwhile for the quality of the beer. It has a good reputation as a superior food pub, almost gastro but not quite due to the public bar. A fine choice of 5 very well kept micro and regional brewers beers plus a selection of Belgian bottles. The furniture all matches which isn't always a good sign, but it's ok here. It seemed less busy than on my last visit about four years ago but it has not diminished in quality.
