Reviews of Cafe Metro (Average Rating: 7 of 10) Add Review see review guidelines

Graham Coombs left this review about Cafe Metro

A café bar with a cosy armchair bit at the front, and a plainer room behind. The main feature of interest is a bank of four handpumps on the small counter serving interesting and well-kept ales. Visiting early evening, it was populated by regular locals popping in for a pint or two and a chat, with a good community feel. A bit of a shame that they have to convert a shop to replace the numerous pubs that have closed at this end of town but there you go. Note that opening hours vary during the week.

Soup Dragon left this review about Cafe Metro

A tidy looking terraced cafe-bar, although I have never been here during the day, so I can only comment on it as a micropub. The interior is a single room and is a slight dog-leg, with the bar at the front. It is in brown, white an green, with mixed photos and prints. TV wasn't on and the music is at a decent level. The service is friendly and the place is usually busy with a more mature clientele. Always has 4 changing real ales on: Butty Bach (very good), Kelham Island Shooting Star, Abbeydale Absolution (very good) and Purple Moose Elderflower. A really great place, and one I always do in Bilston
