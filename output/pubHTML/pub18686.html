Old Boots left this review about The York

A traditional single room pub, traditionally furnished and close to the station. The counter is at the back and dispenses beers from the Marstons stable, one from Ringwood and a well kept one from Wychwood on this occasion, the two other pumps had nothing on. There's an alcove at the front but it's lined with mirrors which was a bit off-putting for an ugly bloke like me, otherwise a comfortable pub if a little basic and masculine. The lights, speakers and mirror ball suggests they have discos and/or karaoke on a regular basis. Pool and darts are also played here as well as being equipped with the usual screen and machines.

ROB Camra left this review about The York Hotel

Small one roomed pub with 3 distinct areas. There's a pool room to the left as you enter and a seating area to the far right. The main bar area is mainly standing and there's also a bandit and a quiz machine. The friendly barstaff served me quickly and efficiently and even shouted goodbye when I left. 4 handpumps on the bar, but only one on. This was Wychwood Cherry Picker and was not very good. At the time I thought it may have been the beer as I'm not a great fan of cherry beers but I've had the beer since in a GBG listed pub and it was much better. It's a decent little pub to call in for one after an hour on the train as I did.

On 22nd July 2011 - rating: 6
