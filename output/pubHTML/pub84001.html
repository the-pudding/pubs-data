Real Ale Ray left this review about Little Ale House

As Just a Quick Pint describes a small and compact micro pub, which was converted from an estate agents office. The landlord was welcoming and chatty, along with the locals, who soon included us in their conversation. The main features to spot in here was the collection of toy mice around the place and I especially liked the one positioned in a hole made in the front of the bar. The three bar stools along the front of the bar where upholstered using denim jeans. Four ales on our visit, Farmers Blonde on handpump on the bar. Three on gravity, Bradfield Farmers Stout, Oakham Inferno and Hopback Winter Lightning. The pub also had a good selection of ciders.

On 9th February 2019 - rating: 8

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Little Ale House

Tiny micro-pub, almost a nano-pub, in a converted shop near the top end of the High Street. Just a couple of tables and a few stools by the compact counter, but all nicely done. Adnams Lighthouse on handpump, three more real ales on gravity - Potbelly Piggin' Proud (£3.20), Little Red Riding Hood from Castle Rock and Phipps' Cascadia IPA - and a Guinness font. Had a chat with the friendly landlord, and would definitely recommend.

On 12th February 2017 - rating: 8
