E TA left this review about Harvester The Griffin

A fairly new but nonetheless battered Harvester adjacent to a Holiday Inn. There is a substantially large car park serving both businesses, but little by way of garden. Inside, the single large room is demarcated into a tiny bar area and a much larger dining room with tables packed in to varying degrees. The staff were polite, the muzak truly awful and the food unappealing. The décor is better than many Harvesters – exposed brick walls, wooden floors and a couple of open fireplaces. Two ales on draft, Doombar and Atlantic, both over-chilled but in reasonable condition. It's functional, and it is convenient for a break from the motorway, but it's not somewhere I'd go deliberately for a relaxing evening.

On 24th November 2017 - rating: 5
