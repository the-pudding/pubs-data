Aqualung . left this review about Wheatsheaf

This is essentially a country pub on the outskirts of Crowborough. Having said that it really isn't that far from Crowborough Station, probably a five minute walk for anyone that isn't a shambolic wreck like myself. If you turn left out of the station and follow the road through the industrial estate there is a short footpath at the end that leads to the pub.
It's a lovely weather boarded old place that I suspect was originally owned by the Brighton branch of Watney's. Inside there is a central bar with three areas off it. At the side of the rear area is a small room with some speakers stashed in a corner. The front area is carpeted and the rear area has a brick floor while the rest has wooden floorboards. I assume the reason for the brick floor is that there is a massive open fireplace at the end. There was a collection of non-Harvey's pump clips on display which I thought was rather odd as there were no guest ales. This pub also has an outside Gent's which admittedly is only a few yards from the pub door but still counts as far as I'm concerned. Sadly there were no leaves that needed sweeping away, probably because it was early summer.
It became a Harvey's pub in 1998. There were four Harvey's beers on, Best, Mild, IPA and Copperwheat. I had a couple of pints of Copperwheat (£3.80), the first of which was rather tired and hadn't been pulled through and the second which was better but not as good as the one I had at their East Grinstead outlet a fortnight earlier and it was more expwensive.
It's a lovely old pub but sadly the beer quality means it only gets an 8 rather than a 9. It's GBG 2015 listed.

On 27th June 2015 - rating: 8
