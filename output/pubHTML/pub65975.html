Alan Hurdle left this review about The Travellers Rest

Remote moorland pub on the A53 between Buxton and Leek. Once infamously listed as the pub with the most "draft" (meaning bulk) beers served, with over 50 keg fonts on a circular bar. On later visits 3 or 4 handpumped "Real" draught beers were available.
Being the 3rd highest pub in the UK, it lies in a high moorland area on the Cheshire/ Staffordshire/Derbyshire borders which boasts the 2nd, 3rd, 5th, 6th, 7th, and 8th highest inns in a relatively small area. (Tan Hill, 1st and Kirkstone Inn, 4th are the out of the area other two).
