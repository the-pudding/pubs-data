ROB Camra left this review about The Bar Stewards

Managed to call in on Sunday after our abortive attempt last time we were in Sheffield as they were closed after a robbery. A very basic room with a bare brick wall down one side and some pretty uncomfortable bench seating. There's a outdoor area in a yard which didn't appeal even though it was sunny. The bar back looks to be made of chipboard with 4 craft keg taps peering out from it. There are also 4 handpumps on the bar. Pretty quiet during our visit with only 4 other people dotted about. It's not that comfortable and there was a definite lack of chat that epitomises micropubs. Not really impressed, but we'll probably give it another go next time we're in Sheffield.

Malden man left this review about The Bar Stewards

Well it is still here so hopefully now a permanent fixture, a shop conversion micropub with unusually a central bar with space in front and to the rear where there are stacked tables and an opening with a garage door. A kitchen to the rear side with a single unisex toilet beyond. An old chimney breast and fireplace looks to have been loosely bricked up leaving a fully exposed brick wall, the inside face of the front door is covered in postcards and flyers. Handpumps here rather than stillage, on cask were Arbor Smac My Brew Up, Gun Brewery Scaramanga Extra Pale, Tiny Rebel Hank and Beer Ink Noire Stout. Several keg beers too with taps on a MDF backboard, a fridge has a selection of tins. There's a good chippy a few doors up.

On 4th March 2018 - rating: 7
