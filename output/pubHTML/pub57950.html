Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Inn At Farnborough

Very attractive pub, built like many other buildings in the picturesque village of Farnborough from the local Hornton stone. Retains a two room layout - one a comfortable bar and the other effectively a restaurant - plus the 'Kitchen Library' housed in a small extension to the rear. Semi-modern furniture and decor, but tastefully done. Sloping beer garden overlooking the main façade. Upmarket menu, but obviously plenty of people are willing to pay since the place was busy on a Thursday lunchtime. Two Purity beers on handpump: Pure UBU and Pure Gold. Gives the impression of being very well run, welcoming, 'dog and wellie friendly' and all-in-all a very pleasant place to stop.

On 28th June 2014 - rating: 8
