Graham Coombs left this review about The Mitre

A large bustling corner pub on a crossroads with the bar immersed in the centre. The front right is the large games area with several pool tables and darts, while there is a lounge area up a step to the left. Further back is mainly a dining area, with a small patio at the rear. Four handpumps supply GK IPA and Abbot with two guests, although sadly the latter were not ready at time of visit, being St Austell Proper Job and Flack Manor Black Jack. It has all the ingredients of a good pub but is a bit large and impersonal.

On 21st March 2019 - rating: 6

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Mitre

Greene King pub with several distinct zones within the opened-out single bar layout. To the right of the front door is a large games area with three pool tables and various machines. On the left-hand side there is a raised seating area with a few low sofas plus some modern seating at a more sensible height for those wishing to eat. Behind that, there are some high stools is an area which looks like it is used for music events, yet more seating near the bar and then a beer garden out back. Two of three handpumps were working when I visited, dispensing a reasonable pint of IPA and also OSH.

On 9th September 2010 - rating: 6
