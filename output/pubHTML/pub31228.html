Alex Conway left this review about The Bramley Apple

The Bramley apple is located a short walk from the Minster in the delightful village of Southwell. From the outside the pub looks very old and traditional with a nice pub sign and Springhead Brewery banner. Inside things are a little more modern and less traditional the front façade would suggest with hard stone flagged floors and more bright/modern colour scheme. The pub is one room with a few different sections with he seating areas to the front with two comfy couches on the right hand side with more chairs and tables at the back with a pool table round the corner. We sat on the couches but if these were busy this would not be the most comfortable place to sit with just hard wooden chairs on the hard floor. This pub was closed the last time I came to Southwell and I hear rumours that there has been a bit of turmoil with previous landlords to things have been a little rocky here as of late. It is still owned by the Springhead Brewery (A brewery I like rather a lot) but a friend informed me of her disappointment a few months earlier of the lack of ale which was concerning. Undeterred I visited only cold January afternoon only to find it closed but no worry as it opened at four midweek so we Just ventured to another pub and came back a little later. Shock horror, NO ALE! There were no hand pumps and not a drop to drink so we settled for a Guinness at £3.80. Coincidence? I think not, and the “our delivery is a day late” is a complete B*****ks to be honest. It was ok but not a patch on some of Southwells other gems and with no ale on takes the title of second worst pub in Southwell after the lousy Crown up the road. Id return if the beer line-up returned but left rather unimpressed on this visit,

On 15th February 2018 - rating: 6
