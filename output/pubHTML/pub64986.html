Bucking Fastard left this review about Ye Olde Swan

Located at a honey spot site on the banks of the Upper Thames,it will get mobbed in fine weather with families who want pub grub meals and so it proved on our visit.The garden was packed but the interior quiet,the Oxfordshire stone is exposed in much of the interior ,while beyond a dining area to the right is a charming small snug.The bar has an open fire ,but a suit of armour left a tacky feel.The etched glass and other signs for the Old Forge Brewery proved a misnomer,none of their beer was available .
The five handpumps only offered Otter Ale and Ramsbury Red Velvet.On ordering this red ale it soon became clear to be pure vinegar ,to which the dippy barmaid initially said that the Red Velvet was an acquired taste ! Encouraged to try it for herself ,she proved to be a spitter and did at least take the pints back and reverse the clip.Her suggestion of bottled Ramsbury Gold was taken up,but the glasses given out were seriously marked and dirty ,at which point the firm impression was left that this pub is poorly run,it's tacky and dirty and will be avoided at all costs in future.We got a cab for Faringdon.

On 22nd April 2018 - rating: 3

Komakino . left this review about Ye Olde Swan

No longer under GK's clutches. Now owned by the people in charge at The Radnor Arms in Coleshill and proferring their Old Forge beers.

September 2016
Popular with walkers and boaters, given the location, this GK house is stapled-shaped internally, with an eating area behind the bar: the latter of which faces you upon entry. Predictably, there's various boating and aquatic paraphernalia adorned on the walls, and the ale line-up consisted of Old Speckled Hen, Ale Fresco, Black Sheep Pathfinder and a house ale called Radcot Cruiser (either rebadged GK IPA or H&H). A (sparklered) half of the Pathfinder was £1.85 and was as soulless as the interior and as vague as the staff. Visit for the tick, but don't enter with any expectations.

On 7th June 2017 - rating: 3
