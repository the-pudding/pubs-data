Quinno _ left this review about The Angel

Recently refurbished and brews its own beer (Bicster brewery). Done inside to a good standard, it's modern but has some character and is light and bright. The main bar area has an old inglenook off to the left with bay window bench seating also. The rear area is undressed stone with another fireplace. The brewery is located in old stables to the rear along with a bottle shop and compacted pebble garden. Note the old water pump here. Six pumps with four on: Loddon, XT Animal, Purity UBU and Bicester Pale. Had a half of the latter which wasn't a particularly brilliant beer but was in decent nick. Scatty barman forgot my change. Very quiet on my Saturday afternoon visit - hope it succeeds though, I think it has only been in this guise for a short period.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Angel Inn

Old pub dating from 17th-century retaining a two-room layout and features such as the extended cantilever sign but refurbished inside in a semi-modern style. The main bar at the front is a compact affair with plenty of old beams and columns, and the slightly larger back bar houses a pool table. Doom Bar and 6X (£3.40) from the pair of handpumps.
