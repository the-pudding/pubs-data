Real Ale Ray left this review about Paget Arms

After a lengthy walk from the train station, it was good to see the Steamin Billy dog on the pub sign. We immediately went for the Steamin Billy Tipsy Fisherman, which hit the spot. We also tried a local microbrewery beer, Charnwood Salvation, which was also another good beer. They also had another Steamin Billy beer and two more guests.
The pub consists of two rooms served by a central bar. The left hand side was slightly bigger and was an L shaped room. This side had high table and chairs and further seating around the corner with a tiled fireplace. There was a lobby connecting the two rooms, which was wallpapered depicting landmark London Street names. The slightly smaller right hand side room had various sporting equipment dotted around the walls, old tennis rackets, a pair of skates, boxing clothes etc. The tables this side were made from old cast iron sewing machine stands, with scrubbed pine table tops.
