Real Ale Ray left this review about The West End House

A well kept pub, both inside and out, with a very impressive beer garden. The pub also has two Jack Russell's and a ginger cat, who wander around the place, no problem there. My gripe was the loud piped music being played on a Sunday afternoon visit and was very off-putting.
The interior of the pub is traditional in style with low ceilings, with beams, horse brasses and carpeted throughout.
Five handpumps on the bar, with four in use. The choices were all nationals, Robinson's Dizzy Blonde, Directors, Doombar and Adnams Southwold. We went for the Southwold, which was well kept and presented.

Bucking Fastard left this review about The West End House

Attractive,low ceiling local with four drinking rooms,three of which are served by the central bar.The interior is very traditional with a real fire in the main saloon.Five handpumps including London Pride,Spitfire,Adnams Bitter and Courage Directors all in good condition.Beer range varies and next up were Doom Bar,Black Sheep and Woodforde's Wherry but the ales served here do appear to come from a distributors list,hence a lot of national brewers ales and few from local micros.Musak also played too loud but these are minor criticisms of a decent pub.Very lively Jack Russell lives here !
