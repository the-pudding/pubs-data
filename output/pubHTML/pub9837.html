Steve C left this review about The Britannia

The Britannia is an L shaped pub that was busy with locals when I visited during Monday lunchtime. There is a TV that was showing Good Morning on ITV and there is a pool table situated in the rear of the pub. The bar is stocked with standard draught products and there are three hand pumps that were drawing Ruddles, Old Speckled Hen and Bombardier. There is an advertised beer garden out the back that I did not visit.

This is a rough and ready boozer that hasn’t changed for years and I would have no problem returning, but it certainly isn’t a place I would actively seek out.

E TA left this review about The Britannia

The Brittania is a traditional high street boozer, rooted in the 1960s, catering mostly for locals, a rare find these days. It's a single small bar, cramped and a little shabby. The outside wall is tiled, an original feature which ought to be preserved. The only irritation was the jukebox which was too loud. There were three pumps on the bar but only two ales on, Spitfire and Ruddles Best. Sadly, both were past their best but still drinkable. It's worth dropping in for a no-frills pint and it's reasonably convenient for the station if waiting for a train.

On 11th October 2010 - rating: 5
