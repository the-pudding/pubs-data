Will Larter left this review about The Big Bulls Head

No real ale, but this is a really nice pub with a quality wooden bar counter with hooks. A tradtional pub with leather seating which would be a real destination pub with the addition of one or two real ales. My drinking companions were getting stuck into the various food offerings, while I contented myself with a swift half of execrable Sharps Wolf Rock 4.5%. Did I mention that there's no real ale here?

Date of visit: 11th July 2018

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Big Bulls Head

Quite a surprise here, in a place I have always previously avoided, which turns out to be quite an attractive traditional two-bar pub. The main bar has been given a refurbishment in a unexpectedly light style which works quite well, but the substantial dark-wood bar-back remains. There is also a separate bar and dining room to the side, served through an oversized hatch. Furthermore, real ale - in the former of Sharp's Atlantic (£3.10) - turned out to be available too... Hardly great, but it's always good to have one's expectations confounded and exceeded in a positive way.

Al Bundy left this review about Big Bulls Head

The exterior was covered in scaffolding on my visit which made it looked closed. However it wasn't and inside I saw at least 2 rooms. I only went in the small bar so don't know what the other looked like. In the bar was a pool table to the left of the bar and next to that was a narky bloke. The bar had one handpump on that was reversed. It wasn't too bad a pub but it wasn't great. As long as the scraggy haired tit isn't in then the pub is just about acceptable.
