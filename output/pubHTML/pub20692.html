Real Ale Ray left this review about The Rutland & Derby Arms

UPDATE FEBRUARY 2014
This pub has changed profoundly since our last visit. We found the staff this time to be disinterested in any new visits to the pub. Our order for food was taken and they got it half right. Their stone baked pizzas they once served have gone, only to be replaced by some stodgy, tasteless flatbreads, which are overpriced.

March 2013
This pub really struck the big points for me. It's spotlessly clean unlike the the neighbouring pub on the corner beginning with C. Staff friendly and knowledgeable. I had one of the best homecooked pizza I've ever had. Four ales on, the Everards Tiger was fab. Looking forward to my next visit.

Sadly our visit was nowhere near as good as Rays. Yes the place is spotless, but it's a bit modern with loads of pine around. The barmaid was totally clueless. Steve of N21 left this review about The Rutland & Derby Arms

This old Victorian brick exterior hides what is an ultra modern, bright clean interior more aligned to a cocktail bar than a pub.
I could not work out if it work out if it had undergone a recently refurb, but it certainly looked like it, with comfortable big spongey sofa's, lots of abstract art on the walls and modern lighting, and a pristine wooden flooring that looked like it hadn't had a drop of drink spilt on it yet.
Lots of shiney lager taps but also a couple of hand pumps supporting GK Abbott and Yorkshire Terrier from the York brewery.
Now I'm sure this place is or will be very popular with the 20 - 30 somethings, but I prefer my pubs with a bit more character than an uber modern bar more in the style of an Ikea showroom.
And as excellent as our pints of Terrier were, I didn't feel comfortable and thought I should have been drinking a large pinot grigio or something with an umbrella and a sparkler.
Fortunately the Criterion is a short walk down the road and I was much more comfortable here and I'll leave The Rutland and Derby to you young trendy things…
