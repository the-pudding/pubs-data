Will Larter left this review about Horse & Jockey

A former "no real ale" pub that is now the brewery tap for Stancill Brewery. There are ten hand pumps on the bar, each one of them selling a different Stancill beer. At £2 a pint! In oversized glasses! It was a little hard to tell what the pub was like, because it was absolutely heaving - this was a Friday evening in December, so hardly surprising. I'll come back another time for a more leisurely visit.

(The opening times quoted on the What Pub site are not accurate - I turned up here at just after 12 and there was no sign of life. Glad I came back later, though.)

Date of visit: 9th December 2016

The Horse and Jockey is a decent looking pub that is next to a road junction.
Once inside this pub has been opened out and is now one largish room that seemed a bit bare.
There was no real ale on here the keg beers were Stones bitter and Tetley biter.
