Will Larter left this review about The Hunters Arms

A handsome brick building, dated 1879. I was doing a cycling trip around here and timed my arrival just right, approaching the front door just as the young woman on duty was opening it. The inside has been opened up to some extent, with pillars replacing walls, but it's still a pub rather than a drinking barn. To confirm this, there's a small fireplace in the exposed brick chimney breast to the left of the bar. The bar itself is of stained wood panelling, and has hooks and stools, as well as six hand pumps. (See photos.) I went for the Bass, which was in good condition. There is a separate bar for ciders. I liked it here and if I lived in this part of Derbyshire I'm sure I would become a regular visitor.

Date of visit: 1st June 2017
