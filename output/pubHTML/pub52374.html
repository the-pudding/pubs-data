Aqualung . left this review about The Blue Boar (JD Wetherspoon)

Despite some negative previous reviews I quite liked this Spoons. It is a shop conversion and inside extends back a long way. On the bar were 10 pumps selling Wadworth Fat Head's Rocket Man, Adnam's Mayday, Pedigree, the two usual GK beers, Growler Border Bitter, Shalford Stoneley Bitter, Brentwood Best and two ciders. I went for the Stoneley Bitter (£2.20), a completely new brewery for me and it was in very good nick. I also indulged in the Ddraig Flamllyd Curry Club curry which was also excellent and included a pint of the Mayday.
This was the only one of the five Essex Spoons visited giving a serious go at a Locale beer choice and was my favourite of them.
It is GBG 2014 listed.

lea kor left this review about The Blue Boar

Fantastic local beer festival in Feb, meet the brewer evening with brentwood brewery and it was nice to see so many camera members there. The pub put on a free buffet and was selling raffle tickets to win a day at brenwood brewery, the proceeds were going to the charity CLIC. Overall a fantastic evening, can see why they are in the 'Good Beer Guide'

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Blue Boar (JD Wetherspoon)

Run-of-the-mill 'spoons, and one with the staff seemingly more interested in doing this and that behind the bar than actually serving customers. However, my pint of Lumberjack from the local Brentwood Brewing Company (at 5.2%, designed "for the man who can handle his chopper!", apparently, according to the pump clip) was fine.
