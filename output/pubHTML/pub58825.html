Graham Coombs left this review about Smiths of Bourne

An imaginative conversion of a multi-level shop, extending across several floors with various stairways and corridors, so finding a quiet corner is not difficult. The whole place is decorated with miscellaneous signs, objects and artefacts. To the side is a long tabled patio, leading to a lawned area at the back. 6 handpumps serves a changing range of beers from local brewers and further afield and those sampled were in excellent condition. Recommended - the sort of pub you can lose a day in.

On 9th October 2019 - rating: 8

Alan Winfield left this review about Smiths of Bourne

This is a newish pub that has been converted from other use.
Once inside the bar was to the right and the front area had bare boards and large chunky wooden tables there was another drinking area which extended to the rear of the pub and there was also a room upstairs which was for familys with children.
There was a very good choice of beers on the bar with six real ales on i had a drink of Ufford Ales White Hart and this was a very nice drink the other beers were Ufford Ales Wardog, Black Sheep Golden Sheep, Black Country Bradly's Finest Golden, Hobgoblin and a beer from Langton brewery.
The pub was very busy and had a really nice atmosphere,we sat at the front of the pub looking out of the window and watching the world go by.
This was a really nice pub and it is well wotrh a visit.

On 17th July 2011 - rating: 9
