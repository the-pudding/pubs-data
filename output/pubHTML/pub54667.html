Reviews of The Boot and Shoe (Average Rating: 5 of 10) Add Review see review guidelines

Aqualung . left this review about The Boot and Shoe

I turned up here on a January Friday to find it quiet. It's an old pub that has low ceilings and a central bar serving various small areas or rooms off it. All the TVs were turned off on my visit but there was a music track playing. The young student barmaid was friendly enough and the one punter while I was there was an old bloke who had moved to Nantwich after losing his wife.
There were four hand pumps which had Landlord, Doom Bore and Fuggle Bunny Festive Fuggle & Cotton Tail. I went for the Festive Fuggle which I had to send back but the Cotton Tail (£3.60) which replaced it was fine. I found this place to be OK and thought at first it might be expensive but it's Nantwich itself that is expensive.

Real Ale Ray left this review about The Boot and Shoe

Four ales on handpump, Pride, Bombardier, Tetleys Original and Tetleys Gold. I decided on the London Pride and have to say it was excellent, but very overpriced. The barmaid hadn't a clue when trying to fill my pint glass, she kept pulling the beer into the glass, trying to get rid of the lively head. I saw a least a full pint wasted into the glass tray. Maybe that's why they charge so much.
The pub is traditional in style with low ceilings and different seating areas. The downside to this pub is the amount of gaming machines scattered throughout the place. The other irritation was the pretentious customers it attracts, enough to make you vomit, especially the blokes, who all want to be best pals with the female staff. You see a lot of phoney hugs and kisses on each cheek here. Would I return? Err! no chance.

On 30th April 2014 - rating: 2
