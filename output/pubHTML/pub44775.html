Aqualung . left this review about Blue Bell

As this is a Punch Taverns pub my expectations were low. Inside it's a single roughly square room which was obviously once two bars but all that's left now is a half partition. I turned up here on a Sunday afternoon and it was quiet apart from an acoustic folkish sort of group playing on the stage to the right. Their instruments sounded good but I doubt that vocally they would even make it to the comedy part of the X Factor. I was tempted to ask the barman if I should call for an ambulance.
The bar has four hand pumps which had a cider and unusually three fixed beer options. These were Weetwood Cheshire Cat, Titanic Plum Porter and Conwy Welsh Pride. I went for the Titanic (£3.70) which was in excellent condition.
I enjoyed this one much more than I had anticipated.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Blue Bell

Reasonable town pub with a remodelled interior and refurbished furniture / decor in several seating areas around the central bar. Also has a pool table to one side. However, if the weather permits, the rear split-level decking beer garden is probably the best spot. A bank of four handpumps on the bar counter offer the local Conwy Welsh Pride (£3.20) plus Doom Bar, Hobgoblin and a cider.
