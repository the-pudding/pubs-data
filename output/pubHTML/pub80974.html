Will Larter left this review about The Duke of York

I went in here soon after visiting the Eagle and Child, the new Leeds pub in High Petergate. As with all the Leeds Brewery pubs I've been in, this building too has been painstakingly, almost faultlessly adapted, if seen from an aesthetic point of view, but as a pub it is totally lacking in warmth and ambience. As with the new pub, there are 12 hand pumps, with Leeds's core range duplicated on a second set of four, though here the Midnight Bell gives way to a cider on one set. There are four guest beers. I don't rate the Leeds pubs much as pubs - perhaps they'll improve with age (or perhaps I'll become less fastidious, who knows).

Alan Winfield left this review about The Duke of York

The Duke of York is a new pub which is situated in the middle of the shopping area,so very handy for a drink while having a look round the shops.
Once inside i had a drink in the ground level room,i did'nt take a look upstairs,the ground level room is split into two halfs,the left side has normal tables and chairs,i think the right side had a few settees,but the pub was so busy on my Saturday afternoon visit to get a proper look round.
The pub is a Leeds brewer tied house,there was a very good choice of real ales on the bar,i had a drink of Leeds Pale which went down well,the other beers i noted were Yorkshire Gold,Best,Imperial and Midnight Bell all from Leeds Brewery,i did'nt note any guest beers.
I would have prefered to have done this pub when less busy,but i still quite liked the pub and will no doubt go in again when in York.

Pub visited 23/5/2015
