Alan Winfield left this review about Colpitts Hotel

The Colpitts Hotel is situated on the apex of two roads.
Once inside the main room is oblong shaped,there is also a smaller room in the front corner of the pub which has a pool table and darts board in it,there is one more room which is off the main room,the seating is bench type and small stools.
The pub is a Samuel Smiths tied house,there was one real ale on the bar which was Samuel Smiths Old Brewery bitter which was a very nice drink and very cheap at 90p for a half.
The pub was very busy on my Saturday afternoon visit.
I quite liked this pub.

Quinno _ left this review about Colpitts Hotel

Corner junction Sam Smiths pub which is pretty unspoilt inside. A public bar area in the building’s apex, with a pool room and comfy lounge side room (quite a bit of character) elsewhere. Main bar seems to be where the action is, fairly spartan in décor but warm and welcoming with a collection of friendly barflys plus a matriarch landlady. Selection of board games above the bar. Quiz night Tuesdays. Usual Sams drinks selection with OBB available and in pretty good shape. Worth popping in if you're doing the excellent Elm Tree over the way, a proper traditional town boozer.

Malden man left this review about Colpitts Hotel

A wedge shaped, albeit with a rounded end, stone built pub a short walk out of town and close to the imposing railway viaduct. Timeless feel inside, the main bar had a clutch of regulars gathered around the bar, chatting and ribbing one another, a couple played dominoes, there were games on a shelf to choose from. As well as the main room there is an intimate snug and a back room with a dartboard and pool table. Wooden flooring, small round tables, leather benches and stools. There are old railway notices and I spotted a nice Bell's Whisky mirror.
It's a Sam Smith's house so the beer choice on the real side is limited to OBB, which is a solid option if not likely to set the pulse racing and as always exceptional value, my half was 90p! I've paid more in a pub for crisps.
It is a must visit pub maybe if not for the ale, but certainly to experience a proper old fashioned pub that does what pubs are all about.
