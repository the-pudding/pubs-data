Soup Dragon left this review about Blue Peter Inn

A harbourside end terraced freehaouse pub and eatery with a couple of patio areas on two levels and is a GBG regular. It is a white painted stone building with blue paintwork. The main front porched entrance is actually blocked and access is obtained up some steps to the side.

The interior is spread over a couple of floors. The main bar room is on the ground floor, with an overspill and more foody area upstairs. The main bar room is an L-shaped room around the serving bar that faces what was the main entrance. The decor is exposed stone and French mustard painted stone walls, with a wood and beam ceiling. The place was adorned with local pictures, books and other fun ephemera. The small upstairs room has coffee walls, a white and beam ceiling and a red carpet floor. It has tables for dining and similar pictures on the walls as in the bar. They do food in this pub, but i didn't eat so can't comment, but what i saw seemed fine. There was no TV that i saw and the background music was general stuff at a decent level - but they do have live music nights - sadly which i missed. The service was functional, likely as it was rammed with what seemed like a mix of both drinkers and diners, all ages, holidaymakers and clearly locals.

Beer; usual tap stuff with St Austell Trelawny, Skinner's Betty Stoggs, Bays Gold and Bays Topsail on handpull, all were very good.

This is a great pub. It is a victim of its own success in the fact that it is quite small and was rammed on all three occasions that i visited (different times and different days), so i felt quite cramped. This means i can't stay too long as i am so miserable, i can't handle busy places too long. Visit it - all i can say really.
