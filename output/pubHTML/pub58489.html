Old Boots left this review about The Milbank Arms

As a small village pub renowned for its food offering it's naturally laid out for dining in the bar and in a rear dining room. The small counter against a side wall has two pulls with Black Sheep Bitter and Theakstons best bitter, decently kept but perishingly cold. There are low ceilings with black beams and there's board floors even a bit of white slightly scruffy tongue & groove. The red painted walls have a profusion of prints, pictures, and posters. There's also a few brasses and plates completing the cozy village pub style.
