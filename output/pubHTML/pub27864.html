Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Falcon

Comfortable village pub, housed in a fine old building with the entrance up a short flight of steps. Comprises three rooms, a small traditional bar inside the main door, a modernised dining area (with nice fireplace) to one side and another room back down a few steps to the rear (the latter spaces two having much lighter decor). Also offers B&B rooms. Four handpumps, offering TT Landlord, Brakspear, Sharp's Own and Bombardier (£3.40). You would never imagine that you are just a couple of miles from Uxbridge town centre, and well worth seeking out.

Danny O'Revey left this review about The Falcon

Nice old inn in pretty village, partly modernised inside with some light wood panels, and pine tables that slightly spoil the olde worlde type place it should be.

3 separate areas, one down some steps is more modern and foody. Rustic old tables in main area.

Friendly, and overall a nice place.

4 real, but uninspiring ales - Doom bar, Bombadier, Landlord, Breakespear

Quinno _ left this review about The Falcon

Denham’s Good Beer Guide entry. Large 16th century building though space inside the roomed interior is a loss less than you’d think, the bar area is especially tight. A large chunk of the place is given over to diners and thus the bar area gets pretty rammed when busy (as my trip was) and I ended up standing. Four ales on the go, a fairly safe selection (Bombardier, Brakspear, TT Landlord). My Landlord was fine. Perhaps this is one to do when quieter, I couldn’t settle and felt a bit out of place being merely a drop-in drinker.
