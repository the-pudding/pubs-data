Al Bundy left this review about Tilted Wig

Trendy arsed winebar-like place. Dark inside and modernish. The L-shaped bar has 6 handpulls but 3 were off and one was for cider. Not cheap and as a result it was empty, even on Saturday market day. Probably best to avoid.

Aqualung . left this review about Tilted Wig

This is set on Warwick's Market Place almost next door to the Wetherspoon. It was busy enough inside to force me to go to the seats on the pavement area outside. Inside it's Olde Worlde and food orientated which was successfully attracting the tourists.
I counted six hand pumps which had two unused and the rather dull choice of Sharp's Sea Fury & Doom Bore, Slaughterhouse Saddleback and Purity UBU. I went for the UBU which was in good nick but expensive at £3.90.
I didn't take to this place as it was touristy, too food orientated, expensive and had a very dull beer choice. I was quite relieved to make the short journey to the JDW. It's GBG 2018 listed.

Quinno _ left this review about Tilted Wig

Mid-range gastropub with a middle class crowd. Opened out and blandized inside, though the fake slab flooring is ok. Staple-shaped with olive green, mauve and cream shades. Some painted wood panelling and - oddly - gold painted stone walls. The large Inglenook fireplace now houses a wood burning stove. Arched, square hatched windows give a nice view over the pretty piazza which also has a fair amount of al fresco seating. Beer is the reason to pop in as there’s six pumps. Five ales on my visit; Doom Bar, GK Old Golden Hen, Slaughterhouse Saddleback, Jennings Snecklifter and Purity UBU (good form) with Old Rosie as the cider. Decent set of bottles in the fridge too. Plenty of staff but a pretty workshy bunch, too busy yapping amongst themselves. Decent enough place as part of a crawl. 6.5
