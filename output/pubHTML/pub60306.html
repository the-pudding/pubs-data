Will Larter left this review about The Mission

This conversion of an old seaman's mission and chapel could have been so much better. The building looks fantastic on approaching, but once inside there's such a horrible mish-mash of deliberately naff brickwork, with reclaimed bricks laid any old how and interspersed with old gin bottles and even crushed lager cans, that it almost made me feel sick to look at it. The bar takes up a lot of room along the back wall, but with only Old Mill ordinary bitter available on the handful of hand pumps, there was little of interest for me there either. In fact, the beer I was served was in such poor condition that it was on the borderline of being complainable.

Date of visit: 18th March 2016

ROB Camra left this review about The Mission

Very large impressive pub converted from a seamans mission. It still has the chapel, complete with stained glass windows, as one of the rooms. There are also 2 raised areas to the left of the entrance as you enter. There's a separate entrance to the side for very busy times. There's loads of seating in most of the areas and a space for vertical drinking in front of the bar. We called in on a Monday night when it acts as a pre club venue for the Sugar Mill across the road. It was very quiet at 9:30, but I'll bet it gets rather busier later on. A massive bar with 3 handpumps, but just the one solitary cask ale Old Mill Bitter, which was in decent nick. It's worth calling in for a look at the impressive interior alone, although I'm guessing Friday night at 11 might not be the time to try it.
