Tim Lawson left this review about Bridge Inn

First and foremost I have to point out that I am the landlord of the bridge at Stanford bridge, and have been for the last seven years, so this review might be just a tad biased, but as there wasn't a review at all I thought I would at least try and purvey what the pub is about! At the bridge we pride ourselves in being a proper community country pub, serving a good selection of cask ales and traditional ciders, as well as serving wholesome home cooked food in a traditional pub atmosphere. Situated by the river Teme in the beautiful Teme valley, our pub is a great starting point for walking or just sitting and relaxing in the beer garden.
We have been in the Camra good beer guide for the last 5 years, and run beer festivals over the Easter beer weekend and October. We also run many other events, notably the charity duck race on the Sunday of the spring bank holiday weekend and a promises auction in November. The bridge is a family run pub, and although popular with the locals it is also incredibly friendly and welcoming to all comers. Children, dogs and muddy boots welcome!
We have six cask ales at all times, including hob sons mild, hob sons twisted spire, wye valley hpa, otter bright and two guest pumps, one of which is always a stout in winter and enville ginger beer in summer. We also sell westons or thatchers cider and perry, and this year will be selling our own cider too!
Basically, the bridge is a proper pub in a mad, mad world..... But don't take my word for it, come and see for yourself!

On 4th March 2013 - rating: 10
