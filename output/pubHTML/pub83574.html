ROB Camra left this review about Wapentake

Agree with JAQPTIO, this is definitely a café bar. It was very busy on our Sunday afternoon visit with rugby being shown downstairs and football in the upstairs area. Due to the footy I couldn't really get a look at the upstairs. Friendly enough downstairs with 3 beers available. My pint was in good nick, but wasn't really to my taste. I wouldn't mind calling in again when it's not as busy.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Wapentake

Very much a café bar, with cakes on the counter as well as four handpumps. Eclectic mix of furniture and decor in the slightly split-level ground-floor bar and smaller upstairs seating area. Beer-wise, I found Acorn Barnsley Bitter, Sunbeam Break of Day (£3.40), Kirkstall Dissolution IPA and Roosters London Thunder. Quite good in its own way, but I did have a major problem with inconsiderate customers leaving the back door open with no thought for the rest of us on a windy winter day...

Real Ale Ray left this review about Wapentake

An interesting medium sized bar with a patio garden at the rear. The small and compact bar area was situated on the right of entrance, which was elaborately finished in a dark stained timber. Four handpumps with two in use and as luck would have it, Roosters Yankee and Kirkstall Three Swords, so I went for a pint of each and one was as good as the other.
