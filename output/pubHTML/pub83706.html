Komakino . left this review about The Blue Ball

Another pub in the sticks and a self-proclaimed freehouse, this offered Young's Bitter, Adnam's Ghost Ship, Sharp's Doom Bar Amber and Fuller's London Pride which displays a bit of a lack of invention. This has an L-shaped interior with an entrance-facing brick-built bar, a converted inglenook fireplace to the left and a brick-built cousin on the opposite wall. The ales are hidden behind a brick column on the right-angle of the L and a small dining area is located out back. There's a covered patio to the right of the building and a generous beer garden out back along with a couple of pub benches on a green strip the other side of the road. There's a nice pub ambience here only let down by the pedestrian ale range and although I'm not sure whether they are meant to be tongue-in-cheek or not, but the 'dogs playing pool' prints in the Gents are something I haven't seen in a pub for quite a while.

On 23rd May 2017 - rating: 5
