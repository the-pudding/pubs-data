Real Ale Ray left this review about Tankerville Arms

So glad I planned our route so well, as I wouldn't want to miss this one out. Once through the door you enter the main bar and lounge. The room was low timber beams, with the bar facing you as you enter, which had an aged pine panelled front and a mahogany counter. The pub was tastefully decorated in keeping with its period. The owner's wife is an antique dealer, so the interior is a collection of sought after paintings and various ornaments. There was also a various collection of tables and chairs, throughout the pub leading into the dining area at the far end. The young barmaid on our visit was friendly and chatty. There were three hand pumps, two from Hadrian and Border and a Tyneside Blonde, which was excellent. There was a wood burning stove either end of the main lounge, so I can imagine this to be a cosy pub in winter.

Jack Nicholson left this review about Tankerville Arms

A charming country inn dating from 1851, nestling in the picturesque Northumbrian village of Eglingham. On our visit we had a pint of Wylam ‘Spring Thing’ and a very enjoyable meal. It has accommodation of three en-suite bedrooms and is doggie friendly. The pub is listed in the CAMRA Good Beer Guide 2011.
