Soup Dragon left this review about The Swan With Two Necks

A multi-phased, detached, 18th century country pub, in white pained brick, with beer garden.

The interior had a couple of rooms that i saw. There is a dining room, laid out with tables of course, which is in the white artex look, with a nice brick fireplace range and there are plates and jugs for decoration. The open plan bar area is in white and wood panel, with support and ceiling beams. There is a tiled floor, a couple of brick fire ranges, one being a central feature and some photos on the walls. There was a TV, which wasn't on at our visit. The service was fine and the clientele seemigly friendly locals that gave an all over happy atmosphere.

Beer; tap stuff, with three handpulls - sadly i missed the Bathams Bitter that is on on Fridays! There was Church End's What the Fox Had, Morgan Ales's Barmaids Tipple and Tollgate's TGL - all were decent.

We didn't eat, so cant comment on that, but the pub is well worth a visit - all round nice experience.
