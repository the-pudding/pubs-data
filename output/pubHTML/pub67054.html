Komakino . left this review about The Old New Inn

As below, this pub, despite having the trappings of one, is merely a bar for the Model Village next door. This has two bars, one in the room you enter into from the front entrance and another in a larger, second room, accessed via a doorway to the front bar's left which leads onto a large beer garden. The front bar is predominantly wooded floor save for a tiled bar apron and a large rug covered by standard tables.and chairs. An inter-war style stone fireplace accomplices the left-hand wall with a flat screen TV hanging above playing 90s music hits videos. The larger room out back is L-shaped with a larger wooded bar counter and has a University common room feel. There's more seating here along with a pool table and fussball table. Two hand pumps but only one ale on - Hook Norton Hooky (£1.85 for a half). Nothing special, but at least it does operate as a pub, albeit for half the year.
