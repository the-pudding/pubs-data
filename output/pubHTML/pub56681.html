Reviews of The Little Angel (Average Rating: 5 of 10) Add Review see review guidelines

Komakino . left this review about The Little Angel

Re-opened a little ahead of schedule in the February of 2019 after a refurb which reinforced its gastro leaning. A J-shaped bar faces, with a small pubby snug to the left. There's a table in the window to the right and some raised tables and seats in front of a semi-partioned bit to the right. Seating dead ahead, with a hidden room behind the bar and a new conservatory bit added to the far right which looked rather elegant. Be-aproned waiting staff and four pumps to choose from - Brakspear Bitter x2, Oxford Gold and Special. To my disappointment, the Special was off as was one of the Bitters, so a pint of OK-ish Oxford Gold it was for £4.20. I had to suggest the barman reverse the two "off" clips, which he did to a little cajoling - one thinks the beer husbandry isn't one of the main priorities here.

On 4th March 2019 - rating: 5

February 2013
A nice old building on the outside, with the inside having been done out in a very 'modern ambient' feel. Two ales on (Brakspear Bitter and Marstons list guest) which are usually OK without being overly inspiring. Large rear conservatory is laid out for food - friends have eaten here and enjoyed it.
