Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Miners Arms

Popular community pub on the main road, partly opened-out inside to leave a 'L'-shaped bar with a dartboard and pool table in what would once have been separate rear rooms. Simply furnished and plainly decorated, but all tidy enough. No real ale, unfortunately, so went for the Worthington's (£2.80) from the limited keg range.

Alan Winfield left this review about Miners Arms

The Miners Arms is a small pub that is on the main road through Brereton.
Once inside there is a small single L shaped room with the bar facing,there is bench seating to the front and small stools,there are normal tables and chairs to the rear right,there is also a darts board to the rear right.
There is one real ale on the bar which was GK IPA,this was a decent enough drink.
The pub was packed on our Saturday afternoon visit,background music was playing.
This is a nice and lively pub to have a drink in.

Soup Dragon left this review about Miners Arms

A neat looking little undressed brick cottage styled pub, with render on the side. There are front and back patio areas.

The interior was an open plan L-shaped room, around the bar. The decor was red, white and mustard coloured walls, with some red painted wood panel and wood floor. There were a couple of boxing cartoons on the walls, other than that, it was a bit bare, except for the dart board and the TV, with the footy on. The service was fine, the clientele a good mix, but some could be loud!

Beer; all tap stuff, no MILD or real ale - i had a Boddington's shandy

Another place in Rugeley and without real ale, or MILD. Very popular with the locals, so good on them, just nothing for a real ale or MILD drinker.
