Tris C left this review about The Oak Wigmore

This was formerly called The Olde Oak so presumably was known as The Oak before that.
I came here with family and friends one summer in the early 1970s after a visit to the ruin of Wigmore Castle so can't remember much about the place but I do recall that we sat in the garden and ate freshly cut ham sandwiches with lashings of English mustard; it seemed quite magical.
Today, I doubt you'd get sliced white bread and watery ham because now the emphasis is firmly on food with an enticing menu and where a steak can set you back up to £30.
I'll probably return when next in the area.
