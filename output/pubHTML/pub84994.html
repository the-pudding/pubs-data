Real Ale Ray left this review about Cobblers

Situated down a side street off the main street and is away from the main thoroughfare. This seems to be predominantly a local's pub as visitors to the town wouldn't really know it was here. The interior consisted of a long narrow room with a raised bar and small seating area near the front window and brown leatherette banquette seating along the right hand side. There was also a small room at the rear of the pub. The interior also looked to have been recently decorated and the place was very clean and tidy. Six handpumps with five in use, this is a freehouse so expect to find some interesting beers alongside their two regulars Bass and GK Abbott. We went for the Salopian Fat Cat, which was in good shape and the prices were slightly lees than the main high street.

A fairly decent side street pub. Its basically a 2 roomed pub that stretches back. The servery is as you enter and has 6 handpumps on. I was hoping for a better selection of beers than I got today with an interesting Wincle concoction, Butcombe Gold Propaganda and the rather dull Abbott and Bass Bitter being the others. Still, it was a nice place for a drink and hopefully the place does well.
