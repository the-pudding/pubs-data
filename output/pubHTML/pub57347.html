Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Roebuck

Attractive old inn, supposedly dating from 1470, partly opened-out inside to leave an elongated 'L'-shaped layout. The main bar at the front transitions through a semi-traditional alehouse-style zone to a brightly-lit restaurant area at the rear. A covered side alley and courtyard leading to the toilets is enlivened by hand-drawn Belle Epoque artwork on some of the walls. Five real ales on handpump, with Greene King IPA, Deuchars IPA, Bombardier, Old Speckled Hen and the 'house' 4.1% Roebuck Ale (£3.75) on (and schooner-sized measures also noted as being available). Although the refurbishment won't be to everybody's taste, the atmosphere was good and the place is worth a visit if in the area.
