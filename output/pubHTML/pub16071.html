Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Britannia Inn

Semi-rural Shepherd Neame pub located west of the Shorncliffe Camp military complex and up the hill from Seabrook, and retaining the character of an unfussy village inn. Traditional single-bar interior with a separate dining room to one side and a front patio terrace (and tiny beer garden round the corner). Pub-grub menu, and the expected Master Brew, Whitstable Bay Pale and Spitfire (in fine form) from the three handpumps. Most notable, though, for the friendly welcome from the landlord and the overall good atmosphere so well worth seeking out.

E TA left this review about Britannia Inn

A quiet Shepherd and Neame local on a rural road just outside town. There is a main bar, floorboarded, with a woodburner and brick floor, a brick fireplace, basically furnished and with sparse décor. A smaller raised area to the side has a wooden floor and is laid for diners. The pub runs regular events such as live acts and Sunday family roasts. There is a tv screen in the bar and a selection of parlour games for entertainment on those long Autumn nights. The terrace at the front has tables and chairs but being adjacent to the road is mainly used as a smoking area. The staff are friendly and we were welcomed warmly. Only two ales on Spitfire and Canterbury Jack which were both in excellent condition. Worth the walk.

On 17th June 2013 - rating: 7
