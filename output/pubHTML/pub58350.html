Old Boots left this review about The White Hart

Two room Edwardian pub the interior is a bit plainer than you may expect but with cracking features including a ceramic counter and sumptuous bar back. Darts, shuffle board and pool in large back room as well as a couple of big screens. 6 pulls on the counter all from small breweries plus usual tap stuff and better .  Beer list is shown on clipboards on one wall in the front bar but very neatly done incorporating the Brewery logos, see photo, with descriptions of beers Noticing my beer was a bit manky the barman quietly took a half off into the back then came back and volunteered a replacement, he was right but I was just going to put it down as a poor pub so brilliant service and you better visit it hadn’t you?

Will Larter left this review about The White Hart

I was very glad to see, in passing by on my bike, that this national inventory pub had reopened. Thankfully the original features which led to its classification remain intact, though there are modern floorboards and the walls have been painted (wait for it...) gray. The magnificent ceramic bar is the outstanding feature (see photo), and it has been enhanced by the presence of half a dozen hand pumps and a number of keg fonts. Beers are listed, innovatively, on printed A4 pages attached each to their own clipboard and hung from hooks on the wall. In the room to the rear of this unusual, slightly curved building are a pool table and two shuffle boards - I don't think I have seen one in a pub before but knew instantly what they were. With an interesting selection of well-kept beers as well as the fascinating interior, this pub is a very welcome re-addition to Hull's real ale scene.

John Bonser left this review about White Hart

A bit out on a limb from the other pubs that comprise the Old Town Ale Trail is The White Hart, not to be confused of course with the much better known Ye Olde White Harte.

The White Hart is an imposing stand alone Edwardian building that dates back to 1904. The pub features in CAMRA’s National Inventory of Historic Pub Interiors for its superb ceramic bar counter, of which apparently only 11 remain in the UK. The front room retains original wood panelling and bench seating and a fine mahogany bar back with unusual glazed cupboards. It’s Grade 2 listed.

Outside we see one of the old trademark roundel signs telling us that it’s a former Mansfield Brewery house, now part of the Marstons empire of course. According to the local CAMRA Branch magazine – Pub Mirror ( Aug – Oct 2012 ) - real ale is available.

Unfortunately a prominent notice in the window tells us that the pub is now only open on Friday and Saturday evenings from 7 pm till late ( information current at August 2012 ) , so I didn’t get to call in on this occasion.

If I ever happened to be in the area again during those hours then, assuming it hasn’t been converted to a Tesco Metro or the like, I think I’d be inclined to at least poke my head round the door.
