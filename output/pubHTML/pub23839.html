Komakino . left this review about The Chandos Arms

I can't quite sum up Emily Ovenden's enthusiasm for this place, but the refurb has certainly made it a bit of a jack-of-all-trades. This is a high-ceilinged L-shape inside and was likely more than one room originally. There's a 'front room'-type area entered into via the Colindale Avenue entrance which houses a single pool table to the rear and is served by the short portion of the crook-shaped bar. The main area contains a variety of seating from comfy sofa to crescent booth to standard tables and chairs. There's an open kitchen hatch at the rear left of this area and a small corridor at the end leads to a small 'beer patio'. A DJ booth is located in front of the sofa and flatscreen TV hung against the rear wall showing BBC's Final Score on silent. Food on offer included pizzas, burgers and open sandwiches and clipboarded menus also had a separate, smaller 'drinks' menu including cocktails. Three ales were at the jump and included Fuller's London Pride, Caledonian Golden XPA and Fuller's American Fall - a half of the latter and a half of Coke came in at £4.00. Plenty of posters / flyers dotted around advertising Quiz Nights, Live Music, Comedy Clubs, Board Games, etc., although the ad for "DJ G-Nutz" is probably one to give a miss.

On 7th October 2017 - rating: 6
