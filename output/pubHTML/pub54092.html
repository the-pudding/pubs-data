Aqualung . left this review about Bishop Blaize (JD Wetherspoon)

This is a small modern Spoons overshadowed by a huge footbore stadium which happily was having no effect on the place on my Thursday midday visit. It's a roughly square room with a front patio, a raised area to the right and a drop down screen on the left. The furnishings are fairly typical. The bar has two sets of five hand pumps which had five beers doubled up. These were Titanic Iceberg, White Star & Cherry Dark, Oakham JHB and the recently watered down Robinsons Trooper which was also 30p dearer than the standard guest price of £2.29. i went for the White Star (£2.29) which was in good nick. I thought this one was OK but fear it would attract the Score Of Shame if I came here on a Saturday.

Al Bundy left this review about Bishop Blaize (JD Wetherspoon)

A fairly dull Wethers with a dull selection of ales. 2 banks of 5 handpumps with each ale duplicated. Pictures of ex Man Utd players dotted around as you would expect. A large rectangular place with a raised area to your right on entering. Naturally it'll be full of Southerners and foreigners on match days.

ROB Camra left this review about Bishop Blaize (JD Wetherspoon)

Fairly standard, new build small JDW. There's a raised area to the right and unusually there's a dartboard as the pub has a team in the local league. On my visit they were having a tap takeover which they do once every month. In this case it was Acorn brewery, so 5 of their beers were on plus one other guest. The Acorn Raku IPA was in good nick. It was absolutely packed on my Friday visit at 5:15, so they're obviously doing something right. I shudder to think what it's like when Utd are at home as the ground is just around the corner.
