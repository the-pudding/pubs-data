Pat Lyons left this review about The Red Lion

Feb 2013;- The Red Lion is Port Talbots premiere "Live Music" pub and seems to be on a mission to improve this aspect constantly. It is located literally besides the main Port Talbot Railway Station such that it shares a number of spaces in the car park. At the time of writing the only negative would be the limited amount of barfood available although it does do free meals during big sport events. Ok its strengths well the staff are consistent and the beer is good. Its well priced also. Only pint above £3 is Guinness which is £3.Age group well for once it seems to be everybody. Some bands bring along their followers and can be in the 18 age group and other times there is a very mixed crowd mainly I would guess around the 18-70 age group (haha) . Lately the pub inside has started to look a bit like Port Talbots Hard Rock Cafe and recently a Ska Festival was held at the venue with a great band made up of the Legendary Specials guitarist Roddy Radiation, Mark Harry and 2Rude, The Riff, Dick Dastardly, The Somethings, Cherry Hive, and The Side Effects.

On 9th February 2013 - rating: 8
