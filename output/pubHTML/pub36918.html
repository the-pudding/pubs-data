Aqualung . left this review about The Clock

This place seems to have changed a bit since previous reviews. It's a rectangular carpeted room split into different areas. TVs were on and music was playing. This is signed as a Craft Union pub which seems to be yet another marketing device by Enterprise (Ei Group) for their failing pub estate.
There are two hand pumps which had one unused and Bass. I didn't want to ruin my day by having to drink Bass in the final pub so went for a Grolsch Lager (£2.90) which was much as I expected ot to be.
I wouldn't return here.

Alan Winfield left this review about The Clock

The Clock is a fairly large looking pub that is situated at the top end of long street.
Once inside there is a decent sized lounge to the left,this room is carpeted and had a long bench seat running down the left side,there was a proper poker table to the front,there were normal tables and chairs elsewhere,there is a small narrow bar to the right which had basic bench seating running down the right side.
There were no real ales on the bar,i had a drink of John Smiths smooth crap which lived up to its name.
The pub was empty with me being the only customer on a latish Saturday afternoon visit,it is also up for let so things dont look that rosy here.
Overall i thought this was a pretty poor pub.

Pub visited 25/10/2014
