Alan Winfield left this review about The Chequers Country Inne

The Chequers Country Inne was the only pub open in Ullesthorpe on our visit here.
This pub is set sideways onto the main street,once inside this is a very big pub with lots of different rooms,i had a drink in the main bar,this was a large room with a beamed ceiling,the room seemed a bit short on tables and chairs making the room seem a bit bare,there was a dining type room to the right which was up a few steps and a small snug to the left,there was another room to the front of the pub.
The pub is a Marstons tied house and there were three real ales on the bar,i had a drink of Ringwood Old Scrumper which was quite a nice drink,the other beers were Pedigree and Jennings Cumberland.
There was some decent music playing and a few people dining,but the pub was quite empty given the size of it.
This is a nice looking pub that is nice enough inside but it seemed a bit dowdy in my opinion.

Pub visited 16/3/2013
