Andy Ven left this review about Wasdale Head Inn

Literally situated up a dead end at the top of Wast Water but it was still half full on Sunday lunch time when I visited. A former farmhouse, extended in the 1800s. The main entrance is to the hotel and is for residents only but to the right hand side is the entrance to Ritson’s, the public bar (named after the 19th Century landlord, Will Ritson) that opens at midday. There is one bar serving the main bar room and room off that, both with a slate floor, old wooden pews for seating and a side room with slightly softer covered seats. No fancy furnishings but a good place to visit nevertheless and in a great location. The Great Gable micro-brewery is based here http://www.greatgablebrewing.com/

On handpull there was Yate’s Cumbrian Ale, Great Gable, Yewbarrow Mild, Black Sheep. 4 more handpulls are used in summer when, I was told, the place gets really busy with walkers etc. The bar food menu is displayed on the board in the bar. Nothing fancy but you would still find something good enough for lunch.

On 18th February 2011 - rating: 7
