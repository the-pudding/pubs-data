ROB Camra left this review about Northern Whisper Taproom

Called in here several times in the last year, but have only just got around to reviewing it. The pub is as described by Peter below, but on all my visits there have only been Northern Whisper beers available on cask. I've tried all of them over time and they've always been in good nick. It attracts an eclectic mix of locals and visitors. Ted Robbins was in on our last visit, I believe he lives fairly locally. It's a good pub and one I'll keep on visiting.

peter ashworth left this review about Northern Whisper Taproom

Spacious and adequately sized micro pub situated adjacent to Rawtenstall market that opened in early October by the nearby based Northern Whisper Brewing Company
On entry to the left there are 4 booths which seats about 6 in each and to the right there are about 5 brightly coloured stools in front of a long wall mounted shelf. Moving forward there is a mustard coloured backrest seating area to the right then the long brick built bar.On the left are some large windows with chairs in front and a long concrete shelf for your drinks.
There were 6 casks on the go on my afternoon visit with 4 from there own brewery and 2 from The Mad Squirrel Brewery.There was also a large selection of 13 beers on tap plus a good selection of 11 gins if that is your tipple.
This place opens at 10am every day apart from Sunday when it does not open till 12 Noon.The reasonably priced beers range from £2.70p a pint to £2.90p with the Blighty Bitter £2.20p a pint (Weekdays 10am-5pm)
Certainly worth a visit
