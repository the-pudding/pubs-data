Quinno _ left this review about Horse & Groom

Small C18 pub with a thatched roof – ignore the Brakspear-style signage, this is a genuine freehouse. Small main bar as you enter with the servery to the right and a roaring log fire to left, as well as some wooden pew seating. To the rear is a small cosy formal dining room with jugs hanging from the beams. Small but nice beer garden out front. Big on grub and food majors on French style cuisine at French style prices, a main will set you back 15 quid. GBG 2016 listed and at the bar there were four on; White Horse Bitter (mediocre condition), Church End Goats Milk (very good nick), Church End Stout (good) and Cottage Try Me (I didn’t). It’s a decent little place, perhaps a bit small to cope with both the food and drinking brigades though fair play for trying to spin both plates simultaneously.

On 30th March 2016 - rating: 7
