E TA left this review about County Arms

Victorian corner pub on a main road, surrounded by hospital and university buildings. Currently operated by GK as one of their Meet and Eat chain, the ground floor has been knocked through into a J shape, the rear of which is, as Quinno describes below, on three successively lower levels. Pool table, gaming machines, a cash machine and TV screens are present, the noise from all was drowned out by the jukebox. The WiFi didn't work. The staff were pleasant and friendly, while the food was the usual chain slop. Only one ale on, GK IPA, which was drinkable but uninspiring. Fine for a pint if passing but I wouldn't make the effort to come from the City centre.

Long L shaped (note that the rear part is on three levels) Greene King pub with a very modern makeover. All very clinical but tidy enough and quite well done. Pool table at the far end plus plenty of TVs for sport. Just GK IPA (fair) and Abbot on. Best left to the locals.
