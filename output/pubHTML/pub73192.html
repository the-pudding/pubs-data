Quinno _ left this review about Danny's

Formerly an Irish bar called Conran’s, Danny’s is a revamped place that claims to be a ‘craft’ bar, but on closer inspection the selection was probably made by Danny Dyer. A single bland room over two separate areas separated by a central staircase (leading up to another, unexplored, room) and finished off with loud music of the Kangol hat / Kiss FM variety, just like the customers. Two ale pumps with Sharps Doom Bar and a stale Hop Back Summer Lightning. Perhaps this is where people from the Cathedral Hotel graduate after reaching the appropriate points total, like a Salisbury Scientologist.

E TA left this review about Conran's

Conran's is an Irish bar which has occasional live music and a reputation for real ale. On my last visit, however, there was no music on, and the only ale available was Landlord. The beer tasted very acidic, and when I mentioned it, the barman said it was they hadn't quite got the CO2 delivery right (not wishing to be pedantic, but that rules it out as real ale, I think). To his credit, he said that they had considered gravity dispense, but that would make the beer "too warm". I remain unconvinced of the real ale credential. It is worth visiting as part of a crawl, and I've yet to form a judgement about the music.

On 21st March 2011 - rating: 4
