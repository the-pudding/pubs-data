Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Hennessey's Bar

Strange place - I walked right by it initially but then turned back when I realised that it was a bar. Much more modern inside than one might expect, with the main seating supplemented by a side area up one step. DJ booth at the other end. Beer garden to rear (full of snow when I visited). Sports TVs. Open late (and until 04:00 Sunday morning). Three handpumps, offering Pride (£3.20, and in rather poor condition), Old Speckled Hen and Thatchers Cider.

On 27th January 2013 - rating: 5

Danny O'Revey left this review about Hennessey's Bar

Modern fit out of a traditional Digbeth industrial unit, its adjacent Moor Street Station car park and in the Irish Quarter. There is a beer garden at the rear.

Basically a sports bar, including 3D, one open room with chunky furniture, light and airy. Wouldn't win any grand design awards, but serves a purpose and it is what it is.

Lots of lager pumps but includes 2 real ales - Doom Bar and Tribute - served cold.

I would be happy watching a football game here but thats about it.

On 1st October 2012 - rating: 5
