David Ross left this review about Cairngorm Hotel

The Hotel has a bar in its main section next to the Reception area, but it seemed to be used mainly by residents or people dining. The real bar is through the door under the "Lounge Bar" sign to the side of the main entrance. It is very popular and was therefore extremely busy on both the Friday and Saturday nights I visited, despite the additional seating in the outdoor terrace at the front. There were three TVs, but these were turned down to allow customers to hear the folk singers. Prices were reasonable, with Cairngorm Stag costing £3.40 a pint and Cairngorm Trade Winds at £3.60. Tennent's, Carling, Cairn, Coors and Peroni lagers, plus Belhaven Best, Caledonia Best and Guinness were also on draught. Best described as a traditional style British pub, this is definitely one of the top drinking places in Aviemore.
