Alan Winfield left this review about The Hideaway Bar

The Hideaway Bar is situated roughly between Tufnell Park and Archway tube stations on the busy Junction Road.
Once inside there is a small oblong shaped room which runs to the rear where the bar faces,the room is bare boarded ,there is bench seating to the front and down the left side,there are normal tables and chairs on the right side.
There were no real ales on the bar which was no surprise,i had a drink of Kozel,which was too fizzy for my liking,there was also Peroni and Guinness on the bar.
The bar was empty on my Friday dinner visit,so i could not really get a proper impression of what it would be like when busier.
Not my type of place at all,but it was open so i had to go in.

Will Larter left this review about Hideaway

An unusual "neighbourhood bar and kitchen" in a shop unit, though this is no micropub. The door leads into a fairly small oblong room with the bar at the back. The floorboards are pretty scruffy, the tables and chairs have mostly seen better days and there are a couple of dark red leather sofas and a pair of armchairs further back near the bar. Some subdued pop music was being played on the sound system.

On the bar itself is a modest offering of just two lagers and Guinness. There is no real ale here. With a choice of Staropramen and Camden Hells I went for the latter, but only because I've never tried it before. I probably won't be drinking it again.

The chief attractions in the Hideaway are the paintings on the wall, apparently based on the covers of books of the sort that would have described people having sex if it hadn't been illegal to do so at the time. Appreciating the artist's efforts in depicting naked breasts, while simultaneously not depicting any nipples, helped to pass the time until I had finished my half.
