moby duck left this review about The Black Boy

Modernised foody pub, Greene King owned so I was prepared for disappointment on the ale front, it was good prep as the four handpumps offered up GK IPA, Belhaven Grand Slam , Yardbird and the intriguing (not) Black Boy Bitter 3.9 %, I had Guinness. Not a place I plan to return to in a hurry.

Quinno _ left this review about The Black Boy

A huge amount of money has been spent here and it is impressive. There's now a massive rear undercover drinking area with booth seating. GK ales still on and mighty expensive; however the quality is usually pretty good.

November 2012
'Country pub' routine is ruined somewhat by bring slap-bang on the A327 and next to a busy section of the M4. A recent(ish) refit has seen the place go a bit more upmarket though keeping the nice beamed feel. Beer is Greene King, three of their obvious ones (OSH, IPA, Abbot) served fairly well but not really much to retain the interest beyond a pint really. I guess the selling point is the food, which I haven't tried. Trivia: built around ancient wisteria tree located to rear of the pub, believed to be oldest wisteria in the country.

Ian Mapp left this review about The Black Boy

Not exactly made to feel welcome at this pub. Came in at 8:25 and asked if they were doing food. A curt check on the till and told to find my table and order at the bar.

I did this, and then realised that a) they have a restaurant around the back with table service and b) they had a specials menu that no-one told me about.

Got the feeling I was in the way.

Food was fine and service good. Thirsty after a walk, so had a Stella and cannot comment on the real ale.

On 9th July 2015 - rating: 6
