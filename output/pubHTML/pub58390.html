Real Ale Ray left this review about The Horse Shoes Inn

This is a two roomed pub, with the lounge and bar as you enter and the dining room on the left. The bar area had white painted walls and ceilings, which to me looked very bland and lacked character. There were a few blue and white porcelain walls plates along a shelf, which added a little interest. There was a couple of drinkers in on our lunchtime mid week visit, but the majority of the customers were here for a bite to eat. Three handpumps, two from Hadrian and Border and Tyneside Blonde. We sat out front in the beer garden, were I enjoyed the H&D Farne Island.

Iain Wallis left this review about The Horse Shoes Inn

Visit this friendly village local regularly. Hosts MarK & Louise and their team make you very welcome with great real ales and good pub food with an ever changing specials board. Good food well cooked with a local feel: just love the Northumbrian lamb :=). Wonderful to sit in the beer garden to watch the world go by or the sun set or sit inside and keep snug around the fire in the winter. Loads of parking and good mixture of friendly vilage locals and tourists.

On 20th June 2011 - rating: 10
