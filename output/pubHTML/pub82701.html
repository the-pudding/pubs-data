Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Georgian Townhouse

The name gives a strong clue, although this pub / restaurant and hotel is a conglomeration of at least three different buildings. The 'L'-shaped bar is at the Eaton end of the range, with dining rooms beyond and to the left. All refurbished in an upmarket semi-modern style. Patio / grassed beer garden to the rear. Just two of eight handpumps in operation, with Woodfoorde's Wherry and Grain 316 (£3.80) available, plus a reversed clip for Cork Cutters IPA on another handle.
