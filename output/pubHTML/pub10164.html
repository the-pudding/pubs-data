Old Blue left this review about Elm Tree Inn

Nice, traditional village pub. There is a small, square bar in front of you as you enter, two rooms to the right and a further room to the left with a large recessed fireplace, all with white walls and dark wooden beams, and a well looked after garden to the side. Amongst the drinks selection are two real ales, Otter Ale being permanent and the other changeable- Ringwood Best during our visit. It had a nice atmosphere and seemed to do a good balance of food and drinks trade, although tending more towards the food side. The Sunday carvery was £9 and other mains around £10+. We needed to eat something but weren’t overly hungry, so we just had starters, which were very good and generously portioned.

I liked this pub and would certainly come again for a drink and/or to eat.

On 2nd June 2012 - rating: 7
