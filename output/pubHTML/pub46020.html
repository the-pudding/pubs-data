Soup Dragon left this review about Beaufort Arms

This has been a pub since at least the later Victorian period, when all that was in Kittle was a few quarries, limekilns and dirt-tracks connecting them. The pub has grown over time, from what appears as a single extended cottage into two, side-by-side. It is in white render and white painted stone, with a porch, as well as front and back patio areas. The interior has a couple of rooms. The lounge is at the front and is smaller. It is in white and stone, with a nice fireplace and red seating. It felt the diningy kind of area. Victorian prints are on the walls and the TV wasn't on. The bar has military ephemera, home nation flags and some old photos. It too is in white and stone, with wood floor. The TV here had the news on, silently. There is a wurlitzer jukebox, but there was no music. The serivce was fine and a few were in, mainly eating. We didn't eat so can't comment on that. Beer; usual tap stuff with Brain's Stout - on handpull there was SA Gold (decent), an IPA (gravity dispensed) and sadly, the Dark went as i requested it. A nice overall pub, worth a visit for a swifty at least.
