Real Ale Ray left this review about The Wallace Hartley (JD Wetherspoon)

A very respectable Spoons this one that has been tastefully finished. The main features in here was the black glazed tiles along the bar front and ceiling columns throughout the pub, there was also a lot wall sections finished with patterned silver wallpaper. The rest of the walls were finished in oak panelling. Ten handpumps, with seven in use, we went for the Worsthorne Blonde Violin, which was in great shape.

Aqualung . left this review about The Wallace Hartley (JD Wetherspoon)

Apparently this was built as a pub / hotel called the Kings Head but was a Greek restaurant before JDW moved in. It's an open plan roughly square room with fairly typical fixtures and fittings. It was busy with a mixed clientele on my Wednesday evening visit.
The bar has ten hand pumps which had two unused, one available soon, a cider, the two GK beers, Hobgoblin, Coach House Blueberry, Peerless Penny Red Rye and Moorhouse Blond Witch. I went for the Penny Red Rye (£1.99) which was first class and superb value.
I thought this was a good Spoons.
