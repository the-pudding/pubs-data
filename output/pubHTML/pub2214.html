Komakino . left this review about The White Hart

Opposite the now-defunct Poachers, this has touches of character with old beams and posts (sadly now painted black) and a low-ceilinged part with an open brick fireplace to the right as you enter. The rather small bar is located far to the left in the bare-boarded area with a 2-Tone bar apron and some narrow bench seating opposite and flatscreen TV on the wall separating bar and seats. The main pub area has various standard tables and chairs and semi-sub-divisions with dark olive-coloured half-height wood panelling. A cosier area in front of the aforementioned fireplace has some upholstered / leather bench seats, varying tables and chairs and rendered walls. A further area to the back of the pub houses a dartboard and a "cubby hole" housing a selection of books. Four ales available, which is a good shout, albeit an unimaginative one, with Sharp's Doom Bar, St Austell Tribute, Brakspear Bitter and Marston's Pedigree. I had a pint of Adnam's Mosaic from keg which was a Chinese Burn at £4.30 but did the job nonetheless. I thought the interior clean, tidy and sympathetic to the building's age and heritage, but it's a tad 'local' and an iTunes-type playlist was unnecessarily on the loud side which docks a further mark.

On 19th May 2017 - rating: 4
