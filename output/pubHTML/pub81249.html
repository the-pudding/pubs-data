Soup Dragon left this review about Verdo Lounge

This is an end terraced 1930s building, which was originally a bank - it is now a cafe/bar. The frontage is of brick, with a glass and grey extended lower - some of which appear, from the inside, to be retractable doors. The interior is in brick and a snotty green colour, with a wood floor. The walls are filled with eclectic pictures and, other than a few sofas, the room is populated by wood tables that are part stained in blues/pinks etc. It is a cafe by day (when i visited) so had a family feel, with cake and birthday party balloons etc. I didn't see a TV and the music was easy and at a decent level. The service was fine. After the General's review below, i wish i had visited later, as i felt a tad out of place at my visit. Beer; a few tap drinks, nut i noticed the Bath Ale's Toga Man, so had that - it was a little cloudy, but tasted fine (didn't notice at first as a bit gloomy inside). Happy i popped in, but if i went back it would be later on an evening.
