Jon B left this review about The Masons Arms

tucked away on a road to nowhere on an old corner of the village, by far the best pub in this area. a free house that has been in the same family since seemingly the beginning of time, with all the quirkiness that you get from such places. plenty of bric a brac and various sports and music memorabilia make up the decor. 5 real ales available, on constant rotation so you never know what you're going to get. my local and highly recommended.

Anthony Sankey left this review about The Masons Arms

We discovered this hidden gem of a country pub when we were out for a walk between Billinge and Carr Mill Dam. There were some really well kept cask ales, good wines, an open fire, friendly staff and locals, a beer garden overlooking open fields, and the most civilized smoking shelter we've ever come across. The interior had been tastefully restored, and there were all kinds of interesting artifacts, including rare rugby league memorabilia. It had just won the CAMRA pub of the year 2010 award for the area. We liked it so much, I offered to do them a website (for free) - it's at http://www.masonsarmsbillinge.co.uk - they're lovely people. Well worth a visit - we'll be back.‎

On 9th June 2010 - rating: 9
