Nick Davies left this review about Fox & Hounds

Charwelton's biggest claim to fame is probably the BT tower which is visible from miles around. Other than that it's a tiny village near the source of the Cherwell. The traditonal Cotswold stone pub is on the main road which skirts the village's western side, and has existed for maybe five hundred years. After a period of closure it's now in the hands of a local cooperative, who have turned it into a Michelin listed restaurant with a reasonably pubby bar on the side. It is all intensely gastrified, of course, and the bar bit is short on usable furniture unless you can cope with low sofas or highchairs, and you need a lot of money. Eight quid for a pint and a half of very pleasant Nobby's bitter (that's all there was) and a pack of lactose-free cheese (??) and onion is pushing it. You looking at over a tenner for a granded-up sandwich.

This is a fairly remote, un-touristed area, and I do wonder if there's the custom round here for somewhere so self-consciously middle-class. But if it's a nice day the tables outside are pleasant to linger at if your travelling this way.
