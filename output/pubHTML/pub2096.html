Komakino . left this review about Rothschild Arms

Situated in a row of terraced houses, this appears to have originally been a two-roomer with red-painted brick fireplaces on either side of the boxy room and a partition post in front of the rear wall-located bar. Half-height white panelling runs along the walls with artex-type wallpaper above. Two bay windows either side of the central entrance contain wooden bench seating - the one on the right containing board games hidden underneath as well as, bizarrely, housing a female mannequin in the window. A pool table is housed in a sunken area to the left of the room in what seems to be an extension to the original interior. Other paraphernalia included dartboard and rubber oche against the right-hand wall, American licence plates affixed to the ceiling beams and a 'Celebrity Death List' chalked up on two small blackboards. Bar snacks were available in a fridge behind the bar (with a microwave next to it), including sausage rolls. No real ale - the only option (Sharp's Doom Bar) was reversed, so scored deliberately low until a revisit can be made.

On 12th May 2017 - rating: 3
