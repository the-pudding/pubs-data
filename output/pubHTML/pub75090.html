Oggwyn Great left this review about The Cheshire Cat

Just across the road from the excellent Black Lion , this is a Black and White half timbered building dating from the 1670s , once inside the interior is a strange mixture of old wood and modern glass and chrome , with a glass wall dividing the pub from the small courtyard area .
I stayed in the side bar as it was more pub like , two handpulls , one hiding behind a pile of menus with Weetwood Cheshire Cat and TT Landlord .
As the previous review points out it is rather self important .
Not my sort of place at all , but it was pretty busy so must be doing something right .

On 24th January 2018 - no rating submitted

Al Bundy left this review about The Cheshire Cat

Edge of town place set in the surroundings of its own backside. A mixture of old style and arty farty nonsense. 2 roomed with low beams and wooden posts. There are a total of 3 handpumps, one in the larger room and 2 in the smaller room to the left, dispensing Doom Bore and Tim Taylor Boltmaker today. Has a half decent courtyard area but is very much food led. Expensive and pretentious. Very much like the clientele.
