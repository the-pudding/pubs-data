Aqualung . left this review about White Lion Inn

Heptonstall's second pub is a bit more open plan with the bar area on the left as you enter dominated by a fireplace and some seating around the wall. Over to the right is an area of low tables and chairs. I didn't investigate any further than this. Happily this one has no TV screens.
The bar has five hand pumps and I noticed three non mainstream ciders. The beers were Thwaites Wainwright, Goose Eye Chinook Pale, Elland 1872 Porter, Bath Special and Empire White Wolf. I went for the Chinook Pale (£3.20) and the 1872 (£3.30) both in good condition but the 1872 being particularly good. Both village pubs make the short bus ride up here (you wouldn't want to walk it!) well worth while. This one is GBG 2016 listed.

Steve of N21 left this review about White Lion Inn

This is the second pub in the village of Heptonstall a little walk up the hill from the Cross Inn.
It is a comfortable traditional décor three roomed Thwaites house with a public bar with flat screen tele off to the left and more comfortable lounge area off to the right. And then through the lounge is a third room laid out as a dining area. Then out the back is a fairly small but pleasant paved garden area. We didn't eat but several people were and the steak sandwiches and burgers were substantial and looked pretty good.
Three Thwaites ales were on at the time of my visit, Original , Wainrights and Bomber. All very passable and the Wainrights was particularly pleasant. All in all, a solid pub if you are visiting or walking in the vicinity of this picturesque village..

On 4th June 2009 - rating: 7
