Old Boots left this review about Bruce Arms Public House

Traditional pub tucked away in a side street off the market place. White washed building partly with exposed stone, ceiling beams, and wood interior, it comprises one room that appears to have been created out of three smaller ones. Tired Black Sheep Bitter plus Theakston's Best and John Smith's Cask, a fourth handpull was not in use during my visit. Run of the mill range of kegs; Becks, 1664, Fosters, Carling plus Guinness and a cider. Bench seating all round, a large TV in one room plus a selection of machines and a dart board, there is also a pleasant rear garden. When I was there only the landlord was working, serving a few quiet older men and women. Limited weekday opening hours.

===Summer 2010 Update===

Now sharing the building with an Indian Restaurant and Takeaway, "Spice Lounge", as the pungent smells will affirm, otherwise much the same. My Golden Sheep was excellent though.
