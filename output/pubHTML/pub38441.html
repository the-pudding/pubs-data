Real Ale Ray left this review about The Park Tavern

This was our first pub to visit on our Vintage bus tour of the Black Country and what a good place to start. The service was excellent in spite of the large number of people entering the pub at once. A phone call ahead of our visit did help and the pub was well staffed. We spent a full hour here, which was a good choice as the beer was excellent, staff friendly and helpful and their cobs on the bar were top notch at £1.50 each.
I went for the Enville, which was in peak condition. Other beers on was the Enville Ginger, Bathams, Timothy Taylor and the guest was a Bombardier Special. The interior is as Soupy describes, the landlord made the effort to stand at the door and thank us for our visit when we were leaving.

Soup Dragon left this review about The Park Tavern

This is a detached Victorian corner pub with a beer garden. The pub is of a cottage-style, extended at the back and is in cream, with black bay windows on a stone foundation, and a dog-tooth cornice. The interior has a couple of rooms. To the left is the lounge, which is in a cream flower wallpaper, with red chimney breasts and a white and beam ceiling. Red perimeter seats are matched with the carpet. There are a couple of fireplaces. There are lots of paintings of the pub and other places on the walls. The TV wasn't on in here. The other room is the bar - where a coiple of TVs were on showing the tennis. This room is in green and white, with a green brick fire. There is a dart board and a few plates and ornaments. The service was fine and the place busy with a mixed clientele. Beer; usual tap stuff with Enville Ginger, Bathams Bitter and Timothy Taylor Landlord on handpull - the beer was really good. A class little place and one you could spend all day in. Definitely worth a visit.
