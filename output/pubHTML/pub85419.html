Graham Coombs left this review about The Cabin

With the demise of the last of the many pubs that used to be on the London Road (Cambridge Hotel) in 2016, the Cabin brings a licensed premise back to this somewhat shabby end of town, otherwise populated by tattoo parlours and Polish grocers. It is a one room shop conversion, decorated with an esoteric range of assorted junk and random furnishings producing a friendly if somewhat BoHo feel. There is a surprisingly large outdoor patio area at the rear. Sadly no real ales as yet, but it does offer a fair range of bottled English beers, albeit at prices rather more than double you can find them in supermarkets. Food is restricted to paninis, cheeseboards and the like. Not a conventional pub by any means, but it works as a chilled-out place to relax.
