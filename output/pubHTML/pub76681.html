Alan Winfield left this review about Bakers Arms

The Bakers Arms looks quite nice from the outside with white painted walls and a thatched roof.
Once inside there was one room that had different drinking areas,the small bar was to the left and there were only two pumps on it one for a lager and one with real ale on.
The pub was carpeted throughout and had different sizes of chunky wooden tables each of these tables had a lit candle on it but were not laid out for diners so normal drinkers could sit at any table,the pub was fairly dimly lit and it would probably suit a couple of lovers not a beer drinker like my self.
The one real ale was Langton Inclined Plane which was drinkable but slighly past its best.
The pub was doing meals which were quite expensive but there were quite a few people dining so the food must be fairly decent.

Pub visited 10/3/2012
