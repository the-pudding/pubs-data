Nick Davies left this review about The Plough

Very nice 18th century Cotswold stone village pub comprising a warren of small rooms with a modern restaurant at the back. Outdoor seating to the front and rear. Claims to be a coaching inn though I doubt many coaches passed this way, though those who like such things may wish to learn about the Welsh Road which passes nearby. A friendly welcome on a hot Bank Holiday afteroon, where to our surpise a mini beer festival complete with live band was taking place in the back garden. Unfortunetly driving meant we could't take part beyond a very pleasant pint of Great Oakley Wagtail. The handpumps in the bar seem to dispense more routine refreshments such as GKIPA.

It's in the GBG, and well worth a visit, but watch the time as it's only open evenings during the week.

On 29th August 2017 - rating: 7

Ian Mapp left this review about The Plough

Nice village pub that is seemingly unchanged for years.

All stone floors and little nooks and crannies inside.

Three real ales on - including a never seen before effort from the Great Oakley Brewery.

Food and service were both excellent and good value.

A nice country Inn.

Photos at my blog - http://bit.ly/2cEyX9c
