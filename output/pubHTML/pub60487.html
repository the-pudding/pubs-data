Steve C left this review about The White Hart

The location of the White Hart dictates that it has gone down the dry sales route to become a ‘Country Dining and Free House’ pub. The interior consists of a thin bar that faces the entrance at the front of the pub. To the left is seating overlooked by a large inglenook and a larger seating area is found to the right where another inglenook can be found. At the rear is a conservatory that can be used for private functions.
Available from the bar during my recent Friday evening visit were Longman Brewery’s Best Bitter and Harvey’s IPA alongside a premium keg range. This pub is closed on Mondays and during the rest of the week opens for lunch (12:00 – 15:00) and dinner (18:00 – 23:00). The pub is open all day Saturdays (12:00 – 23:00) and Sunday (12:00 – 22:00). I saw people being turned away for food, so I would book in advance if eating.
Out the back is a beer garden and to the left of the pub is a large car park. I found the staff to be very polite and friendly, but this isn’t a pub I would visit again just for a beer.

On 8th October 2018 - rating: 6
