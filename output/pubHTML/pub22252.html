Rex Rattus left this review about The Iron Duke

This is a narrow fronted pub with some impressively floribund hanging baskets adorning the facade. It doesn’t seem to have changed since my last visit a couple of years ago. The room as you enter is predictably narrow with the counter on the right taking up a fair bit of the room, but it opens up a bit at the back. It’s attractively bare boarded with narrow parquet style varnished boards; there are some tall stools/tables but the majority of the furnishings are upholstered benches and normal tables and chairs. Obviously there are numerous prints on the walls featuring the Iron Duke himself. I also spotted a couple of TVs on, but muted, showing some golf during my Wednesday lunchtime visit.
This is a Fuller’s pub, which had on ESB, Discovery and London Pride (£3.50 a pint) on. The food menus are attached to clipboards on the tables. The selection seemed to be reasonable pub grub – a cheese and pickle sandwich with a bit of salad is £4.95 and the steak & ale pie is £8.95.
The service was friendly enough. This is a decent little pub in an area where they are not exactly thick on the ground. I’ll probably be in again sometime.
