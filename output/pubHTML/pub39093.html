Ian Mapp left this review about The Duke of Cambridge

Black country ales pub - doing more or less what they all do - usual BFG, Pig on the Wall and Fireside and then a few random ales from breweries from around the UK.

Nice building, converted from C17th cottages - bar to left, snug to right and a pool room on the back.

Good Beer Guide 2019.

My Fixed Wheel Shell Corner looked dreadful, completely hazy. Maybe a one off.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Duke of Cambridge

Excellent Black Country Ales pub, retaining a multi-room layout with very traditional serveries in the bar and snug on either side of the entrance corridor and two other rooms served by hatches further back. There is also a partly covered side patio and a rear beer garden. I found seven real ales - including three of their own beers and guests such as the Stratford Malty Pig Bitter (£3.20) - and a cider divided between the various handpumps on the two counters. Well worth seeking out.

Danny O'Revey left this review about The Duke Of Cambridge

Fantastic local, part of the Black Country Ales chain and a fine advert for them. Various seating areas around the bar, split into individual areas. A really cosy little place with bags of atmosphere. Its a shame the fire isn't real.

The beer quality of the 6 real ales is top notch.
