Graham Coombs left this review about The Griffin Inn

Upretentious and unspoilt rural local. Opened into one room but retaining three distinct areas - a main room with tiled floor, a snug area and a games room behind, all plainly but tidily furnished. Two ales on pump (IPA and Landlord at time of visit) which were very drinkable - Cask Marque listed. Food of simple rather than gastro variety, but quite acceptable and reasonably priced (not all sessions though). Outside there is a garden area with barbecue and the pub has its own camping/caravanning field behind. A proper pub and worth supporting.

On 1st September 2016 - rating: 8
