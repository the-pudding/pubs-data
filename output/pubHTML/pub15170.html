Graham Coombs left this review about The King Lud

A rather boisterous and fairly basic single room pub strategically located opposite Esplanade station. OK for a quick pint when quiet, but probably best avoided when it is crowded. It also has a late night licence. Has a fair range of beers, including local and national brews, including some sourced from round the back of the bar, possibly straight from the barrel. Quality and availability seems a bit hit and miss however.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The King Lud

The distinctive mock-Tudor façade stands out amongst the Esplanade buildings, but there is nothing very distinctive about the plainly furnished rectangular interior. Also has several benches out front looking out towards the pier. Basic menu. Bombardier, Cornish Coaster and Gales HSB being served from three of four handpumps, with the other displaying clips for two draught ciders.
