moby duck left this review about Red, White and Blue

A rather basic street corner pub, single roomed with wood floor and white walls and basic seating . A number of darts trophies are lined up on a high shelf so I guess the dart board see's some action. The pub was empty on my arrival 15 minutes into opening time on a Sunday, four pumps on the bar all clipped but were doubled up with Pride and HSB. There was nothing to this pub that made me want to hang around to long.

Malden man left this review about Red, White and Blue

A small street corner pub with a single room which narrows to the rear. The bar is situated to one side, the front area is boarded changing to carpet towards the back bit. The pub has a locals regulars type of feel with people all seeming to know each other and engaging with the chap behind the bar who I took to be the landlord. Community activities include a Monday quiz and a Sunday meat raffle. A piano sits against a side wall so perhaps there is the occasional knees up and singalong, it feels like that sort of place, there is a dartboard opposite the bar on a side wall, live sport is shown however on Friday early evening I had to endure a commercial radio station.
Lower walls are wainscoted and light coloured, seating is firmly traditional, décor includes a large Fuller, Smith and Turner mirror, clearly an import in a former Gale's house but also a wonderful old Victorian map of Portsmouth, plus a few small framed prints of old manor houses. Beers nowadays from Fullers, Pride on two pumps plus Seafarers and HSB. A proper old fashioned street corner boozer.

E TA left this review about Red, White and Blue

A small single bar late Victorian Fullers (ex-Gales) corner pub in a busy high street area. The flooring is a mix of carpets and boards, there is a tv screen and a projector, and there are newspapers available. There is 'free' wifi but it is Fullers's own and you have to give them your mobile number to use it. I found the pub very homely and friendly and the staff helpful. Two ales on – I had a pint of Bengal Tiger which was in very good condition and served at the right temperature. Worth dropping in for a pint if passing.
