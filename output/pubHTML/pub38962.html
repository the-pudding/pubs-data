Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about White Horse

Housing estate pub, with the lounge bar featuring an unusual, almost circular, layout with an expansive bow-windowed frontage. The busier rectangular side bar houses the pool table, dartboard, table football, etc. Despite some external Marston's signage, the real ales from two of four handpumps were Landlord and a decent Wye Valley Bitter (£2.90).

Soup Dragon left this review about White Horse

Repainted in Marston's dull way, but then sold to a another Pubco. The layout is the same, although now redecorated. It seems more stable after suffering under a succession of publicans, it now seems to have an interested and friendly couple looking to take it on. They do a small but home-cooked menu. The bar has pool, darts, bar footy and a TV for sport. It now has 5 real ales: Banks's MILD (nice), bitter, Hobgoblin Gold, and, as it is no longer tied to Marston's, Tim Taylor Landlord and Doombar. I will keep and eye on this one again.
*******************************
June 2011: This is a detached Marstons 60s looking pub and eatery in undressed brick, so it tends to look bland from the outside. It does have a beer garden and kid's play area.

The interior has a couple of rooms, a bar and a lounge/dining area. The bar is in green and cream walls, with white ceiling and a brown and cream chessboard style flooring. There are a few TVs showing Sky Sports usually and a few signed footy shirts on the walls for interest. It has high stools and brown perimeter seats. This room seems to be for the younger crowd and the sport watchers. The lounge/dining room has a round room with a conservatory extension. It is in the same decor, with carpeting. I have eaten here and the food is generally good. There is a TV here as well, often not on. This room attractes the maturer visitor. The bar staff have always been friendly and the music varies, but is never too loud.

There are the usual taps, including Peroni lager. Handpulls included Banks's MILD and bitter, Brakspear Buzz, Hobgoblin and Cocker-Hoop. The beer tends to be consistently good.

I pop in here on a semi-regular basis, but tend to go in the lounge. Good for families in the nice weather with the garden and play area.
