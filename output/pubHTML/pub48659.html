Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Dynamo Dundee

Craft beer outlet with a narrow bar and a small saloon at the back. Both parts are quite traditional in appearance, especially with the dark-wood bar-backs, but this one is definitely aimed at a younger market. No real ale on handpump, although it is not possible to determine whether any of the 24 taps are key-keg or just keg. However, the choice of styles from owners Six°North and a wide range of guests is very impressive, and I enjoyed my pint of Skeleton Blues US wheat beer (£4.85).

David Ross left this review about The Star & Garter

This consists of a long, narrow, dark bar with a tiny lounge through a door at the back. There is a good range of beer on draught, including McEwan's 80 Shilling, Guinness, Belhaven Best, McEwan's IPA and Tennent's, Stella Artois and Heverlee lagers, as well as Magner's cider. With the exception of the uninspiring Tay Bar on the platform, this is, probably, the nearest pub to Dundee Railway Station, which is worth knowing if you're travelling to the city by train.

On 19th March 2016 - rating: 8
