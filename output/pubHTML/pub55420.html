ROB Camra left this review about West Park Hotel

Very modern looking hotel bar that really smells of cooking. They specialise in table service to diners although there are seats at the bar. After we sat down at the bar we waited and waited and waited, but the barman was far too busy doing "things" to serve customers who weren't dining. Only Black Sheep & Golden Sheep on the bar anyway, so after a bit more waiting we thought "sod it" and left and went to the far superior Coach & Horses instead..

Old Boots left this review about West Park Hotel

After a major and long lasting refurb it is now completely changed from its previous incarnation becoming a very swish modern bar-restaurant-hotel. One large space is subdivided by windows and pierced wooden screens. Flooring is laminate and the ceiling an interesting essay of black blocks and metallised silver tiles. Leather booth plus leather arm-stools complement more standard chairs and tables in modern furnishings. The tables are of course candle powered. Automatic front doors are nice touch for the elderly or drunk, as is the projection of the name onto the pavement outside. The counter is relocated to a side wall and the three pulls dispense extraordinarily bland Black Sheep and Theakstons Best - the drinks menu lists only Black Sheep Bitter and Golden Sheep along with wines, spirits, cocktails and bottled beers. The keg choice includes Brooklyn. Naturally the decor is in trendy grey but skilfully applied, everything here, with the exception of the beer, exudes an air of high quality. The many staff wear long grey aprons except the Maitre D'. Not one for the dedicated beer drinker perhaps but a high quality venue none the less.
