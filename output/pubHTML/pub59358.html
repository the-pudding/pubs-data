Ian Mapp left this review about The Bridgewater Arms

Situated in great walking country - the Chiltern Way runs from the pub car park.

Impressive looking pub inside and out. Very busy on a Sunday Lunch - and not just with diners - a high number of regular drinkerss were at the top part in a separate bar area.

A couple of wood burning stoves.

Beer wise - Greene king - so not the most exciting selection imaginable.

Will Larter left this review about The Bridgewater Arms

It's a few years since I've been here, and I think they must have had some internal rearrangement, because I didn't recognise the pub's interior at all. There's a large car park opposite, and this is a popular spot for people coming out from nearby towns (even from London, I dare say) to Ashridge and Ivinghoe Beacon.

The bar counter had five hand pumps: Green King IPA, Abbot, Old Speckled Hen and London Glory, plus an unexpected guest in Tyneside Blonde. I can remember this place from when I used to live in Hemel Hempstead, and it was much more of a traditional village pub, or at least it was a hotel with much more of a traditional public bar. There's a lot of blurring of lines nowadays which I'm not sure is a good thing.

Date of visit: 31st January 2017
