Nick Davies left this review about The Royal Oak

This is a beautifully situated old pub an a rural location a good way from anywhere, but then the heart sinks when all the signs advertise the gastronomic delights therein: "fresh fish and crustacea delivered daily," that sort of thing. However, bouyed up by the knowledge that the pub has been in the GBG for a year or two so can't be too bad you soldier on. Once in you find it isn't all that bad, or about 20% of it isn't anyway. The bar at the front and the area to the left look unchanged from when this was properly a rural local but the rest of it, a huge extension to the rear isn't a pub at all but an expensive restaurant for the hugely wealthy people who live round here.

As long as you keep to the pub bit it's fine, and the beer is very good too, Larkin's, Bill King, Harvey's in respectable form, and friendly service - in all too many pubs of this sort wanting just a drink and not spending 30 quid on food is seen as aberrant behaviour and you're made to stand in a corner by the bog door to drink it, but no problem here.

Stop by if you are in the area, but there are better choices around.

On 20th September 2010 - rating: 7
