Bucking Fastard left this review about Resting Hare

The entrance is quite grand ,up a stone staircase with seating terraces to either side,the high ceiling bar has some art deco features and was clearly a bar for the hotel next door,but these days there is no connection.The wooden floor and hip vibe plus loud muzak makes this place noisy when busy but the seating is mainly large banquettes with just a few high tables in the centre and lots of vertical drinking options infront of the U shaped bar with a classy bar back incorporating the rear obsured glass window.
The chalk board describes the 12 keg options,some leading microbrewers were featured but some selections looked mainstream.The 4 handpumps were offering Adnams Southwold Bitter and Old Ale,Redemption Trinity and Old Dairy Uber Brew(decent).A worthy selection but this bar will never displace the nearby Euston Tap for the real ale fan.
Pub Grub style food is served,and I could have been tempted to stay longer,it's worth popping in to see what's on offer.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Resting Hare

Previously dull hotel bar reinvented as a new-style alehouse with a separate side-street entrance a few years ago. The rectangular room features a compact horseshoe servery at the far end and about as much seating of various types as can be fitted in (which isn't a huge amount). Decorative features include some attractive Art Deco lanterns and mirrors, and the colour scheme is reasonably light. Also has a patio terrace by the steps leading to the main door. Generally efficient staff. Four handpumps, with Moor So'hop (£4.00), two beers from Adnams - Southwold and Mosaic - and a cider were available from the four handpumps on this visit. There is also a decent selection of craft beers available from the ten keg taps too.
