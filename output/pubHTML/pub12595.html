Quinno _ left this review about Air Balloon

The infamous Air Balloon (infamous to drivers unfortunate enough to have to do battle with the ridiculous junction it occupies) ended up being an emergency stop after Desmond’s Mini Cooper conked-out after conquering the preceding hill. So a quick stop here to grab a few litres of water for the parched radiator (the friendly licensee offered to fill the vessel himself). Aimed at the (slowly) passing food trade, the exterior is pretty and well-described below. Three GK ales on; OSH, IPA Gold and a pretty decent Abbot. Didn’t stay long enough to make much note about the interior bar some low ceilings and undressed Cotswold Stone, but it was certainly pretty busy.

Soup Dragon left this review about Air Balloon

An undressed stone Old English Inns chain pub and eatery, with a couple of beer gardens, patio and a kid's play area. It looks like a former farmouse, now at a major traffic island. Underwent a refurbishment in May 2010.

The interior is open plan and on a couple of levels. It is turned over mainly to dining, though there is a sofa area where there is a nice stone fireplace. The decor is a mix of undressed Cotswold stone, white, a bit of green and purple, along with ceiling and support beams. The walls mainly have arty pictures, though there are a few bits of fun ephemera, like the balloon light fixture - and the tables have candles. The place is very popular with families and a general mixed clientele, due to its location and one has to assume, its food - it didn't eat, so i can't comment on that. The service was fine, when you got it - as i say, it was actually too busy, so i just wanted to get out to be honest (not that the pub would be complaining!)

Beer; tap stuff with GK IPA, Abbots, Spekky Hen and GK LBW on handpull. The Spekky was fine.

I would be happy to stop here to eat, which really is its main target audience. Not really my kind of place just for a drink unless in the beer garden. I am not really a Greene King fan either.
