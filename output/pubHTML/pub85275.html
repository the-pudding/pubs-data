David Ross left this review about Kellyburn Bar

Being the bar of Kellybank Caravan Park, this is part of a larger complex which also includes games rooms, an amusement arcade, an outdoor children's play area and a beer garden. There is also a lounge bar which was closed on the Saturday afternoon of my visit, but was being prepared for what looked like an evening function. The bar was very busy, with several muted TVs, loud music and children running around. The customers appeared to be mainly caravanners, but it seems to be open to the general public too. No one questioned my presence, although I admitted that I wasn't a resident and had just been out walking the nearby Kelly Cut footpath and was in for a thirst quenching pint. Despite the fact that it involves a strenuous uphill walk from Wemyss Bay, it might also attact some local custom due to there being only one pub in the village itself. The Wemyss Bay Holiday Park, further down the hill, also has a bar. A pint of Carling lager here cost me £3.00 and Tennent's and Coors lagers, Caffrey's, Guinness and Strongbow cider were also on draught.
