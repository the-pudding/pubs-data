Will Larter left this review about The Town Street Tap

Having missed an opportunity for a drink here a few weeks before, when I passed through Duffield some hours before opening time, a quick look at the bus timetable when in Darley Abbey showed me a window of opportunity. The next bus going to Belper would drop me off near the pub some 13 minutes before the next one going south would take me back to Derby. So this review is based on the briefest of acquaintances, but I must say I was quietly impressed. As both Alan and Ray have observed, this is a pub without a bar counter, the beers and ciders being listed on blackboards on both sides of a beam across the middle of the shop. Service was very prompt and the beer was in delightful condition. The cold room where the beers are stillaged is visible when following the signs to the toilet, and there were clearly a lot of beers waiting to come on (with, I think, four or five listed as available). There's a real fire and a friendly sort of atmosphere - there were about 10 other drinkers already here when I arrived 10 minutes after opening time. For those who like that sort of thing, there were pork pies and scotch eggs available, and bowls of water for canine companions. Obviously a well used and well loved pub. I'll be back for a more leisurely visit.

Date of visit: 28th December 2017

Real Ale Ray left this review about The Town Street Tap

Tollgate brewery's second micropub venture, the first one is in Ashby de la Zouch. The interior is modern and has the same colour scheme as the Tap at No 76 in Ashby, which is dark greys and orange. The pub was busy on our Saturday eve visit and we managed to get a table in the corner, where another couple joined us, so a conversation soon stuck up.
We didn't spend long on this visit as the beer choice was all APA's and one dark beer, which is fine on other occasions, but I wanted something light and hoppy.
As Alan mentions below, there is no bar, so we were met by one of the staff on arrival, taken through the choice of five ales and then told we will bring the beer over to you. We went for the Vog Brewery Barry Speak Easy, a 4.6% APA, which after a good afternoon's crawl around the locality, was slightly overbearing for me.

On 13th August 2017 - rating: 7
