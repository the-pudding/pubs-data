Real Ale Ray left this review about The White Lion Hotel

This is a multi roomed pub, which is traditional in style and I found it to be very tastefully decorated. The main bar had low beamed ceilings and a low slung bar counter. The area along the bar was quite narrow between the counter and the tables, which was ok for us standing at the bar when the place was quiet. The staff in here were quite polished and prices were on the steep side, never the less, I did like this pub. I especially liked my visit winter time, when the fires are lit and the lamps around the place gave nice subdued lighting. Five pumps on the bar, we all went for the Saltaire Blonde, which was in very good shape.

Alan Winfield left this review about The White Lion Hotel

The White Lion is a stone built pub that is situated at the top end of Hebden Bridge.
Once inside there is a single room with dining area to the front left,which was busy,the main bar area has some normal tables and chairs and posh looking chairs and tables.
There was a good choice of real ales on the bar,i had a drink of Copper Dragon Golden Pippin which went down very well,the other beers noted were Black Sheep Bitter,Taylor Landlord,Thwaites Wainwright,plus one more i failed to read my notes of.
I was happy enough having a drink in this pub.

Old Boots left this review about The White Lion Hotel

Smaller than it looks from the outside, this is a fairly standard type of town centre pub in market towns, the semi-posh hotel bar and dining catering to familes and older punters. There is nothing to frighten the horses in the beer line up, Black Sheep, Taylors, Wainwright and Copper Dragon, all reassuringly familiar to Yorkshire drinkers, the more interesting beer is White Lion Amber, a classic bitter brewed by Stod Fold so not perhaps that interesting but well enough kept. The keg line up is national brands plus a couple of wildcards in the form of Kozel and Ashahi. The space is broken up into several areas, all set up for dining, with a bit of an old world feel, the counter lines one wall of a long room running front to back, there's a snug behind that and a couple of rooms either side of the front door, more space is lurking down a service corridor for the toilets and kitchen. Decor as expected with old photos, old bills and photos of miscellaneous bit of architecture. Staffing also as expected very much in the cold, efficient, hotel style.
