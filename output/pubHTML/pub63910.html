Tris C left this review about Trafalgar Tavern

A mentioned, this is an imposing riverside pub dating from 1837 so depending on which side of 20th June 1837 it was built, it's either Georgian or Victorian with an interior that is fairly ornate Regency albeit 25 years too late. In effect it looks like a grand hotel and overall is very attractive, and accords with the description submitted below by Mr. Man; that's the good bit. The restaurant room, bar area and side annex were extremely stygian though not so that I couldn't see the ugly condiment bottles on the tables; only the entrance room seemed to have any illumination. It seemed that we had arrived on quiz night; I don't know if each quiz is music-themed, but this was and snippets of songs were being blasted out of monitor speakers by the quizmaster at deafening levels - I've never been to a pub quiz that is trying to out decibel The Who. Most of the customers seemed to be students from the neighbouring University of Greenwich. I did wonder if this place doubled as the student union bar.
Ales: Nelson Brewery's Trafalgar and IPA, Sharp's Doom Bar and Adnam's Mosaic and Broadside at £4.50 a pint which is less than The Yacht next door, a Taylor Walker pub no less; it was as good as Broadside ever gets.
We drank up sharpish and left, to avoid getting tinnitus. I didn't care much for this place and don't envisage a return visit - The Cutty Sark, a few hundred yards to the east is far superior.

On 26th November 2016 - rating: 3

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Trafalgar Tavern

An imposing building on the eastern side of the Royal Naval College, partially opened out inside to leave a 'L'-shaped bar with various seating areas and at least two dining rooms beyond. Refurbished with modern decor but traditional furniture on bare boards. Music, but not intrusive. Patio seating area to one side. Impressive river views from the front windows and terrace. Initially just one, but eventually four of the six handpumps were operational on this visit: Doom Bar, Mosaic and some excellent Old from Adnams, and Nelson Revenge.
