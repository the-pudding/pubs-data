Quinno _ left this review about The Bee

At the top of the High St, overlooking the junction. Looks like it was a Brakspear pub a few years ago but no sign of this in evidence now. L-shaped interior, a knock-through of what once may have been quite a characterful multi-room pub. Made up inside to a decent standard, the best place to bag a seat is the small booth with a pair of old pews facing each other. Three ales over four pumps; Brakspear Bitter, Wychwood Hobgoblin and an unexpectedly leftfield offering of Revisionist Canadian Red (a little long in the tooth but tasty enough and at £1.75 a half, less than I expected). Just a couple of old boys in situ but the barmaid was friendly enough. Live music Saturdays.

On 6th May 2015 - rating: 6
