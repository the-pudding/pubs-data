E TA left this review about Windwhistle Inn

This rather scruffy 17th Century roadside inn is currently owned by St Austell Brewery. It sits in a superb location overlooking some of the Somerset Levels, and has a large car park, sometimes used by caravans as an overnight stop, and a sizeable garden with spectacular views. Inside, there is a large square bar with a central servery, and a separate restaurant area at the side as well as a conservatory at the back. There is also a separate function room. The atmosphere would be very well served by the traditional décor, open stone fireplaces and exposed timbers, but it is let down by the lack of welcome from the staff and general air of neglect. The menu was interesting and original, though given that the menu cards were laminated, not changing very often. It is also expensive; the dishes are overpriced and the beer is around 15% more expensive than in other pubs in the area. Ales on were Bath Gem, Tribute and Liquid Sunshine, reasonably well kept but not enough to justify the price. It wouldn’t be my first choice of venue.
