Aqualung . left this review about The Gateway (JD Wetherspoon)

This is an original pub being a fairly typical large main road suburban pub. This means that unusually for a Spoons it has a car park. Inside it consists of various areas set around a central bar. It seemed almost gentrified compared to most town centre JDWs but the service seemed rather shambolic.
The bar only has eight hand pumps which had two unused, Ruddles, Conwy Clogwyn Gold & Beachcomber, a house Gateway from Brightside, Ticketybrew Jasmine Green Tea and Beartwon Bear Faced. I went for the Clogwyn Gold (£2.49) which wasn't too clear and didn't taste great. Despite being a posher Spoons than normal I thought it was poor.

ROB Camra left this review about The Gateway (JD Wetherspoon)

A good looking JDW that was originally a pub before being taken over. This means that it has several areas that obviously used to be different rooms. There are models of tightrope walkers over the bar which is unusual. Sadly the staff were useless and couldn't serve in order even though I told them what the order was. Breakfast menus still on the tables at 15:15 on a Friday. Lots of dirty tables with plates etc on them. My pint of Roosters Fort Smith was in good order though. A good looking pub let down by laziness.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Gateway (JD Wetherspoon)

Proper inter-war roadhouse pub on the A34, erected before the surrounding area became built up but now conveniently located from the East Didsbury entertainment complex. Given the usual Wetherspoon treatment inside, but the various seating / dining areas around the central bar retain a reasonable sense of what an imposing place this must have originally been. Reasonable selection from a row of eight handpumps, with Naylor's North proving a decent accompaniment to a meal.
