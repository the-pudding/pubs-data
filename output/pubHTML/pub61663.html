Quinno _ left this review about Broad Street Tavern

A revisit last week. Some idiot has painted the wood panelling grey. Guest beer of Hooky Gold which was fine but the price was eye-watering.

October 2013
My first visit in a post Clive Wallis-world (after 15 years he’s moved on). Five Waddies beers on the bar including Swordfish and Farmers Glory (£1.85 a half). My Farmers was ok - no great shakes, perhaps nearing the barrel bottom. A real cider (Westons) also available, a rarity for the town. The interior is still a multi-roomed wood-panelled warren at the front though the rear area has had a refurb and is rather nicer and more punter-friendly than previous. The decked beer garden is still Wokingham’s best. Free WiFi available and what looked like a working sit-down retro arcade machine. The big screen was switched off and there was no background music. I do like this pub and I hope that Clive’s high standards are maintained. Rated 7

July 2009
Continues to serve a cracking pint of Wadworth (plus guest - York Brewery's Centurions Ghost on Sunday) but it's getting a bit expensive, even for Wokingham. You won't get much (if any) change from a tenner for three random pints. Rated 8

July 2006
Still doing a good job here, usually five Wadworth ales on plus Amstell and Chimay in bottles if that's your bag. Nice big garden out the back, with a bar for a busy Friday or Saturday. Also has a table service on when it's busy so you can avoid the arduous trek to the (admittedly stupidly small) bar, which is fab. Rated 8

September 2005
A bit like a TARDIS - a lot bigger when you get in than it looks from the outside though very awkwardly shaped, especially by the bar/toilet entrance. There's a great beer garden out back. Hosts a good range of ale and though one of them was on the turn the guy behind the bar immediately went to change it when I told him, so credit there. Definitely one of the best in Wokingham at the moment. Rated 7

Ian Mapp left this review about Broad Street Tavern

2nd of the Good Beer Guide entries in town. Nice walk through some architecturally pretty streets. In fact, Wokingham is very pretty indeed.

This is a greene king pub but they do offer LocALE brews and I went for a never seen before Twyford Tipple, after being offered an approving sample.

Decent enough pint but the head instantly disappeared, leaving it a touch lifeless.
