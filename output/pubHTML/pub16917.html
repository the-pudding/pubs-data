E TA left this review about The Woolpack Inn

Reflecting the wool trade with nearby Sheppey, the Woolpack is a busy village pub,the only one in the village. It's a large Georgian building which has been modernised and knocked through to form a pub and restaurant. There is a small but well-kept garden reached through the restaurant area and a decent sized car park to the rear. Only one ale on, Bombardier, which was not that well kept. OK to pop in if passing for lunch or a swift half if visiting, and it's the last pub before leaving the mainland (important to know if you're cycling or walking to the Island), but I wouldn't make an evening of it.
