Quinno _ left this review about Jolly Farmer

Paid a revisit last week and pleased to report that little has changed here. It’s a nice little place, well-appointed overlooking the village green and church, a quintessentially English scene. A three-roomed quiet pub, purchased by the community in 1987. The rustic main bar is for drinking, with character provided by a large fireplace and horse brasses. There are rooms either side of this for dining. Traditionally furnished throughout. There’s a garden area off to the right with apple trees. This continues down the side of the pub and opens up to a larger grassed area to the rear which also boasts a kids play area. Five pumps with four on - Rebellion IPA, Rebellion Smuggler, TT Landlord and Courage Best with Harveys Best turned. Old Rosie cider also on. I went for the Smuggler and enjoyed a good half. Staff were friendly and efficient and there was already a decent trade beginning to build up in the early afternoon. A place to be cherished and emulated.

August 2008
Lovely rustic village pub (owned by the village in fact), boasting five ales, all of which were fairly mainstream - could do with a little more imagination on that front. The three I had were served in good condition. No electronic distractions, so a great place to go for good conversation and a couple of ales. Rated 7
