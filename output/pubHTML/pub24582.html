Rex Rattus left this review about Lost & Co

Having walked past this place on numerous occasions, in a moment of abandon I decided to enter. There's no real ale, but a board to the right of the counter advertised the "craft" beers on tap, and included among them was Longhammer IPA, which I had only previous had in bottles. A half of it set me back £3, but it is a 6%+ beer, and the barmaid went to some effort to get a full half in the glass. Paulaner was another of the offerings. Food seems to be mainly pizze, most at £11.50 a pop, but with the "This little piggy" (fresh fig, goat's cheese, chicken, rosemary with a balsamic glaze) at £12.50.

This is a modern bar, and nothing like my idea of a pub. There are backless benches in the front area, with cheap tables and small round stools. There's a bit of banquette seating at the back, plus the inevitable tall tables/tall stools. The walls are varied in their appearance - an exposed brick wall at the back had copper piping all over it, as did the ceiling at the back. Another wall was covering in reclaimed timber, and a wall at the front had loads of desk drawers affixed to it. All a bit Antic. Quirky or ridiculous? - take your pick. It's not really what I'm looking for in a pub, but I guess that I'm not the demographic that they're aiming at.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Lost & Co

Part cocktail bar / part craft beer pub, with a refurbished gastro-pub interior that has been one opened out at some stage. Mixed seating, with one wall decorated but otherwise quite plain. Large folding front windows, open on a warm summer evening. Background music. Nine anonymous taps on the bar back, so you need to consult the handwritten list (which indicated that only four of these were actually on). Also has two handpumps on the counter, dispensing 'Madog's Ale' (from Purple Moose, £4.00) and something unstated from the Late Knight Brewery.
