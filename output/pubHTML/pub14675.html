Komakino . left this review about Saracens Head

I can attest to Thucky's review of 6 years ago, as this is still an agreeable spot on the drive-thru village of Kings Langley. Two Tring ales in the form of Ridgeway and Side Pocket For A Toad, along with St Austell Tribute and Sharp's Doom Bar (the latter's clip reversed - Oh The Humanity!). Weathered bar stools, felt-cushioned banquettes, representations of Saracens' heads on the bar eaves and bottled versions of Tring's Veritable Tea Kettle Stout gets a nod from me, and this is well worth a stop of you can grab a space on the busy High Street. There's also some limited seating out front if it's nice. The only gripe was the radio station-supplied muzak, which limits you to whatever the output is during your visit (even if I continue to confuse the intro to that Elastica tune with Wire's 'Three Girl Rhumba').

Thuck Phat left this review about Saracens Head

A traditional, inviting, single room boozer, carpeted and with comfy seats. Staff are friendly and the whole place has a warm atmosphere.
The beers are all similar in style, trad. brown bitters, but are well kept and served. Tring Ridgeway, Fullers London Pride and ESB and Adnams are on offer. Thoroughly deserves its GBG entry in an area not especially well served for real ale but with four handpumps on maybe a broader range of styles would be possible.
Definitely the pick for beer in Kings Langley.

Bucking Fastard left this review about Saracens Head

Whenever I am passing through KL ,this is the pub I pop into.You step down through the front door to an open plan lounge with a low ceiling.The decoration is traditional , with some interesting features and there are tables arranged for either drinking or eating.The bar is towards the rear of this single room and there is well kept Pride, ESB,Tring Rigeway and a guest on handpump Adnams Bitter on my last visit.There is a small(terrestrial) tv by the bar which is unobtrusive and may be switched on at the request of customers.Good quality bar snacks and pub grub are available at lunchtime with a traditional sunday lunch and this is an adults only pub(over 14) which helps maintains the calm atmosphere.There is always a warm welcome from behind the bar ,and the locals are also a chatty bunch.Has been a GBG regular for many years and this is thoroughly deserved.The fact that the pub was busy on a wet Monday evening is a testament in itself.
