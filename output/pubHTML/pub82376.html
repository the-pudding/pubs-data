Real Ale Ray left this review about Shepherds' Hall Ale House

A decent micro pub this one across from the town centre's church. The pub had a relaxed atmosphere with music played low. The interior consisted of light grey painted walls and sage green painted timber work, which included the original picture rail. The bar also had a painted timber front and a tiled counter top. This micro for its size had ample seating, consisting of upholstered bench seating in the window bay, along with low tables and chairs and six bar stools around the bar. Six ales on handpump, with an average price of £2.90. We went for the Brightside Brewery Odin which was excellent.

Aqualung . left this review about Shepherds' Hall Ale House

This is a micropub that will have been going three years this summer. It's a typical one consisting of a smallish rectangular room with a wooden floor, low tables benches and stools and a few bits of taller furniture at the front. There are some pictures on the walls and the obligatory collection of pump clips.
The small bar counter has five hand pumps which had RAT Mother Rat & Ratmospheric, Phoenix Navvy, Brightside Amarillo and Blackedge Pike. I went for the Mother Rat (£3.00) which was excellent.
This is Chorley's first micropub and a good one. It's a town that now has four and somewhere I would more than happily return to when I would revisit this GBG 2017 listed micropub.

On 30th January 2017 - rating: 8
