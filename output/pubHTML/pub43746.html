Quinno _ left this review about Ship & Castle

The refurb has fully bedded-in now and it was a superb stop-off here, a great atmosphere and all three of my beers were great shape. There’s also a selection of proper ciders and an increasing amount of craft keg (in Aber!!). Good to see Blairo still behind the bar as well. Still a top pub in my book.

June 2014
Real ale and cider house. Usually has five handpulls on the go, dispensing a number of unusual brews from Wales and the Midlands (Wye Valley Brewery is a regular) in excellent condition. There's a large range of whiskies and foreign beers, as well as at least one real cider or perry. Refurbishment a few years back has been effective. Gets rammed on Friday and Saturday after 8pm, so best sampled when quieter. The only must-visit pub in Aber. Rated 9

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Ship & Castle

The High Street is a bit of a backwater these days, but a number of venerable establishments (such as this) remain. That said, the interior of this pub has been comprehensively refurbished and now comprises a 'L'-shaped bar with a pool table just to the side of the entrance and a further seating area down a couple of steps at the back. Six handpumps on the counter, offering a cider and an interesting selection of beers (including two from Wye Valley and the Tettnanger Deutsch Ale from Harbour Brewing).

Will Larter left this review about Ship & Castle

We arrived here just before opening time, so had to take a little stroll. On our return, we found ourselves to be the first customers of the day (at 4pm). There are five ales and a cider on the six hand pumps, and we went for the Spitting Feathers Special Ale, which was very good. We quite liked it here, but there was a radio DJ on the video screen (Steve Wright, FFS - is he still alive?) which rather spoiled things for me. If we hadn't been in the very busy and welcoming Glengower previous to our visit here, I might have agreed with the reviewer below, but now I think the Ship & Castle might have some catching up to do.

Date of visit: 20th December 2016
