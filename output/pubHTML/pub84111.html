Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Crafty Pint

With two rooms, separated by a couple of steps, this might be more of a mini-pub than a micro one (although very much of that ethos). Reasonable amount of seating, but very plain decor (although the gents toilet is notably well presented). Compact servery with six real ales on handpump - including Butty Bach, Enville Ginger and Salopian Hop Twister - plus four taps on the side wall dispensing ciders.

Real Ale Ray left this review about The Crafty Pint

A two roomed micro pub, which was once a former pet shop on the bar side and a curtain and blind shop on the left which is now a seating area, with a couple of toilets at the rear. The small and compact bar hosted five ceramic and brass handpumps. The bar had a solid oak top with a reclaimed timber bar front. The walls were painted in a cream colour and the woodwork was in white. A decent choice of five ales on our visit, Sarah Hughes Dark Ruby Mild, Oakham Inferno, Wye Valley Butty Back, which are their regulars. One changing guest from Fixed Wheel Brewery, which was Chain Reaction and their second guest was Titanic Magnum PI. We went for the Fixed Wheel Chain Reaction, along with a ham and tomato cob, both of which were superb.
