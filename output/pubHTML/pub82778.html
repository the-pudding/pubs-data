Komakino . left this review about The Black Horse

A GK pub a little in the sticks which serves as both a local and an eatery with separate bar areas accessed via doors upon entry. The drinking bar is the larger and more spacious of the two, including pool tables, dartboard and TV screens. The narrower eating room is served by its own bar and has two black leather sofas upon entry with wooden tables and chairs all set out for dining (not that any were occupied at all on my visit). Just the one ale available - Morland Original - at £2 for a half, with a second unclipped and a half glass over the handle and a third (looked to be OSH) reversed. Exposed stone in the front bar, and exposed brick in the drinkers' den, meaning the latter may possible have been an extension at one stage. Not great, and one to give a miss to in this area.

On 19th November 2017 - rating: 4
