Aqualung . left this review about The White Lion

I can clearly remember this one being a Banks's house and in fact stayed here for a night many years ago. I don't know when Marston's sold it to Punch but given the new owners my expectations were low. You enter into the main area which is a single old beamed open plan room split into areas. The bar counter is over to the far left and a huge TV screen was turned off. There is a further area tucked around the back. It may be misleading to describe it as a hotel bar or food led as it's very much a normal town pub used by locals and visitors alike. On my February Wednesday afternoon visit it wasn't busy but there was an assorted crowd in.
The bar has six hand pumps which were all in use and had Cwrw Llyn Crys Coch, Sandsptone Tap Tackle, Marstons Bombardier Gold, Theakston Double Cross IPA, Cwrw Cader Cwrw Coch and Big Bog Hinkypunk. I went for the Crys Coch and the Cwrw Coch (both £3.20) which were both in excellent condition. I later spotted a single unused pump which is used for cider in the summer months.
I thought this was the best pub of five visited in Mach (The White Horse is a late opener) and goes to show that Punch and Enterprise houses don't have to be poor.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The White Lion

Popular food-led pub and hotel with an impressive façade and a porticoed entrance. Opened out inside to leave fair-sized bar and dining areas separated by a single step, but also retaining another smaller room at the back. Semi-traditional furniture and decor, with a few benches out front and a beer garden at the back of the car park. Seven handpumps on the counter, offering a good range of six beers plus a cider. None seemed particularly local, so I went for the J Buckley Original Best (which I'm guessing does at least come from Wales).

Beer Belly left this review about The White Lion

Has gradually become the most popular pub in town both with the locals old and young, the CAT(Shit & Wind) crowd, newcomers and visitors. And offers something for all. A long term Bar Manager possibly the longest in Mach has ensured that standards are kept high after a very short term hiccup on the food side shortly after re-opening 2,5 years ago.
It offers a rare porter and good selection of Real Ale if a bit too biased towards the light hoppy types for the time of year, from North Wales and Mach's staple bitter Banks. The food is very good and reasonably priced. If your lucky you may even get a fire side seat.
Some diners may get upset with the massive TV in the bar area but this is generally left for sports events.
A cobbled entrance and a long walk to the rear garden could be an issue.
And where has the White Lion gone from the porch?
