ROB Camra left this review about Thomas's

Well described by OB below, so I won't bother except to say that there's yet another room if you go down to the lower level then up a few steps to the right. Bombardier, two beers from Wentworth and Sarah Hughes Ruby Mild available on our visit. The Wentworth Milestone was in good nick, the staff were friendly and fun. The staff T-Shirts all say TOY on them which stands for Thomas's Of York. There's a sign outside that says "Come In And Play With Our TOYs". :-)) We'll definitely call in again soon.

Old Boots left this review about Thomas's

A bit of a mad pub decor wise, the elegant Palladian frontage has a faux Victorian interior as you enter then a lower level beyond that and a dining room upstairs. The floor has coloured lines hospital style and a sign saying if you want a drink go left if you want to eat take a seat. The counter in the top section has two pulls and the lower one has three, most of the beers were from Wentworth and in good nick when I called in. The decor is eclectic or slightly mad depending on your viewpoint, with a big Union Jack painted on the ceiling of the lower section, display cases of stuff, traffic lights and walls papered with odd posters and handbills. There are a couple of busts of Queen Victoria and a number of defaced Mona Lisas. Equally daft is the furniture, a mix of everything you've ever seen in a pub , I wrote this review sat on a leather Chesterfield with my pint resting on a table made of an old pallet cut in half and painted black. The exuberant barmaid tried to serve me with a handled jug but as she hadn't seen Get Carter it was a wasted moment.
