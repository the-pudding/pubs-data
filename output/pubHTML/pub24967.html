Rex Rattus left this review about The Shakespeare

This is one of those atractive looking pubs from the outside, with its original Truman's signage, that entices one to enter. I was a little surprised to see two real ales available - London Pride (£1.60 a half), and Greene King Old Spooky Hen, which must be some halloween themed beer based on their other ale with a very similar name. There was no sign of any food on Tuesday lunchtime.

This is small single-room bar; bare-boarded; counter by the left hand wall past the dartboard; and seating is banquettes and some normal tables and chairs. So far, so good. There was an old jukebox by the wall on the right, but am unsure whether it was responsible for the unwelcome music blaring out. There was just me and one other chap in there chatting to the landlady, and I can't see how the loud music would have aided the quality of their conversation. But maybe they prefer it that way.

Obviously it's very hard to form an overall opinion of a pub when it's so quiet (figuratively, that is) in there, but it has the look of a locals' drinking pub to me. It was a lot better than the Misty Moon just down the road, and the London Pride was in reasonable shape as well. It's fine for what it is, but it's certainly not really worth going out of your way to visit.
