Komakino . left this review about The Carriers Arms

Situated on a one-way street off the town centre, this is a pleasingly ungentrified country pub almost at the foot of the Chiltern Hills which offered four average ales, including Mitchell & Butler "X1" for a bargain £2.50 a pint. A touch on the 'local' side (the Fat Fox is upmarket and the Chequers too far from the centre), Radio 2 playing loudly through a TV set seemed unnecessary, but a smallish beer garden with a great view of the hills means this isn't quite the lost cause it makes itself out to be and the cavernous space behind the bar is a bit of an oddity.

At the foot of Watlington Hill with Red Kites circling overhead. A small friendly pub (freehouse) serving simple at old fashioned prices. Would like it as my local. Ideal for a break if walking in the area or exploring this interesting little town.
