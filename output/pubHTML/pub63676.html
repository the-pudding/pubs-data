peter ashworth left this review about The Queens

Built in 1825 and later turned into a beerhouse in 1837 to commemorate the ascension to the throne of Queen Victoria,the Queens Arms has a prominent position at the top of Manor Park Road.
After trudging the lengthy walk in inclement weather from the town centre, was slightly nonplussed to find all 3 pubs closed although i suppose it was a little early at 11-30am. The Queens did open around 11-45am and there was a good selection of 5 cask beers on including Thwaites Wainwright and Doom Bar, but i opted for Adnams Ghost Ship which was a fair old pint.

Alan Winfield left this review about The Queens

The Queens is a three storey stone built pub that is on the edge of the old town area of Glossop.
It looked like the pub was a Bass house from the outside but once inside there was a decent choice of beers on the bar,the pub was quite nice inside and was the sort of pub i could have stayed in longer.
There were four real ales on here i had a drink of Black Sheep bitter and this was a very nice well kept pint the other beers were Adnams bitter, Youngs bitter and Bombadier.
This was quite a nice pub.
