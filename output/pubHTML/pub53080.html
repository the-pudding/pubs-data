Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Duke of York

Appealing Fuller's pub that has been substantially refurbished in an upmarket yet traditional beerhouse style. Not a lot of space around the bar, but there are some small tables out in the pedestrianised area and York's dining room at first-floor level. Six handpumps on the counter; with Pride, Seafarers, ESB and the seasonal Old Winter Ale (4.40) plus Proper Job as a guest and Cornish Orchards vintage cider available on this occasion. Also has ten keg taps to the rear or the bar dispensing a good selection of craft beers.

Malden man left this review about Duke Of York

A rare old school pub in The Pantiles area, boarded floor, distressed boarded ceiling although the gnarled beams and posts looked to be affectations. Some wood panelling also looked to be deliberately worn but the wonky floor must be real. The words of the old rhyme "The Grand Old Duke of York" are on display as is a shelf of old red suited lead toy soldiers. A roaring fire helped the ambience on a chilly day, there were stacks of logs all around.
Beers from Fullers, Pride, ESB, Seafarers, Oliver's Island plus guesting Hooky Bitter and a cider from Cornish Orchards. Various "nights" advertised, curry, steaks, open mic, not my thing I just prefer the quiet comfy pub of an afternoon which is what I found. A decent spot and a grand drop of ESB.

On 27th February 2016 - rating: 7
