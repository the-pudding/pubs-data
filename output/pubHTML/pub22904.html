Graham Coombs left this review about The Comedy

A large establishment, spread over three floors. The dark interior of the main bar gives it a slightly clubby feel, while downstairs there is a plasticky 'cocktail bar', and upstairs a sports bar. Several TV screens were showing a selection of sports, and that was just the main bar - didn't venture upstairs. Draught beers on offer were Doom Bar, London Pride, Trooper and Whitstable Bay and seemed to be in fair condition, albeit pricey even for the West End.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Comedy

Slightly odd sort of place, with the ground-floor pub sandwiched between the basement bar (often used for live music) and the upstairs lounge and comedy club. The place seems to have been refurbished since my last visit some four years ago, with smarter dark-wood furniture but retaining the pictures of famous performers on the walls. All four handpumps were working this time too, with Pride, Doom Bar, Whitstable Bay and the seasonal Bread of Heaven from Brains (costly at £4.35) available. Overall, something of an improvement.

On 5th February 2015 - rating: 6
