Real Ale Ray left this review about NauticAles

As Aqualung mentions there is a LOOP bus that passes the door, or if you decide to walk up from Ramsgate train station it is a good 20 min walk up a hill, and obviously quicker on the way back. Whatpub states that this micro opens at 4pm mid week, good job I phoned their mobile, as it opens at 5pm. There was a choice of three ales on stillage in a chiller along the back wall and also a choice of 17 ciders. The three ales on our visit was Ramsgate Citra, Gadds No 5 and Four Candles Maiden Voyage. I went for the Maiden Voyage, which was a good beer, but served far too cold, which might have been alright on warm day, but not on a cold and wet November evening.

Aqualung . left this review about NauticAles

This is a newer micropub dating from Spring 2016 set in a Northern suburb of Ramsgate. The useful LOOP bus passes by. It's a proper micropub (no music or TVs) consisting of a small L shaped room with a laminate floor. It's bright and clean and there is a mix of low and high furnishings. There is a small bar but beers and ciders are all stored in a chill cabinet behind it. This one produces their own beers at the Four Candles brewery not far from here.
There were three beers on my visit, NauticAles Green Hop & Maiden Voyage and Dark Star Hophead. I got here just in time for the 50p discount so the Green Hop was £2.80 while the other two were £3.30. All were in good nick. I didn't try any of the numerous ciders.
This is another good micropub.

On 9th October 2017 - rating: 8
