Real Ale Ray left this review about The Vine

This pub has an excellent example of Victorian glazed tiles on the exterior, which also continued into the corner entrance lobby. There was some stained glass above the door frame which was also original. Another unusual feature was the bar counter top that was constructed from a solid piece of Scott's pine. This heavy piece of timber had a bull nose edge and a lip on the top to stop any glasses or bottles from sliding off the bar, which was quite unique and I haven't seen anything like that elsewhere. Four handpumps on our visit, one of which was a cider. The Cotswold Shagweaver was the regular and it was in good shape. The barmaid who was on duty wasn't friendly and didn't engage in any conversation, even though we were the only ones in at 4pm, when they opened. The Thai food theme mostly prevailed in here, but if you're going to serve three beers and act as a pub, at least try and make an effort with visiting drinkers.
