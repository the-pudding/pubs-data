Reviews of Toby Carvery Hilsea (Average Rating: 6 of 10) Add Review see review guidelines

E TA left this review about Toby Carvery Hilsea

A modern timber-framed barn-like structure dating from the 1990s with a central brick chimney, previously called The Green Farm after the farm that used to be here. There is a large car park at the side and a small, tidy paved garden area only really useful for smoking. The interior is high-ceilinged and has a lot of cobwebbed structural timbers on display, failing to give that authentic rustic feel. The young staff were pleasant, the clientele were fairly civilised and the food was as good as could be expected given the quality of the ingredients, well cooked and well presented. There was annoying muzak, gaming machines and mute TV screens to distract us. Ales on were Tribute and Doombar, both in OK condition. It is good at being what it is.

Graham Coombs left this review about Toby Carvery Hilsea

Typical family dining establishment in a pseudo-medieval barn with Travelodge next door. I suppose there are worse places to drink at and it fulfils a need. Draught ales were Doom Bar, London Pride and St Austell Tribute but the condition was questionable, despite Cask Marque accreditation.
