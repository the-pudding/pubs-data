E TA left this review about Swan Inn

A roadside inn which offers B&B, it has a riverside garden with its own private fishing licence, although the two are separated by the A36, so you take your life and your beer in your hands when crossing. There is also a large car park, this too on the other side of the road from the pub. The building, which has 17th Century origins and was substantially changed in Victorian times, has been reorganised to provide a hotel foyer at one end, while the original door has been closed to prevent customers walking straight into the road. There is a skittle alley which doubles as a function room. The staff were welcoming, but the atmosphere was a bit dead. Food is the usual average restaurant food, reasonably priced and well cooked. Two ales on – I had a pint of Butcombe,s which was in fair condition. Worth stopping if passing, and I’d be happy to stay here for the night.
