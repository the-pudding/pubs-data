Quinno _ left this review about The Lord Harrowby

Traditional two room local tucked away in a back street. Victorian style public bar to left with 2x dart boards. Lounge to the left which is carpeted with padded bench seating, a coal fire and sporting a tiled bar top. Look out for the stars of the 80’s signed and framed photos above the bar. Five ales over the two bars with local ales and a blackboard with colour jars. My Oldershaw Blonde (excellent) River Head Butterley Bitter (very good) Long Man Old Ale (superb) and Jurassic Bitter (good). A friendly, mature clientele in situ. We really enjoyed our time here and stayed for longer than planned. It’s easily Grantham’s best pub in my opinion and one of the best in the county I'd wager.

On 15th December 2017 - rating: 9

Real Ale Ray left this review about The Lord Harrowby

Opens at 3pm Mon-Fri and is a 10 min walk from the train station. This pub is worth a visit as the landlady is friendly and chatty as well as the locals. It is a two room pub with the bar side quite 70's looking. The lounge side is more mordern in appearance. Five ales on handpump, Sharps Doom Bar is there reg, two from Oldershaws, a Wells and Tom Wood Best Bitter.
