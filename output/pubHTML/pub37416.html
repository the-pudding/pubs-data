Aqualung . left this review about The New Inn

This is a smallish Holden's pub situated a few yards from the main road between Wolverhampton and Birmingham. Inside it is a long and narrow single room with a mix of tiled and carpeted flooring with the rear area more suited to eating. The bar is centrally located and was selling Holden's Mild, Bitter, Golden Glow and a seasonal beer. I went for the Golden Glow (£2.80) which was in good nick. This place was somewhat more upmarket than some of the Holden's houses visited earlier in the day but seemed a bit run of the mill to me.

Danny O'Revey left this review about The New Inn

A real mish mash of interior design here well described by Mr Dragon below, artex, modern brick, iron fire place, fish tank, brasses, an old hand pull on the wall, you name it! Opened up into one room with a modern pine bar extending well into the room in the middle, seating either end. Its all a little eclectical, but works well in this Holdens street local just off the main Brummie New Road and not too far from Coseley Railway Station.

Friendly, the elderly lady behind the bar seems to move with the speed of a 20-year old!

Its a Holdens pub, selling their mild, bitter, Golden Glow and a guest. Despite being a Black Country institution Holdens is not my favourite beer, however, here it was I think the best ever pint I have ever had of Holdens bitter and was very drinkable and very appreciated.

On 3rd September 2011 - rating: 8
