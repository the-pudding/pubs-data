Aqualung . left this review about The Star Inn (JD Wetherspoon)

Honiton's JDW is an original pub and quite obviously so looking at it from the main road. Inside the original pub has been taken up with low tables and chairs and the bar is in what looks like a modern extension at the rear. Although open plan it is split into smaller areas. The whole place has a low ceiling and seems to have been completely modernised.
The bar has ten hand pumps which had the JDW Trio Of Doom with the GK beers doubled, two ciders, Otter Bright, Dartmoor Jail Ale and Bays Devon Dumpling. I went for the Devon Dumpling (£2.15) which was in good nick. I also went for the Fish Friday (£6.49) which is cheaper than average here despite other food clubs being slightly dearer.
I thought this was OK for a small town Spoons.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Star Inn (JD Wetherspoon)

Claims on the façade to be "possibly oldest pub in Honiton circa 1800 and the centre of pigeon racing in the 1960s", but much extended and completely refurbished in a semi-modern style. Disappointingly, only five real ales were available from a total of ten handpumps, and only the Exmoor Dark (£1.05, half) was of much interest. Still, doing good business on a Saturday morning...
