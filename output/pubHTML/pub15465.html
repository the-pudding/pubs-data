Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Kings Head

Solid three-storey building in the centre of this small town, built in 1870 and still operating as a inn (albeit now a new-fangled 'Kitchen + Bar + Rooms' establishment). As you would expect from the new description, the interior has been completely remodelled and refurbished in a mix of faux-rustic and contemporary styles. The main space now comprises a 'L'-shaped arrangement with a compact bar and partially opened-out side dining area at the front (with piped music, but a very welcome real fire) and the main dining room stretching to the back. Also has a few small metal tables out front and some chunky wooden benches in the covered side alleyway (that you surprisingly - in a very 19th-century way - still have to follow to reach the gents toilets). Two Shepherd Neame ales on handpump: Spitfire Gold and Whitstable Bay Pale (£4.10). Fairly busy with diners on a Sunday lunchtime, but only one other customer just having a pint.
