Will Larter left this review about Kilton Inn

This is a community pub, being far enough from the town centre to attract people from the local area rather than shoppers or out-of-towners with time to kill before a bus. The original layout of smaller rooms is long since gone, of course, but the present layout does make a fair attempt at areas with different character, and overall it is smartly and comfortably furnished. The two hand pumps on the bar were dispensing Doom Bar and Old Rosie, and there were the usual fonts for non-real beers. A decent beer or two would make this one worth the walk from the town centre for the casual real ale drinker, though I imagine that most people in the community who use the place must be quite happy with it as it is.

Alan Winfield left this review about Kilton Inn

The Kilton Inn is about a 5 minute walk from the centre of Worksop and i thought it was well worth the walk.
Once inside the bar was facing,there was a very smart looking carpeted area to the left this had comfortable bench seating there was a larger area to the right which was bare boarded and had a pool table,the whole pub seemed very smart and well kept.
There were no brewery signs outside but i thought the pub could be an Everards tied house as there were two of their beers on the bar and all the beermats were Everards.
There were two real ales on here i had a drink of Everards Tiger bitter and this was a very nice drink the other beer was Everards Sunchaser.
The pub was fairly busy late Saturday afternoon and i found it very comfortable with a nice laid back atmosphere.
Overall a pleasant pub to have a few drinks in.

Pub visited 2/6/2012
