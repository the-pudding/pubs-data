. Wittenden left this review about The Bull Inn

What a difference! The last and only time I visited this pub, at least fifteen years ago, it was dark and malevolent, a real fighting pub. A bit out of place in what seems to be a somewhat upstage village, former residents include Ian Dury and Joanna Lumley (not necessarily at the same time.) Now it is bright and open and a bit soulless. A traditional village building, the front door opens onto a long, somewhat cluttered bar giving onto all areas.
My fleeting visit was on a bright December Saturday lunchtime:a shooting party having enjoyed lunch had some problems with welly identification. Amongst the keg and lager fonts on the bar were two handpumps serving Harvey’s Sussex Best Bitter and Old Dairy’s Red Top, brewed just down the road. I didn’t try the Harvey’s, but the Old Dairy seemed in good condition.
The folk behind the bar said the new management had been there for about eight months and was developing their catering offering. They deserve to do well, and when the initial brightness tones in The Bull will be an asset for the area.
