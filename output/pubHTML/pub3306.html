Thuck Phat left this review about The Bridge Inn

The Bridge sits on the canal just outside the town centre, has been recently refurbished and has a sign facing the canal stating 'Home of Real Ale'.
This was enough to tempt me to put my head through the door but sadly I couldn't stay for a drink due to a total lack of funds but thought it worth scratching a note or two here as it has clearly so recently changed.
The building itself is large and Victorian, I'd guess. Inside, the bar is at the back - canal side - and the whole has been done out with wooden floors and tall tables and stools as well as some more conventional seating to the sides.
Outside there is a newly decked which looks onto the canal.
I spotted 5 handpumps which offered TT Landlord and Tribute along with a couple of beers whose clips I didn't recognise - they weren't nationals or large regionals but without any cash I didn't get a closer look.
The place had a few people in on Sunday afternoon and if I'm ever passing again and actually have some money then I'll happily stop in for a beer.

On 19th September 2013 - no rating submitted
