NIGEL WILLIAMS left this review about The Bell Inn

The bell Inn has been managed over the last year by temporary Managers, naturally this will reflect in the experience you receive during that period. There are New Tenants going in on the 3rd February 2015, Nigel & Andrea will be writing the next chapter in the life of the pub.

Nigel & Andrea want to preserve that Traditional Country Pub feel but with a modern twist, Community pubs are such wonderful places to visit with diversity of clients and lots going on, Live Music, Darts, Popular sports shown, Great Ales, Lagers & Wines.

The new chef will be creating a completely different menu as you would expect, a lot of research has gone into the types of food that we think the public who visit us would love. We expect the restaurant to be back open by the 5th February.

We have a new website for you to visit www.bearstedbell.co.uk

Steve C left this review about The Bell Inn

The Bell Inn felt like it had just opened when I visited just after 15:00 last Friday afternoon. The bar area is found at the front of the pub where there is some seating to the right and a dartboard to the left. There was some music playing off the plasma screen and I didn’t notice any live sport advertised. There is a seating area at the rear of the pub but the lights were out and the curtains drawn. There is a back door which I imagine leads to some sort of beer garden, but again this was out of bounds.
The bar is stocked with standard draught and supports three hand pumps. One of these was unused, but a cash bag was over the nozzle so I imagine that I just happened to wander in when it wasn’t drawing anything. The other two hand pumps were drawing Greene King IPA and Thwaites’ Wainwright. There is also a cocktail and hot drinks menu and food is served with the mains coming in between £8 and £10.

I found the surroundings to be comfortable enough and the governor was very friendly, but I wouldn’t go out of my way to drink here again.
