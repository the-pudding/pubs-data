E TA left this review about Fox & Goose

A roadside country pub which now caters mainly for diners and is family-friendly. There is a good sized car park at the side, and a large garden outside with a children's play area, complete with swings and climbing frames. The two old bars have been knocked through to form a single bar/dining area which has a rather tired carpet on the floor. There is also a sizable entrance porch with a piano in it. The décor is traditional 1980s, the food good and plentiful, and there are three ales on. I had Plain Ales's The Bitter Wife, which was most enjoyable and very well kept. Worth stopping off for lunch or a few beers in the evening.
