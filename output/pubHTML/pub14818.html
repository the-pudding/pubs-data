Alan Winfield left this review about The Cock

The Cock is a corner pub that is in a good location in the middle of St Albans.
Once inside the bar faces in an L shaped room,the floor is part tiled and bare boarded,there are tall tables and stools to the front left and bench seating and chairs plus some bucket chairs on the right side.
There is an oblong shaped dining room to the rear left.
There were five real ales on the bar,i had a drink of Trumans Runner,which was a very nice drink,the other real ales were St Austell Liquid Sunshine,Hop Back Summer Lightening,GK Abbot and GK St Edmunds.
The TVs were showing horse racing and the pub was very busy on my Saturday afternoon visit.
I thought this was a decent enough pub to have a drink in.

Pub visited 24/6/2017

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Cock Inn

Semi-modernised old inn - retaining two main rooms; a compact main bar at the front (with a further small lounge to one side), supplemented by another part of the counter in the larger seating / dining area further back. Background music. Implausible claim that "we have the largest range of ales in town" although there was a decent selection from the six handpumps: three Greene King beers - IPA, Abbot and Old Speckled Hen - along with the World Hop Medley collaboration with Hopback Brewery (£3.90) plus Wandle and Sadler's Worcester Sorcerer as guests.

Alesonly . left this review about The Cock Inn

This was one of the Pubs we went in on yesterdays Pub Crawl as it was one of the last it was very busy late evening. There are around three or four Ales on Tap But the bar area is far too small with everyone standing around it. But Its still as the last poster said at busy times there is nowhere to stand Or sit down and have a pint yet the bottom Bar was totally empty and all the tables were set for food. In my mind this is just laziness as they could easy clear half these tables in the evenings allowing drinkers to sit down for a beer and gain easer access to getting served at the bar. I'm sure if They done this they would get more customers in the evenings as we were barely able to get in the pub so had one quick Pint and left. Had we been able too sit down we would probably stayed till closing time.
