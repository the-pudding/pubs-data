Old Boots left this review about Brewhouse

Small bar owned by brewery Naylors from near Keighley, opposite the Town Hall. The half round counter has four pulls, two with Naylors own brews plus two guests, Phipps NBC in this case with a good collection of clips on the mesh above the taps, three nice German kegs from an interesting fount as well. As it's brwery run the beer quality is excellent. Shelf and high stools along the large clear front window with a couple of tables and chairs/stools, settles and opposite the counter is a booth. Pies, cakes and coffee are also available along with a crafty / foreign fridgefull. Good soundtrack for old gits like me was playing (Teenage Kicks among others).

Real Ale Ray left this review about Brewhouse

A one roomed micro/café bar, with four handpumps, serving two ales from Naylors and two from Empire. Their line up was Naylors Gold, Naylors Yorkshire, Empire Brewery Blonde and Empire Moonraker Mild. We went for the Naylors Yorkshire and Gold and both were in superb condition and priced at £3 a pint. The interior was traditional in décor with painted red and green woodchip papered walls a wood effect lino flooring and a rustic pine bar area. Seating areas consisted of a drinking shelf along the front window, with a row of barstools and mobile phone charging ports. A low table and chairs next to the bar and two booths over on the right and a raised area at the rear of the bar. The pub was moderately busy on our Wednesday lunch time visit, with a mix of beer drinkers and coffee drinkers. Food was in the form of bar snacks, pork pies, sausage rolls and scotch eggs.
