Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Black Horse

Rather bigger inside than the traditional village local exterior would suggest, with what would appear to be a three-part extension to the rear. The front part has been partially opened-out but retains the impression of two small rooms under the original low ceiling.. Behind this you find what is now an island bar, after which the headroom increases as you navigate through the tables laid up for dining to the rear beer garden and it's green marquee. There is also a separate dining room in an outbuilding connected to the main structure by a linking corridor. Four real ales on handpump: a 'house' beer (source unknown), Pride, Tribute and the inevitable (but tasty) Harvey's (£4.20).

On 25th June 2017 - rating: 7
