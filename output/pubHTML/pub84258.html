Ian Mapp left this review about 1000 Trades

A bar/restaurant for the Rock and Roll Brewery. Thin, long pub with area separate area for drinkers and diners. Twin DJ Decks hint at the entertainment that might be on during a weekend.

Beers are detailed on a chalk board and the Rock and Roll Bitter was perfect.

Gill Smith left this review about 1000 Trades

I liked this place. We were made welcome by friendly staff, and the beer choice was good as Signature Brew had a sort of tap takeover with their beers on both cask and keg. We tried 2 of their cask beers, Nightliner which was a good porter, and the Roadie. The front had been opened up onto the street so 2 tables were facing outside. Weather perfect for it on Saturday. There was a lot of artwork for sale on the walls in the rear area of the bar. There is also a range of bottles and cans in a fridge behind the bar. and an interesting tapas menu.

Real Ale Ray left this review about 1000 Trades

A long and narrow bar with the main bar area over on the right as you enter. The staff in here were welcoming and helpful when choosing a beer and to hand out a couple of samples was no problem. There were four handpulls, with three in use serving a house beer called Best Beta, which is brewed locally by Rock and Roll Brewhouse. Also on was Great Heck "Dave" and Summer Wine Brewery Resistance, which was a dark mild. They also had a good range of bottled and canned beers, so I went for a can of Magic Rock High Wire, as I was requiring a grapefruit hit. They also had a choice of six beers on key keg.
