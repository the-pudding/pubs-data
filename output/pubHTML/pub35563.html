Graham Coombs left this review about Porter & Sorter

Transformed from a small corner boozer by the addition of a very large extension. The latter is fairly comfortable and traditionally furnished, while the old pub side has some garish club-type tables, as well as a pool table hidden round the corner. There is a patio outside. Beers are from the wider Marston's range, such as Pedigree, Ringwood 49er, Hobgoblin and Brakspears.

Lester Ford left this review about Porter & Sorter

A late Saturday morning visit to this large Marston's pub which is just a stones throw from East Croydon station.

This is yet another pub that I have walked & driven past many times over the years but have never ventured inside. Back in the day I understand it used to be a bit of a rough hole, but now it’s been refurbished and is quite attractive inside as well as having an outside area with a smoking shelter.

11.30am and the pub was pretty empty save for a group of 3 lads, and a few couples, however with the “manager” busy making coffee (with froth on top!) the young bar maid was quite slow and was doing everything one at a time, quite annoying when you have waited over 10 minutes just for a pint!

Being a Marston’s pub I knew that the choice would be national ales but I expected the 6 hand pumps to have more than just the 1 beer on which was Ringwood’s Razor Back @ 3.8%, thankfully it was drinkable and worth the wait.

This is a large pub with the space used well in my opinion, the largest part of the pub is full of tables and has several booths for diner’s, I guess the pub get’s busy at lunchtimes and weekends with diners.

Several large tv’s were showing the BBC news and from looking at the chalkboards all manner of sports.

Decent enough for a quick pint but I do wish these places had more than 1 spare staff member, as even at 11.30 on a Saturday pubs do get busy!

On 4th May 2017 - rating: 6
