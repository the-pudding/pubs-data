Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The All Nations Inn

Finally managed to call in at this 'famous four' brew-house following a visit to the Blists Hill Victorian Town museum on the opposite side of the road (once the branch line to Coalport, and still crossed by a footbridge). Could easily be mistaken for a cottage from a distance, with only the customers sat out in the sunshine on the patio terrace giving the game away. Plainly-furnished interior, looking like it had been sensitively refurbished fairly recently (except for the outside toilets, which remain resolutely old-school). No sign of Shire's on the five handpumps, but a 'house' ale from Broughs of Wolverhampton was supplemented by Hobson's Twisted Spire, Weetwood Oast House, Rowton Dark Side Stout (£3.00)and Rosie's Pig cider. With its friendly welcome, traditional charm and important place in brewery history, this really is one of those 'must visit' pubs.

Aqualung . left this review about The All Nations Inn

It's not far off forty years since my single visit to this pub. Back then it was run by a Mrs Lewis who produced a single beer. This was a "light mild" that to be honest was nothing to write home about especially compared to the offerings of the other three brew pubs at the time. What I had forgotten was the fact that it's such a great unspoilt pub or is it the case that so many pubs have been spoiled?
It is situated just outside the Southern edge of Madeley perched on the hillside above the Coalport Road as it starts to plummet into the Severn Gorge. The building is accessed by a fairly steep stepped path.
It's a single room with a central doorway and seating areas on the left and right with the bar in front of you. The floor is made up of red and black tiles rather than wooden floorboards or a carpet which makes a change. Both the Ladies and Gents toilets are situated outside through the garden which must cause great merriment in extreme weather.
The bar had a bank of four handpumps and a single extra one with Rosie's Pig Cider. Sadly there was no sign of the on-site Shire's Brewery offerings. The beers were Goff's Merlin, Rawton Comet, Hobson's 3 Spires and Dickensian Nicholas Nicklebeer. I went for the Goff's Merlin and the Dickensian beer, both at £2.80 and both in excellent condition.
This is a superb unspoilt pub and well worth the effort of finding.

Soup Dragon left this review about The All Nations Inn

This is a detached little Georgian cottage styled pub perched on a hill with an extensive beer garden around it. A GBG entry, it is in white painted brick, with black door and window paintwork. The pub has nice sash windows with rusticated lintels, the keystone of one of which has a date of 1782 on it. There is also a nice porch and a dog-tooth moulded cornice.

The interior is a single room straight room running along the width of the building, with the serving bar facing you on entry. The decor is a beigey colour, with a beige and beamed ceiling. The fact that pub was once two smaller rooms was shown by one half having a tile floor, while the other was wood, Further, there are a couple of fireplaces, one at each end of the pub. There is a lot of Victorian prints and old photographs of the pub on the walls, supplying interest. The service was very friendly - the landlord interested in which beer i preferred and explaining why the MILD was no longer on. The clientele (and it was busy) seemed mainly local and mainly in rugby shirts watching the 6 Nations. There was no music and normally, so the landlord said, no TV - it being here just for the day.

Beer; usual tap stuff with Shire's Dabley Ale and Gold, Cottage Brewery's Between the Sticks and Morton's Essington Scrummage. All were in tip top condition and enjoyable. I was forced to try them all as Mrs Soup insisted on a pot of tea instead of a cup - the sacrifices I make!

Class. Quality. Afre a day at Blist's Hill opposite, cross the road and come to this place - just make sure you are not driving! Mrs Soup can be hired for a pot of tea.
