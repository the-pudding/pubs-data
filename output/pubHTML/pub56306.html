Aqualung . left this review about The Glass House (JD Wetherspoon)

The Glass House was originally an indoor market. It consists of a curved corner room with a further room for dining down some steps. It was doing a good trade on my Thursday midday visit.
Despite the fifteen hand pumps here the beer choice was dismal being no better than the Lloyds where expectations are low. on the first two sets of five were four unused, the JDW Trio Of Doom, Coach House Blonde, a beer from the not so recent festival and Robinson's Magnum IPA. The last five had Abbot, Doom Bore and three ciders.
I went for the Magnum IPA which was totally uninspiring. This one was a complete waste of time.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Glass House (JD Wetherspoon)

Busy 'spoons on the edge of the main shopping centre, with a curved frontage and a generally 'L'-shaped bar within. Fairly plainly furnished and decorated in a more modern style than most. Nine of ten handpumps in operation, with a pretty decent selection of real ales - including three from Dunscar, and others from Cheshire Brew Brothers and Mordue - and my pint of Heritage Ales Victorian Porter was good value at just £2.15.
