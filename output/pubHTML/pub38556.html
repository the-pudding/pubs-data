Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Drawbridge

Large, estate-type Greene King pub with Flaming Grill branding, recently reopened after a refurbishment and notably busy early on a Wednesday evening. Not sure what it was like previously, but the various seating (and notionally dining) areas now have a light semi-modern look. Also has an area with a popular pool table and a dartboard off to one side. Five real ales offered from the two banks of handpumps: IPA, Belhaven Elephant session ale, the seasonal Ale Fresco and Un-Beer-Leveable plus Landlord as a guest.

Bucking Fastard left this review about The Drawbridge

A modern build Flaming Grill pub on the site of a former off licence,it has the feel of an estate pub yet sits alongside the Stratford on Avon Canal towpath with fine views of boat crews operating the drawbridge.There is a concrete compound surrounded by a metal fence which acts as the exterior drinking and smoking area,while the interior is large,open plan and winds around an L shed bar.To one side there are dining type tables running along the windows overlooking the canal,opposite the bar there are high chairs,then a series of banquettes before a side restaurant section with no bar access.It's tidy but the Flaming Grill theme is never far away,and a glance at the menu shows a super value lunch option of two courses for £4.99,quality unknown,and a main menu of insanely large portion dishes topped off by The Trash Can Challenge at £19.99 served in what passes for a dustbin lid to accomodate the mound of food.Quite a few of the lunchtime customers looked like loyal customers of the chain.
At least there was real ale with a choice of Doom Bar and a good Everards Tiger served by friendly,chatty and quite cheeky barmaids which lifted the spirits.
Fine for a couple of pints,the food offering did bring to mind the fate of Mr Creosote ,so easy on the wafer thin mints.

On 17th June 2015 - rating: 7
