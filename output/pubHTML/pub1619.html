moby duck left this review about The Duke of Wellington

A decent two room traditional pub, a few blackened oak beams and uprights reinforce the general feel of the pub. Three beers on, Brakespear Bitter which I imagine is a permanent fixture, also on were Ringwood Boondoggle and Jennings Hop Commotion , the latter being in fair condition but not a particularly good beer to my taste. A pleasant pub but not one I would go out of my way to revisit.

Quinno _ left this review about The Duke of Wellington

A Brakspear-owned local, which feels small and homely despite there being two rooms and a 'dip' area. Three ales from the Marstons list are available which are usually in fair shape, though I've had the odd poor one outside the Brakspear Ordinary. Prices are steep though. The people behind the bar are a usually friendly lot and the punters are usually a good mix of people. Three TV's hook in via separate feeds so sports fans can watch multiple games at once. Some of the interior had a refurb a while back but a fair amount retains an air of being stationed somewhere in the late 70's. It's a nice little place.
