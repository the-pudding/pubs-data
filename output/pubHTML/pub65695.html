Paul Mallett left this review about Blackwell Ox Inn

My wife and I, who were on holiday in the area, had read about the Blackwell Ox Inn on the internet, and as we both like Thai food decided to give it a try. First impressions were good. The pub, which is clean and well maintained on the outside, is situated in the middle of a very well-maintained, attractive village. We were a little surprised to find the carpark busy, even at 7am, though there is a caravan park attached to the back of the carpark. Once inside the pub, however, we found that all the tables in the main dining area were taken. We found a side-room to the front of the building with a couple of tables free. This room was grubby, and the tables still dirty and covered in grease from previous diners, and there were boxes of toys dumped on the floor. The service was poor and unfriendly, and the staff unwelcoming: they gave the impression of not being at all bothered by our arrival. The food (Pad Thai) when it came, was greasy. We have had much nicer Thai meals elsewhere. We ate, then left the continuous stream of noisy diners to have a coffee back at our holiday cottage. The whole evening was a big disappointment. If you're looking for a cheap meal, with absolutely no frills attached, then this may be the place for you. But if you're hoping for a well-cooked, tasty meal, served in a quiet romantic atmosphere, then go elsewhere! We will certainly not want to go there again. Ever.

On 20th May 2010 - rating: 1
