paulof horsham left this review about The Three Moles

Don't know if it had been a busy weekend, but our bike-assisted visit found only 2 ales on offer; the Bowman was in great nick, though, while the Betty Stoggs went untouched by us. Barman's a friendly sort and customers came and went regularly, despite the rural location, so it's clearly got its admirers. Seats out the front are a great place to sit on a warm afternoon. I'll see about a repeat visit next time there's a sunny day and I'm in the vicinity.

Graham Coombs left this review about The Three Moles

Gorgeous, unspoilt and possibly slightly eccentric country pub. Has some excellent (and different) ales and has started doing a (very) limited range of food - enough to keep you going if you are drinking, but not to go for a meal at. Recommended, so long as someone else is doing the driving!

Quinno _ left this review about The Three Moles

A fantastic, unique and cosy pub in the middle of Sticksville, our beer bus visit from the suburbia of North Surry visit found five handpulls on the go, featuring four diverse choices (Skinners Betty Stoggs and three guests), all served in tip-top condition. The pub is set in a picturesque woodland location and the two rooms features a real fire, bar billiards, plus a friendly pub cat. And a lot of plastic moles. There’s no screens, food or machines. Landlady Val is a legend, she was completely unfazed by 20 thirsty drinkers falling out of the bus, took us all in good humour and was happy to chat and answer questions. She would've had a throwing arm thicker than Steve Backley’s after all the pints she served up.

An excellent establishment and definitely worth seeking out for a session

[EDIT – landlady Val has retired since this review was posted – I have yet to re-visit]
