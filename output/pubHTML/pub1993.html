Reviews of The Ship Inn (Average Rating: 7½ of 10) Add Review see review guidelines

Quinno _ left this review about The Ship Inn

As me and my companion were attempting to accomplish ‘the full Wokingham’, it was time to pop back in here after my self-imposed exile. Things seem to have taken a turn upwards, the place feels a lot cleaner and looked-after. A full line-up of Fullers ales on, my Red Fox was beer of the day. Got chatting to the never knowingly understated landlord, who is obviously pleased with the job he has done here. So perhaps it’s time to put this one back on the regular visit list. 7.5

August 2014
Against my better judgement popped in again the other day. Plenty of ales on from the Fullers range plus a guest from Butcombe which was so-so. A pint of that and a Peroni set me back just short of nine quid which is a disgrace even by Wokingham's laughably high prices. Starting to get a bit shabby, the jumbrellas out the back were torn. I doubt I'll be back unless something significant happens. Rated 6

October 2013
A tale of woe yesterday. Initially promising, with five of the Fullers range (Pride, HSB, ESB, Red Fox and another one-off I forgot to note). My Red Fox was £1.90 and was a fair half, though the taste became a bit sour when I caught the barmaid topping-up some of the ales from a glass that had been acting as a drip-tray. Little change inside, two rooms with a rustic feel and easily the busiest pub I visited. But the poor bar staff mean I won't be back until things have changed – is this practice condoned by Fullers I wonder? Knocked off a point. Rated 6

September 2010
A Fuller’s pub situated at the far end of Wokingham town centre, opposite the church. It’s a two-roomed affair, with a central and a corridor leading out to a tidy concreted beer garden. There’s a real fire in winter, Sports TV and a dartboard (rarely used in my experience). The clientèle tend towards a younger slant than a lot of other pubs in the town. The beer is usually three of the Fuller’s standards (Pride, Discovery and ESB) along with a seasonal and occasionally, guest. The quality is usually pretty good, though prices are eye-watering, even for Wokingham. This was real corker of pub when Brian and Emma were in charge – they were the best landlord/landlady combo I’ve ever experienced. They’ve long since moved onto to bigger things and the place doesn’t quite have the ‘must-visit’ feel any more as they added that extra ‘something’ but it’s still a good bet if you’re in town. Rated 7

23 August 2005
Excellent Fuller's pub. Friendly staff, with a cracking quiz on a Sunday. Loads of atmosphere with a well-kept Fuller's ale range, decent food and mixed clientele. Best proper pub in Wokingham without a doubt. Bryan and Emma are doing a great job here. Rated 9
