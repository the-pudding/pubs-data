Malden man left this review about The Kings Arms

A lovely pub, up a side turning off what town centre Arundel has, it actually looked closed from the front but general access is to the side where a buzzy bar area is found with locals engaging with the fat of the day and a bit of banter. This side room seemed on my afternoon visit to be the main area, carpeted, ceiling beams with dangling pewter tankards, pump clips and photos on the walls with the lower area wainscoted, large brick inglenook fireplace has horse brasses affixed. Beyond a rear area is up a brick step, leading to the facilities and a nice garden beyond. Another outdoor area sits to the side. Papers in a rack and there were several piles of games to choose from. Various events advertised, including a quiz and an ale club with brewery showcasing nights.
They don't do food but there are takeaway menus and you can order and the pub provides the crockery etc.
The beer range was Hammerpot Bottle Wreck Porter, Kissingate Chennai IPA, Pride plus one not clipped.
A proper old pub with atmosphere and good ale, my only gripe was the sign about a mobile phone policy. They seemingly have some sort of fine system based on if you make or receive a call, I'm sorry but the second part of that is not controllable, they would have got short shrift should the issue have occurred, no one likes a phone brayer but this is 2015 and everyone has one. A sign saying please go outside would be rather more polite. Ironically, they had commercial radio playing. Down a mark for that. Edit: Do you have to pop outside to phone for your takeaway?

JohnWarks 'tache left this review about The Kings Arms

Oldest pub in Arundel, so the sign says, and well worth a visit. Kind of a games area at the front with dartboard, jukebox and various board games scattered around. Back area more where your regulars gather round the bar and the impressive fire. Jukebox a little bit too dominating for our lunchtime visit, although if you liked your Dad's record collection (Pink Floyd, Status Quo, Meatloaf and the ilk), you'd have been well in. Seemed to ignore our selections when entered. Spent pleasant couple of hours playing darts and drinking Arundel Gold in this ever so slightly out of town pub, which was 10 times more of a pub than the other overly priced ones for the more pretentious tourists
