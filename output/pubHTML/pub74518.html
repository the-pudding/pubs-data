Graham Coombs left this review about Jubilee Garage

As its name suggest, converted from a garage with an esoteric mix of furnishings and decorated with a large quantity of automobilia giving a slight American feel to the place. I didn't visit the upstairs level, which apparently has a bar built from a VW campervan. On handpump were JHB, something from Millstone and a cider or two. Perhaps a bit too contrived but not unpleasant for a change and friendly enough.

Alan Winfield left this review about The Jubilee

The Jubilee is a newish pub that was converted from other use and it is almost opposite Smiths of Bourne.
Once inside there was one largish room and the bar was to the right,there was one very large long table in the middle of the room and this was the only table not laid out for diners.
The seating along the side of the pub was leather bench type seating,the big table in the middle had tall stools which were comfortable enough.
The pub was empty mid Saturday afternoon so i think it must cater for upmarket evening drinkers.
There were no real ales on here though you could see where the handpumps used to be,the only bitter was Tetley smooth crap so i had a Guinness.
This is Not really my sort of pub.
