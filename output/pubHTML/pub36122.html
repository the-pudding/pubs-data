Bucking Fastard left this review about The Fox & Duck

In such an affluent area ,it's a real surprise to find a working class boozer.It's housed in a interwar years brick building.There is a lounge to the right side,but it's basic and a small corner stage offers occasional live music.To the left is the pool room with a darts board , fruit machines and this is where the regulars gather.The flatscreen will show Sky or BT Sport events.
The bar has a single handpump which looked quite new and was clipped with Robinsons Pint of Thrones.I was informed that this wasn't working,nor was a keg tap for Kronenbourg 1666 but "an engineer has been called".This left me ordering a half of Heineken £2.50.
There are seats outside by the busy road but this seemed the best option.On my return visit to the bar to take back my glass ,the barmaid said "See you later" rather more in hope than expectation.It's highly unlikely that she will be proved right.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Fox & Duck

This brick-built roadhouse comes a quite a surprise in above-affluent Petersham, turning out to be quite a basic two-room local. The gloomy bar has been refurbished at some stage with a limited amount of semi-modern furniture (including a couple of tables up a pointless step. The pool room is brighter, busier and louder, but neither room has much in the way of decor. Also has a few tables out on the front patio if you want to watch the traffic crawl by. A solitary handpump was supposed to be dispensing Black Sheep Holy Grail, but this was temporarily off so forced into having a John Smith's (£3.85) since there are no other hostelries in the vicinity.
