Malden man left this review about The Duke's Head

An old building with a low beamed ceiling, now opened out into one space with a central bar. It isn't too difficult to picture the original layout, and separate entrances remain. There is a pool table in a side area and a dartboard in what I'd regard as the main body of the pub. To the rear is a more modern open vaulted ceilinged extension. Several TVs dotted about the place were showing football with volume on Sunday afternoon, I felt this to be overly intrusive despite liking football as I had no real interest in the game and there was nowhere inside to avoid it.
Mostly traditional seating at standard tables but there was the odd sofa here and there. Four handpumps on the bar, one unused, the choice was Pride, Landlord and Hop Back Summer Lightning which was in good shape but expensive at £3.65 but I suppose it is 5%.
There wasn't much of note in the way of decor and although the beer condition and choice were acceptable this pub lacks something and the manner in which it has been altered detracts from the history that clearly lurks beneath the surface.
