Graham Coombs left this review about White Horse Inn

Hidden down a passageway leading to a courtyard, a splendidly unspoilt town pub with an old-fashioned bar area including a little snug at the end and a separate sitting room across a passageway. Round the back is a garden area while the front courtyard is also under an awning during the summer. Draught ales are from Evan Evans with just Cwrw and Blonde on offer at time of visit, but the latter was very drinkable.

Aqualung . left this review about White Horse Inn

I had a slight bit of trouble finding this pub as it's tucked away behind an arch with just one of those old Bass Charrington black plastic boxes from over forty years ago giving the game away. Through the arch is a courtyard with some tables and chairs then the pub behind.
Inside the bar is on the right and there is a separate carpeted lounge or snug on the left. The main bar has a large old fireplace. On my Wednesday afternoon visit there were a few locals in.
The bar has six hand pumps only five of which were in use. These had Evan Evans BB, Cwrw, Warrior, Partisan Snapper and a guest Porter Street Oval 6. I tried the BB (£2.50), Cwrw (£2.75), Partisan Snapper (£2.85) and Oval 6 (£2.85). The Cwrw was hazy but the rest were fine.
This is the Evan Evans brewery tap and is a fine old pub. The brewery is somewhere over near the station apparently.

On 25th June 2015 - rating: 7
