Graham Coombs left this review about The Bridge House (JD Wetherspoon)

A new build pub adjacent to a small retail development off the High Street, alongside the river. The large main room has rather dark décor and not a lot of atmosphere, while down a couple of steps is another room with river views. At the side is a small balcony with a few tables overlooking the river. The front has a strange portico affair, with a smoking area beneath and a surprise room above which is a nice corner to escape to if you don't mind carrying your beer upstairs. The draught range was Doom Bar, Abbot, Marstons Old Empire and Saltaire Blonde along with three ciders.

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Bridge House (JD Wetherspoon)

Slightly unusual 'spoons, with the small 'flying' first-floor extension above the entrance and the decking terrace right by the river. However, although a 'new build', the interior is surprisingly formulaic and fails to take full advantage of its situation. Five real ales (including Exmoor Gold, £2.05) and two ciders available from the ground-floor bar.

E TA left this review about The Bridge House (JD Wetherspoon)

A pleasant, modern riverside pub,open and airy, well described by Aqualung. To complement his description we ventured upstairs only to find a small seating area and the toilets. Six ales on draft: Devon Dew, Doombar, Ruddles, Abbott, Bristol Belle and Harvey's Double Gold, all in excellent condition. Staff were very friendly and helpful. One of the better 'Spoons, worth a visit if in town.
