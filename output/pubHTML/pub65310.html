Reviews of Kro Bar (Average Rating: 6½ of 10) Add Review see review guidelines

Rob Hunter left this review about Kro Bar

Visited mid December on a Saturday night. This is the last of the Kro bars and is vastly different to the one that used to be at Piccadilly. There is a bit of a strange atmosphere perhaps resulting from the big high ceilinged building which just reminded me of a dentist's waiting room. Bare board stairs lead to the upstairs area where the toilets are located and a bit of random seating on the landing.

In terms of the beer offering, cask ale and cider were available but we went for Everything's Peachy by ShinDigger, which was on keg.

Not unbearable, but a bit of an odd experience. I don't think the place suits the kind of building it is in.

ROB Camra left this review about Kro Bar

This was originally the Manchester Temperance building rather ironically. Two rooms, on behind another leading out to a partially covered beer yard at the rear. There are further seats at the front of the pub. Only two ales available, but my pint of Tweed Hopster was in good nick. The barmaid initially claimed that she didn't know anything about the CAMRA discount stated on Whatpub, but then gave me 20p off my pint anyway. This made my pint £2.70 which is great value for around here. Not sure about this place, it has a bit of a strange atmosphere.

On 6th October 2015 - rating: 6

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Kro Bar

Unlike the other Kro outlets I've visited, this one is housed in a traditional building (a converted mansion of some sort). The bar is located in quite a small ground floor room, supplemented by two upstairs lounges, a covered yard area out back and a large patio seating area at the front. The menu looks quite good, if pricey, but the service left something to be desired. Four of the five handpumps were in operation, with the usual Robinsons offering (Dizzy Blonde on this occasion) plus Allgates California (£3.00) and a couple more from other small breweries.

On 21st September 2011 - rating: 6
