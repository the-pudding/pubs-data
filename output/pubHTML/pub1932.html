Quinno _ left this review about The Greyhound

A straightforward locals boozer in the backstreets that appears from history elsewhere to have been re-opened in 2012 after a period of misrule and closure. Single room interior with a few jugs on shelves and an enclosed darts area to the right. Teeny but decent outside area. Football on the telly and a pub dog for company. Three ales; Doom Bar, Pride and (happily) Windsor & Eton Garter (drank well). Skittle alley advertised. Noted a Monday menu discount for pensioners of 20%. Unlike the Shepherds Hut, this place had a number of customers. Not a bad little pub, I’d use it if I were local. It also overlooks the some fields so you can bovine-spot before or after your pint.

On 22nd April 2015 - rating: 7
