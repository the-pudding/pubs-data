Quinno _ left this review about Victoria Cross

Refurbed and now called the Victoria Cross rather than the Trooper Potts - presumably because the Greene King suits didn't approve of the 'Pooper Trotts' nickname coined by the locals.

September 2016
Reading’s newest pub – a large new-build aircraft hanger Greene King outlet and named in honour of the town's only Victoria Cross holder. The main large left front facing bar is for the cheap, deep-fried no-frills family dining experience and has an upper mezzanine level affording breathtaking views of the car park the Basingstoke Road. To the right through a door is a second smaller (but still sizeable ) over 18s bar with TV sports. Five pumps, four on; GK IPA, OSH, Abbot and Robinson Dizzy Blonde, the latter of which was gratifyingly cheap for Reading at just over 3 quid a pint and drank well enough. Punters less challenging than expected. Overall, it's better than I expected though I doubt I'll feel the need to revisit any time soon.
