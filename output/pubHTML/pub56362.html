Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about Greville Arms

Substantial Sizzling pubco outlet, set deep in suburbia but looking more like a 1930s roadhouse. Various seating / dining areas around and off to the side of the central servery, all with the expected anonymous semi-modern furniture and decor. Doing surprisingly good food business early on a Wednesday evening. Doom Bar and Purity Pure UBU (£3.30) available from two of three handpumps.

Soup Dragon left this review about Greville Arms

A large, detached Sizzling pubco place, in brick and white render, with beer patio

The interior is the generic open plan foody kind of thing, with multi-coloured walls; purple, blue, olive, white, red, French mustardy etc. The menu is cheap and the food functional, i having a burger thing. Not much in the way of decoration, apart from a few mirrors and vases and the background music is general. Service was good, the clientele, as ever for this kind of place, mixed and lots of them.

Beer; tap stuff, with an M&B MILD that was too cold even for keg. On handpull there was a so-so Hook Norton Old Hooky, as well as the untried Spitfire and Spekky Hen.

I have visited worse pubcos than Sizzling. This place does what it says on the tin, nothing more.
