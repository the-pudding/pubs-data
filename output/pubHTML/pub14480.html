Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Great Northern

Refurbished pub opposite the railway station, with semi-modern furniture and light decor (that particularly helps towards the back where the few windows are very small). Small patio at the front and a part re-built 'garden' out back. Two real ales available from the four handpumps: Adnams Ghostship (£3.40) and Old Golden Hen. Some of the other customers were on the boisterous side, but otherwise OK for a quick stop.

Bucking Fastard left this review about The Hatfield Arms

The new name has coincided with an internal refurb and real ale is now served.The interior is open plan with a wooden floor throughout and several small flatscreens dotted about.The front section has a real fire,a corner high chair unit by the front window and a fruit machine.Beyond the modest bar there is a darts board proving popular among the locals.Furniture is modern but not out of place in a pub and there is a large smoking shack to the side of the building.In the evening this is a wet led pub.
Like so many pubs which are the closest to a railway station,this pub attracts the kind of customers unwelcome elsewhere ,much fruity language was on offer to which the barmaid was a fair contributor.My presence as a stranger was noted,and there was little in the way of friendly ambiance.
Besides the usual keg offerings there were four handpumps offering GK Abbott,Doom Bar and St Austell Tribute (£3.40) in good nick,the fourth being unused.The Cask Marque accreditation seemed justified.
If you like laddish behaviour,or 4 Jagerbombs for £10 then this place will suit,for me it was only ever going to be a refuge for a quick one.
