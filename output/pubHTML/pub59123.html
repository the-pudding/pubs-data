Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Somerhill

Previously known as the Hooden Horse, this corner pub has a wedge-shaped bar and a larger square room to the rear housing a pool table and used for live music events. Fairly limited furniture and basic decor. Some wooden benches out front, a small decking terrace at one side and a basic beer garden to the rear. Just Harveys (£3.50) available from the two handpumps on my latest visit, with a reversed Pride clip noted on the other, both as my previous one two years ago. Remains all a bit disappointing.

Steve C left this review about The Somerhill

The interior of the Somerhill consists of a bar in the right hand side and a pool table and dartboard to the left. On the right wall is an electronic dart scorer next to a large mirror which I imagine hides another dartboard. Plasma screens are located throughout for showing live sport and there was some background music playing during my visit on a recent Friday evening. Standard draught is available from the bar and the Pride was off leaving Harvey’s best as the lone cask ale option.

Outside the front of the pub are a couple of picnic tables and there is a beer garden at the rear. I did not venture out there, but I did hear some young children playing on a trampoline as the sun set.

I found the service to be polite, but I think that I will leave this boozer to the locals next time I am in the area.

On 22nd June 2015 - rating: 5
