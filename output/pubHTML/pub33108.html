E TA left this review about The Globe Inn

A recently renovated market pub, formerly a coaching inn, with a main L-shaped bar and a smaller public bar and games room to one side. There is a small car park at the back, but it was impossible to get a car in as some moron had blocked it by parking a builder's van in the entrance. The bar is part-tiled, part carpeted, and the one remaining original feature is an inglenook fireplace. The games room has a dartboard and pool table, but there was no-one using either despite the pub's being busy. It was noisy due to the lack of control exercised over noisy children. The landlady was far from welcoming, a pity as this could be a really good pub given its location and history. Four handpumps on the bar, 2 dry, one serving Butcombe's Bitter and one GK IPA. Not somewhere I'd currently make an effort to revisit.

Brew Guru left this review about The Globe Inn

Beers on were Butcombe, Dartmoor and Tribute, with not a great deal of quality. Pretty basic boozer in Somerton, was one of the busier pubs in these dark cold depths of January. The menu looks OK if a bit uninspiring, if you like puddings you got a freee one with each main course. Average.
