Aqualung . left this review about The Full House (JD Wetherspoon)

This was an Odeon Cinema, probably one of the last ones to be built as it was started in 1959. Before becoming a Spoons in Summer 1998 it had become a bingo hall.
As would be expected with a cinema conversion it is a huge space inside with a bar taking up the lower area below where the big screen would have been.
This bar had 18 pumps which I didn't have time to note down as I was in a hurry to get back to London before the worst of the rush hour kicked in. I do remember there being a reasonable selection of ales. I went for a Locale beer Haresfoot Lock Keeper (£2.05) which was in excellent nick. This was the cheapest Spoons of the day and rather more typical of the London ones I'm used to. I would think it would be well worth a look at beer festival time. It's a shame that the centre of Hemel Hempstead is so far from the station.

Clive Thompson left this review about The Full House (JD Wetherspoon)

Small entrance that opens out into a large drinlking area with the bar and a few more tables down a few steps. There is a small raised 'library' area to the left as you enter with bookshelves and a quieter atmosphere. Large mural of what is perhaps supposed to be some sort of Roman/Greek vista behind the bar. The 'beer garden' is a paved smokers area with a few tables cordoned off from the main road by a low wall and some pot plants. Decent range of ales and ciders.
