Soup Dragon left this review about The Swan Inn

This is a detached free-house pub, in the GBG, with a beer garden at the rear. The pub is Victorian, but has a touch of the Georgian too. It is in white render, with black paintwork around the unsympathetically restored windows and on the cornerstones. There is a pilastered doorway and a front gable with finial. It looks tidy, as it does inside. The interior now has a single open plan room (the former rooms being ripped out), which is part partitioned to make a dart area. The place is generally in cream and white, with some blue carpet and some wood flooring. There are a few mixed pictures and a bottle collection, as well as an old kitchen range. There was no food. The TV wasn't on and no music. The service was friendly and a mixed clientele - busy, but mainly local it seemed. Beer; usual tap stuff with Saltaire Raspberry Blonde and Bavarian Black (decent); Yate's Bitter and Landlord's Choice; Hawkeshead Brodie, Lakeland Gold, Windermere Pale and Cumbrian 5 Hop and a Phoenix White Tornado. A great pub, with quality beer. I could stay here all day.
