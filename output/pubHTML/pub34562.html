Soup Dragon left this review about Ox Leathers

A large, modern, low-level, Marston’s estate pub/eatery, in undressed brick with beer garden.

The interior has two rooms. There is a bar room, which like the exterior is in undressed brick. It has two pool tables, a dart board, a TV, with some film on and a few sporty cartoon/prints on the walls. There were a couple of people in. The lounge/dining area is large and in cream and wood panel with moulded cornice. It is on two levels. There is a basic menu, but I didn’t eat so I can’t comment on the food. There were a lot of landscape pictures, a TV with the same Bravo channel film and there was no music. There were a few locals in this room as well, but the place being so big it felt like there was nobody in. The service was fine.

Beer; usual tap stuff, with Banks’s Bitter, Hobgoblin and a decent Banks’s MILD on handpull.

In my view, a better than average estate pub and worth a visit. It has all the facilities from pub games to food.
