Nick Davies left this review about The Bell Inn

The Bell is an old 17th Century coaching inn on the edge of the rather dispersed village of Outwood. It was acquired by Fuller's in recent years and unnecessarily but reasonably tastefully refurbished. Even before Fuller's got hold of it it was largely given over to dining, and that remains much the same, with apart from an area to the front every level surface laid up for eating. If you can get over all of that they do attempt to make the front bit look pubby, with a nice fireplace and TV showing football, exposed beams and the usual decorative knick-knacks though even then few people seem to use it as a pub, and at weekends there are far too many loud 4x4 owning parents with their screaming kids.

There's nothing wrong with beer quality - it's in the GBG - a couple of the standard Fullers range and an ex-Gales guest usually. That the choice is limited is an indication that their priority is not the wet side.

Have a look if you're passing, but these days there are better choices round here.

On 13th June 2010 - rating: 6
