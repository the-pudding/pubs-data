Aqualung . left this review about The Prince Albert

Being a Craft Union (a front organisation for the Ei Group) pub I wasn't surprised to find that this place was a shrine to the God of TV Sport. It's a small L shaped room that on a Saturday evening had all the TVs going but at least people were actually watching it.
There are three hand pumps which had one unused, Sharps Cornish Coaster and London Pride. I went for the London Pride which was around £3.50 and in good condition. I doubt that I would return.

On 26th October 2018 - rating: 5

Just a quick pint, then I'm off left this review about The Prince Albert

Probably the most old-school boozer in the High Street, this Craft Union pub was doing good business with the locals on a sunny Saturday afternoon whereas the visitors were generally in the establishments nearer the beach. Faux-Truman lettering on the bar gantry, but not much else to say about the basic - if traditional - interior, and certainly nothing as interesting as the seemingly historic (but actually 1911) façade. Just Pride and Sharp's Coaster (£3.10) available from two of the three handpumps.
