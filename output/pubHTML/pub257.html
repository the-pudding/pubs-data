Blackthorn _ left this review about Black Horse

Tucked away down a small lane just off of the main road through this village, it’s not the easiest place to find and is unlikely to attract anything in the way of passing trade, but is nonetheless well worth making the effort to track down.

There’s a good sized car park at the rear, and if entering from here you come to an unheated flagstone corridor with the main bar off to the right. This is all together much cosier with a large stone fireplace just inside the doorway that had a log fire blazing away on our recent Sunday afternoon visit. A single chunky joist runs across the ceiling, and this had been painted black as had the supporting pillar and other timberwork, such as that around the windows. Flagstones lined much of the floor with along with some red quarry tiling, and the walls were cream plasterwork that were liberally adorned with plenty of black and white photographs of the pub, village and a number of portrait photographs including couple of The Wurzels. Seating was mostly wooden benches, and some of these could be quite a squeeze to get in to. A smaller snug around the corner was broadly similar in terms of décor, with plenty of wooden beams on the ceiling and a large collection of china jugs hanging from them. Other essential country pub accruements such as horse brasses and a collection of guns were all present and correct. Only the TV up in the corner looked somewhat out of place, but fortunately this was not in use.

Beers on tap were Otter Ale, Tribute, Bristol Beer Factory’s Quartet, Brains SA, Courage Best and Butcombe Original with the latter two being served directly from barrels racked up on stillage behind the bar. Ciders were well represented with Ashton Press (still and sparkling), Thatcher’s Haze, BBF North Street, Stan’s Traditional and Rich’s Farmhouse. All in all, a really great pub.

On 28th January 2019 - rating: 9

Quinno _ left this review about The Black Horse

Things tick nicely along here, though its becoming almost too popular now the moneyed Marina mob have discovered it. A good ale selection is maintained and it's usually a decent drop, if somewhat variable in temperature. Worth diverting off the nearby motorway for.

November 2010
A super timewarp country pub that takes some finding but is well-worthwhile. On entering, a corridor takes you through to the bar area which hosts a huge log fire (which has apparently been lit continuously for many years), flagstone floor, low beams and bare stone walls. Seating is wooden stools, settles and pews. To the rear there is more of a lounge area. Back out in the corridor there’s a little-used side room for particularly busy days. There are usually five real ales (often fairly mainstream choices like Courage/Butcombe/Shepherd Neame but they now have a guest, I saw Skinners in there the other day; also they stock Websters Green label?!?) and almost as many ciders, all from gravity. Ale can sometimes be rather cold in the winter but condition is usually pretty good. There’s some car parking round the back but it can be a bit of a squeeze. Food is available at lunchtimes only. Patrons are an interesting mix of locals, country yahs, bikers and pub explorers.

This is a pub I’d urge all readers to try once.

On 14th July 2012 - rating: 9
