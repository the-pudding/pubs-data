Andy Ven left this review about The Railway Inn

Open again after a closure of around a month while a few financial/tenancy issues were sorted out and after a successful Facebook campaign involving 542 supporters. Not much has changed about the place probably in the last 20 years though, which could be why this place remains popular with the people of Pelsall. It's a traditional 2 roomed local pub consisting of a bar and a lounge and a couple of benches at the front overlooking Pelsall Common. The service and the locals are friendly and welcoming (I frequent the lounge when I get the chance).

Besides the usual tap stuff there are 3 cask ales. One is usually GK Abbot. Last night the other two were Black Country Holden's Bitter and Holden's Golden Glow, priced at £2.85 per pint and today there was Wychwood Gingerbread and Adnam's Flame Runner.

Food is served between 12 and 2 and between 6pm and 8.30pm, Monday to Saturday. Food is served between noon and 6pm on a Sunday. Two main meals cost £7 and two starters or two desserts cost £4 - a bargain. I tend to go for the double sausage, double egg and chips.

There are lots of happy people now that the Railway has come back from the brink. I count myself among them.
