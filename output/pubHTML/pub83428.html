Will Larter left this review about St Andrews Brewing Co.

I approached this pub with optimism, and initial impressions were good. We got a friendly welcome and were plied with numerous tasters. There are two hand pumps, with a stout and a best bitter, but neither was from the house brewery. We explored further and tried some of their own beers, which in taster glasses seemed ok and we asked for a couple of halves. Ah, they only do pints and two-thirds, sorry. OK, then, so we had a couple of two-thirds - which cost £7.60, which averages out at £5.70 a pint! My beer was so cold I went back to the bar to ask for a smaller glass to pour it into, to give it a chance to approach room temperature - we had a bus to catch and I didn't want to drink it too quickly in case some of my fillings cracked and fell out.

I got into a discussion with the barman/manager who had been friendly and helpful, pointing out that I couldn't see the point of brewing their own beer and then serving it so cold that it could have been McEwan's 80/-. He said that was how people expected it to be, and I was obviously not used to it if I normally drank real ale. We remained on friendly terms and he gave us a big smile on the way out, but I couldn't seriously recommend this place unless you like your beer at the same temperature as your ice creams. And have fairly deep pockets.

On 24th September 2017 - rating: 5
