Real Ale Ray left this review about The Gamekeeper's

A well planned and thought out micro pub consisting of a one good sized rectangle shaped room with a small side area which acted as a small snug, on the left of entrance. The bar was situated in the back right hand corner, which had an oak counter top and a sage green panelled bar front. The walls were painted in a pale green colour and all of the walls had sage green painted panelling up to dado height. Another feature was the ceiling lamps and two lamps over the bar which were made up from imitation deer antlers. The flooring was herringbone patterned lino throughout, except for the snug area, which had a tartan patterned carpet. A log burning stove was positioned in the front seating area along the right hand wall.
Five ales on handpump, two from Black Iris, Snake Eyes and Endless Summer, Grasshopper Brewery Irish Stout, Magpie Best and Lenton Lane Citra Smash. The prices in here were also good all beers at £3 a pint.

Will Larter left this review about The Gamekeeper's

A shop conversion where the owner has clearly given a lot of thought as to how to make the best use of the available space. There are five hand pumps, with beers all from local breweries: Blue Monkey, Black Iris, Leatherbritches and Aurora. It was very quiet at the time of my visit, but on a midweek afternoon in November this was not entirely unexpected. There is a lot of competition in Eastwood, with several new or re-established bars and micropubs - I hope this one continues to be a success.

Date of visit: 30th November 2017
