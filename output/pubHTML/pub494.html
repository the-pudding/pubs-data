Blackthorn _ left this review about Colston Arms

Located at a handy point for a pit stop half way up the steep St Michael’s Hill, this is a bit of a cross between a traditional old pub and a sports bar. It consists of a couple of bars, as well as a small seating area at the front with some chunky wooden benches, and a larger patio at the rear.

Décor wise it’s quite traditional with the front bars having a dark wood floor, rough plasterwork on the walls and some contrasting maroon paintwork. There was also some exposed stone walling in places which is unusual in city centre pubs and a nice feature. The smaller rear bar was quite elegant with dark wood panelling on the lower half of the walls, green paintwork above and country portraits such as landscapes and horses on the walls. Unfortunately though, at least when I visited, a football match completely dominated with every area of the pub either having a large screen TV on the wall or a projector. I can imagine it would be quite a pleasant little pub on other occasions, but we just had a quick pint in the front garden and moved on.

There was a good choice of beers on tap with Doom Bar, Butcombe Original, Marston’s EPA, Robinson’s Trooper and Horn’s Diamond Geezer. Ciders meanwhile were Thatcher’s Gold and Aspall’s Suffolk.

On 25th May 2017 - rating: 6

Quinno _ left this review about Colston Arms

[Reviewed as Micawbers Ale House incarnation]
Known until a few years ago as the Colston Arms, this is a small and genial pub snuggled into the hillside. Three handpulls on the go (failed to note exactly what, but nothing too far out of the ordinary) and the place features a pool table. Was pretty dark inside, so be prepared for locals to pop out of the corners. Legend has it that this pub is where condemned men would sup their final pint before being hanged at the top of St Michael's Hill. Nowadays appears to be a shrine to Wales and Manchester United instead!

I thought it was decent stop and would put it on a crawl list for the area.

On 4th January 2007 - rating: 7
