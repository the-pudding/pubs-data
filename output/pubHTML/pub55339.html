Lorraine Scallywag left this review about Anchor Inn

Great friendly pub with charismatic landlord and landlady. Music friendly with great live acts and fab old fashioned rock n roll mixes from the resident team nightly. Huge food portions at great value with the Sunday bin lid a must, finish it if you dare! Was there on holiday for a weekend and had a really lovely welcoming time. Good mix of local residents and holidaymakers, set in a beautiful spot right on the river, just the chillout I needed. Oh yeh and girls, the talent is not bad either!

Old Boots left this review about Anchor Inn

Self styled community pub close to the Ripon canal, it certainly seems to have a good local following of people of all ages from the area of Langthorpe and Milby just over the river from Boroughbridge. The bar is an L shaped one with a pool table and seating in the shorter front section, while through an archway is the barcounter and a room with bench seating and a high table with chairs although not quite in the usual style. It's a pleasant enough room decorated with some prints and a model of Tweety Pie and Sylvester, dark wood and stained glass dividers round the dart board and a door with an interesting stained glass window leading out to the garden. There is also a profusion of audio-visual devices. Beerwise there are four handpumps, a solo one of which looks unused, the others serving Landlord, Black Sheep Bitter and John Smiths Cask in very reasonable condition, there are also a few founts of the well known kegs Strongbow, Guiness, JS Smooth, Fosters and 1664 as usual.
