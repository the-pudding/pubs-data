Al Bundy left this review about The Wire

A large, multi-roomed standalone Victorian pub. There are at least 4 rooms that I saw with a servery I two of them. 3 unused handpumps on each bar. Warrington rugby memorabilia dotted around. It has an attractive and traditional interior. No real ale.

Komakino . left this review about The Original Wire

Although not the closest in proximity to Warrington Wolves' ground, the name of the pub gives you an indication of the main focus here, and signs such as "Warrington Wolves Matchday Madness All Home Games" reinforce this. Internally, it's high-ceilinged and multi-roomed with a small bar centred around the entrance serving two main areas with smaller 'breakout' rooms on the left-hand side, with part-bookshelf wallpaper, upholstered banquettes and badly grammared, tired old alcoholic quotes e.g. "When I read about the evil's of drinking. I give up drinking." (and this has been spelt as read) Obviously a lot hotter on matchdays than on an October Monday evening, in terms of how drinking establishments can combine sport and alcohol, Leyton Orient Supporters' Club this ain't
