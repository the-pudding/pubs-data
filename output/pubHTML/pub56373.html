Graham Coombs left this review about Red Lion

An excellent traditional family-run small hotel in a picturesque village. The aged building has gradually expanded from humble origins and ranges from ancient low-ceilinged panelled rooms at the front to a modern function suite behind. The copper-topped bar is in the centre of the building and boasts a good selection of handpumps serving Yorkshire ales. Outside there is a large riverside patio. The menu is particularly splendid and very popular (booking recommended). The sort of place you would be happy to be snowed in at over Christmas.

Andy Ven left this review about Red Lion

Nicely situated next to the river this pub had the feel of a manor house come pub/restaurant/hotel. The bar area where we sat had a few taxidermy items. It was probably the one room where the tables and chairs looked a little tired. The dining room and the barn-like function room looked quite smart. The patio alongside the river was large, partly covered and would be a good place to spend a summer evening. The service seemed friendly.

On handpull there was Theakston’s Best Bitter (a good pint), Theakston’s Light, Copper Dragon and Timothy Taylor Landlord.
