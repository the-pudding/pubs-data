Komakino . left this review about The Dashwood Arms

Located on the A40 at the bottom of Dashwood Hill, this would have done a roaring trade before the advent of the M40, so it was a surprise to enter here on a Tuesday night to find it packed, with a plethora of motorbikes out front. A rough L-shape, you enter bottom left with the bar facing and a fireplace stack to the ceiling open either side suggesting this is a former two-roomer. The room bends around to the right of the bar back to an area given over to diners. Beams have been painted over in black, and if you half-close your eyes, you can kind of make out the roadside inn the must have once been, and despite its no frills demeanour, some boxes are being ticked here, so a MOTR score of 5 is probably about right here. (Oh, and beers were Sharp's Doom Bar, Rebellion IPA, Rebellion Zebedee and Rebellion Winter Royal).

On 8th March 2017 - rating: 5
