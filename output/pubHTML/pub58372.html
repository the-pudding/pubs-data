Alex Conway left this review about The Old Coach House

Long established pub in as the name suggests an old coaching house. There are six realm ales always available with Doombar regularly accruing but the rest are usually from small micros with Dukeries and Newby Wyke regulatory having their beers showcased. I had a pink of Oakham JHB which was a nicely kept refreshing beer at £3.35. this is a great local pub that is bursting with character and has open fires in winter time when the atmosphere is at its best with people huddled in to its low ceilings and camped alleyways. there is also a nice outside seating area which catches the sun, one of Southwells Best.

On 28th September 2016 - rating: 8

Real Ale Ray left this review about The Old Coach House

A good traditional pub and well worth a visit. We called on Saturday lunchtime and to our dismay it was closed,the pub didn't open until 2pm. The pub doesn't serve food, so maybe that's the reason for their late opening. The bar faces you as you enter and has some settling areas plus another three separate rooms, one of which has a cast iron stove. There were six handpumps. I went for the Belvoir Mild and Oakham JHB.

On 9th December 2012 - rating: 9
